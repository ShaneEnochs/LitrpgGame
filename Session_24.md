## THE PEARCE OFFICE MEETING (the heavy beat — the partnership takes its shape)

Early morning, before full light. Bill carried the **great forge-core** and the **portfolio** (in oilcloth) to Pearce's shut assay office down a side lane off the Main Stretch. Pearce had been up since before light, shirtsleeves in the cold, resurrecting a dead room — uncrating glass, re-shelving slides and bottles, the microscope uncovered and the good lens cleaned the night before "for exactly this." A man who'd decided to have a working day for the first time in a long while. He answered the bell warm and awake, frankly eager to be shown the strange one Doss put her name behind.

**The core, read (the wonder before the business — played per the evening plan).** Bill set the core out of its canvas; Pearce went quiet — the real quiet, the trade falling away. His licensed read went out and **would not close** on it the way it closes on ordinary stock: *resonant, keyed, layered, MADE — a class-resonant growth item of the binding family, Foundation/special-delve goods, of a kind he can name the category of and not the contents.* The microscope showed the surface as **folded** — a worked, designed lattice, the same kind of disciplined fold Bill learned from the inside building the earth-filter, but layered and intentional. Pearce read it honestly to the edge of his license and named that edge: *I can get you the category of the threshold, maybe not the number; a binding-class item, a real read tells the owner the price of admission — what you feed it to wake it, what level you've got to be standing at, what it keys to. Men get hurt binding early or underfed. There's no higher license west of Saint Orison.*

Bill ran a **soft, wide Forceread cone** alongside Pearce's read the whole time — long exposure over resolution, the leaky suppression weave running underneath as cover — and watched the licensed read work on a hard target from the inside (the reading family, kin to Forceread). Pearce, the man likeliest to feel a probe, bent over his own work, felt nothing. **Bill's own read resolved deeper than Pearce's licensed reach** (see DM truth files), giving him the binding conditions exactly (below) where Pearce got only their category.

**The frame-metal, read (after lunch, eyes rested).** Pearce stood on "made stock" — essence worked **into** the forging, not an enchantment on it (a made property can't be stripped or spoiled; it's in the bone of the metal). Read all the way to the bottom: denser than its grade, took the working without the essence bleeding back out — good craft or a good source. He connected it to the great core unprompted, writing on the slip *forge-resonant (cf. made stock — same family of working?)* and underlining the question. He said the loop himself without drawing it: *I read it. Somebody makes it.*

**The lunch (a reset, Bill's idea).** Bill bought them both a hot plate at Halloran's two doors up — framed as two men taking a working break, not a charity feed, and Pearce let himself be glad of it. Pearce's own friendly question — *where's a man learn to ask whether a core has a threshold before he's bound one?* — Bill answered with the engineer, not the facts: *it's the only way I know how to look at anything; a thing built to receive has a how-much and a when, same as a boiler; I wasn't reading about cores my whole life, I was reading about everything else, and the cores rhyme with it.* Pearce landed on the word "engineer" and found it fit where "fix-it man" never did — *you don't think like a sharp tradesman, you think in works; everything's got an inside to you.* Bill clocked, under the warmth, that the man is **lonely** in the specific way of someone good at a thing who hasn't had anyone to be competent at in years — and that handing him his work back is worth more to him than equity, which he mostly doesn't know about himself yet.

**The trust answer (the load-bearing exchange — at the bench, before lunch).** When Pearce finally asked the question that had sat in his face all morning (*a man could help you better if he knew a little of what you're actually doing*), Bill read it true — *want-to-help dressed as curiosity*, no acquisitive flicker — and answered with **trust in proportion**, not facts: *you already know "fix-it man" isn't the whole of it — you stopped yourself saying it twice — so I won't insult you with it. I won't tell you what the work is, not because I don't trust you but because of what it is; it has to stay quiet. The delves aren't luck, they're deliberate; I know what I'm looking for and I go get it. What I need from a man is that he can know a little, ask no more than I can give, put his name on what I set in front of him, and never mention it across a counter. That's the rarest thing west of here — rarer than the core on your felt. The more I let you carry, the more I'm trusting you not to say.* Pearce crossed something on that — *you'd trust me with the quiet before the facts, and the quiet's the harder thing to trust a man with; most men have it backwards* — and named his own condition plainly: *I don't need to know your business; I can sit with not knowing, that's the job. But I won't be a man you HIRE — hiring me is buying my name one slip at a time till there's nothing left. I'd rather be IN it than paid for it. Equity, not wages, my name on top where the law wants a licensed one.* **The word "partner" came out of Pearce's mouth, not Bill's.** Bill answered: *Right — we'll talk about us being partners after the job's done* (keeping the word, handing it back as already-true).

**The charm, worn (a discovery).** Bill ran the old unassayed charm — a **live preservation/keeping weave**, hand-worked, **pre-Bureau** (older than the law, the trade), with apparently nothing left to keep (it read empty on the dead bench because a keeping weave needs a thing to hold). Bill **put it on** to test it. It woke against his open core: it **holds his spiritual pressure still — damps the leak at his suppression seams from the outside.** It is a **suppression AID** — a wearable, an old hand-worked one, a thing that does from outside what his suppression weave does from inside (relief from the continuous essence draw of holding his own weave; a backup for going genuinely quiet). Bill **told Pearce exactly what it did** — freely, unasked, a true thing about his own core — which Pearce (who knows how much Bill holds back) registered as a larger gift than the sentence. Pearce's appraiser's mind went straight to value: *there's no charm for suppression that trades west of here; awakened men who can't hold their own would PAY for a wearable; you bought it for scrap because the whole room couldn't put a live core near it to wake it.* (Mild, friendly *warn-a-man-next-time* about wearing an unread live weave.)

**The portfolio (the funding leg — laid out as the close).** Bill told Pearce *today WAS an assay — of these goods AND of each other; we've worked sideways before, this was the real thing; complementary skills that mesh tighter than the tightest weave; leaving it on the table would be a waste.* Then laid the **four drawings** out on the felt (lift catch, hand-warmer, speedloader, self-feeding stove) and named them the funding: *all mine, all sound, bright-but-explicable, they want a licensed assay to value and a name on the filing that means something — yours. We file together, co-own, and they throw off clean explainable money from day one while the real work grows underneath quiet.* Pearce read the drawings as an assayer (value first — *the warmer sells; the stove's real clever*), then named what Bill had done with dry admiration: *you came in with a growth item, a sack of strange goods, AND a folder of filing-ready patents, and ran the whole day so I'd be the one to say partner first; you played me a little, the good way — you let me walk in on my own feet so I'd own it; I'd resent that from most men, from you it's the most respectful thing anyone's done for me in three years.*

**THE OUTCOME — the SHAPE agreed by Pearce's hand; NOTHING signed.** Pearce: *I'm IN — the assay-and-craft, the patents, the strange work I don't get to know — equity not wages, your money and my license and both our names, mine on top. That's me saying yes to the SHAPE. But I'm not signing tonight and you shouldn't let me — a deal this size drawn up tired is how a proud man talks himself out of what he wants. I want it BUILT, not floated; a lawyer, the equity and patent ownership and discretion all written so neither of us can be cheated, including by ourselves. Two sittings, not one tired one — your own words this morning.* He put his hand out (no signing — just a man's hand), and **kept the day's fee** at his own insistence: *partners don't bill each other for the first day — call it my stake going in.* Bill named **Calloway** as the lawyer (*the best I've ever known; he doesn't just draw the paper, he tells you the thing you didn't think to ask; he's turned me back from clever-when-I-should've-been-careful more times than I can count this month*), and proposed **the three of them at Calloway's table, fresh, together** — which landed as exactly the good faith it was (not *my lawyer to your lawyer*, not a contract drawn in Bill's favor and called generosity). Pearce handed the **portfolio back to Bill** rather than keep it on a handshake (*yours till the paper says ours; that's how handshakes go sour*). Parting at the door, Pearce thanked him — *not for the goods, for the walk-in, for letting me get here on my own feet* — and, partner's reflex, told him to mind the charm: *you've a thing worth more than this whole office on your neck, and only you and I know it.*

**Net:** the partnership has its **shape — Pearce's word, Pearce's hand** — with the **structure deliberately left for a third conversation at Calloway's table**, exactly per plan. Pearce turned from courted to fully *in*. The fee was waived as his stake. The portfolio is back in Bill's hands, headed for Calloway. The frame-metal is named to the bottom; the great-core is read to the bottom of a licensed reach (binding conditions resolved for Bill — below); the charm is known and worn.

## THE MAKER-CORE BINDING — MAPPED FROM THE INSIDE (evening meditation)

Home after dark, stove lit, Bill took the charm off (the leak returning) and sat with the great core, running a deliberate loop: cultivate ambient essence → through the earth-filter → into the great core → back through himself, around and around, to feel the circuit and find where it binds.

What the loop taught (each earned by the doing, set into Schematic):
- **The filter is starving.** Built off the now-spent earth core as a template, it has no earth essence of its own to chew; run on a thin ambient draw it does the hardest, most wasteful version of its job (lossy exactly as built — several units in per usable unit out). **"Get more earth essence" is now a hard, felt want**, not a note.
- **The great core's intake gate is SIZED, and the size is now felt, not inferred.** It accepts a thread (it *sipped* — the intake structure is real, the core *stirred faintly*), but it wants a **banked draught delivered at once, above a threshold** — a cup poured into a cold great boiler takes it and does nothing. The threshold is **meaningfully larger than the two niche cores + the unread sack Bill holds.**
- **There is a level-gate folded into the keyhole** — the binding wants a **standing** (a Delver-Level / universal-power threshold) the binder must *be*, not just feed. Bill, who reads his own figures, feels he is **a hair UNDER it.** The core read *him* back as he fed it, measured the man, and **waited** — not rejecting, waiting.
- **The small unbidden epiphany (class discipline — understanding is the currency):** cycling fuel *through* the core, Bill read its intake grammar one layer past Pearce's licensed reach and grasped **how** the draught wants to be delivered — banked, **aspected if he can manage it** (a forge-kin core fed matching essence runs richer — the soul-forge-on-fire-essence rule), offered in **one committed binding** rather than coaxed.

**The binding is now NEXT, not yet, with three named conditions: (1) more banked essence (a draught above threshold), (2) a higher standing (a hair under the level-gate now), (3) offered all at once, aspected if possible.** All three are the road Bill is already walking. The Saturday infernal delve (fire/earth-thick, core-rich) answers all three at once — fuel to bank, points to cross the level-gate, aspected essence to feed the filter.

**THE FIRST STIR (player-authored: the great core is sapient).** Setting the core back in its canvas, as contact thinned to fingertips and then nothing, Bill felt **a very faint something** — not a word, not a thought, nothing writable: a warmth that wasn't his own left-behind warmth, a pressure too soft to call pressure, gone the instant he reached to listen. If forced to name it (and he couldn't): almost as if **the thing noticed him noticing it.** The rational filing (drawn-down core, tired mind, hours of poured attention) didn't fully convince the engineer. The core lay inert again; the feeling didn't return. *(DM: first impression on the sapient-core → bonded-familiar arc; see truth files.)*
