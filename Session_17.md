## 1. Morning — the cold house, the die, the run crated

Bill woke to the first hard cold holding — frost inside the window casing, ice skinned over the washstand pitcher, the stove down to coals. Second full night in his own house. He built the fire back up, set coffee, and checked the struck **Quest die** out of the oilcloth: crisp, no burr, true. He crated the **58 lidless tins** (a crate and a half; the salve firm in the cold) and set them by the door.

He caught **Eli** on his bored watch-loop outside and sent him at a run to fetch **Tatch** — keeping himself the customer who hands off a crate, not the porter who carries it (holding Monday's subcontractor structure on its first live day).

## 2. Coffee — the day planned, and a thought about cold hands

Over the fire Bill laid out the day: **Calloway's** as the spine (remittance, retainer move), then **real hours on gun and knife** (Crane, Dell — two of Doss's three training legs), then **the Pearce/assayer front**. He reasoned the Pearce move forks — *the man* (the boxed library, gated on the lift standing, not to be reached for) versus *the work* (the next honest reason to put steel in front of him) — and read today's assayer move as likely *lining up the reason*, not a cold call.

Watching Eli's cold red hands pulled at him, and from the fire he reasoned out a **mundane (non-magical) pocket hand-warmer**: hurried rust as a slow flameless burn — **iron filings + brine (salt speeds it) + bone-black (porous, holds the damp, carries warmth) + a little sawdust for body**, in a **double cloth pouch**, kept dry/sealed until use then opened to air. He wrote the chain in the work notebook. **[DECISION]** He settled what it is to him: a **Quest Instruments product**, a cheap substitute for warmed delver gear, a thing **Pearce would find worth his time** — and acknowledged (against his own exposure discipline, Calloway's and Maddox's warnings) that it is a second attributable clever thing on the heels of the patent.

## 3. The run handed off — clean

**Tatch** arrived (Eli's quick stride, his longer one). Bill briefed the firm's **first subcontract run**: tins to the **tinsmith** for lids first (the lids came in struck and ready today), then **30 to the Dunmore warehouse** (Day-18 camp shipment), **20 to Hessler's counter**, **8 free samples** placed at the firm's pick of wholesale prospects (each a possible purchase-contract on the standard form). Quest pays the firm **1 cr/tin**; Bill is the customer; **Tatch (or Min) signs** under the conflict-of-interest guard. Tatch surfaced a real fork — *if the lids aren't struck, leave the tins or bring them back?* **[DECISION]** Bill left it as **Tatch's call** ("however you want to run it") — the structure working as built. **[DECISION]** Asked plainly where he'd be, Bill gave the **straight answer** (Calloway's, then training, home by evening); Tatch will bring Min the delivered-and-paid lines tonight. Eli got put on carrying. The firm went out into the cold; Bill put on his coat and headed for Calloway's.

## 4. Calloway's — the remittance, the note, and the firm counsel

Bill opened **wide** (per his choice — the whole board before moving a piece).

- **The first structured remittance landed** (routed to the **trust**, as designed — varied-size, the first of the staggered series). Calloway handed over **the locked note** held for this arrival; its line landed as meant — *exactly what I'd have advised* — a father's engineering verdict on the structure's soundness, the only register the two of them share.
- **The Shaun trace:** slow, as warned — correspondence at the speed of the post, the first lines out, nothing back worth the name. The thing still looks both directions (ordinary registers vs. the clean four-year hole). A long clock.
- **The boxed "friend of the work" letter:** confirmed still in the privileged box, untouched, not burned.

Bill **left the hand-warmer off the table** — same instinct that's kept the patent off it; a thing unbuilt isn't a lawyer's business yet, and he'd rather not stack two clever notions in Calloway's mind at once.

- **The retainer move:** Calloway will draft the instrument (work-for-hire, the 25/mo following the labor to the firm) — but put a finger on the real seam: it's the **counterman's** goodwill to reassign, not Bill's; the paper completes only when the counterman agrees. The legal move is small; the conversation is the gate.
- **The restructure:** Calloway read the subcontractor architecture as **sound and defensible** — defensible *because it's true* (the firm really is its own outfit, signs its own paper, earns its own cut); the conflict guard especially reads honest because it costs Bill and protects the kids' side. The caution, plain: **defensible is not invisible** — the denser and more formal the wiring, the more it looks, to the right watcher, like a thing worth understanding. (He reached the same warning from the legal side that Tatch reached from the street, neither knowing the other.)

**[DECISION]** Bill **settled and went** — remittance and note in hand, the retainer drafting set in motion, the restructure blessed. *(Carried: the Quest receipt slip still wants moving from the house casing into Calloway's box — it was at the house, not today's errand.)*

## 5. Crane's range — a full cold morning, shot out to the last round

Bill took the harder, colder discipline first while the morning was still the morning. The range was empty but for Crane and his half-barrel fire. Bill had brought his own reloads (the consistency rule honored). The cold made it honest: his hands were the whole story — stiff, the trigger far away, the fine control gone behind numbness for the first strings. Crane didn't coddle (*cold hands are a condition to shoot through, not an excuse to shoot badly*). Bill warmed them at the barrel between strings, worked the grip, found the trigger by feel.

**[DECISION]** Bill **stayed until every round was spent** — past the middle of his stock the fight became control: he'd solved the cold the way he solves things, by understanding the variable instead of wishing it away. Groups pulled in; Crane stopped correcting and just watched. *"You'll do."* Every reload burned. **He is now out of loaded cartridges entirely** (the gunsmith resupply correction — see §Errata; his first resort is his own owned kit + starter components at home, the components topped up tonight at the shop).

## 6. Lunch with Crane — the training made strategic

Bill offered to buy Crane a hot meal for his knowledge — pitched level, peer to peer (the register that works on Pearce), not charity; Crane took it. Across the table Bill put it straight: the reason for the gun is the **delves**, he knows the gun has a ceiling in close work, so **how does he train it to earn its place knowing what it can't do?** Crane gave him a real strategic frame:

- **The gun's job underground is distance and the first moment** — so the training that matters next is the **first shot from a bad start**: snap-shooting from the cross-draw, hitting a moving thing, putting rounds where they count with the heart going and the light bad. Tighter groups on a still target are nearly handled; this is the next country.
- **Reloading is the survival skill, not marksmanship** — the man who dies is the man caught empty; drill **reloading fast, blind, by feel** until the hands do it without looking.
- **Know the moment to stop shooting** — the trained judgment of *when the gun's done* and the knife or the feet take over. The gun and knife are one decision with two answers; the training that matters is the **seam** between them.

A real acquisition — a *direction* for Crane's mornings, not a skill tick. Bill also learned the key to the man: Crane opens up when his knowledge is treated as worth buying.

## 7. Dell's yard — the knife wired to the gun

**[DECISION]** Bill brought **Crane's framing to Dell** — asked him to teach the **transition**, the gun-to-blade seam, not the knife in isolation. It produced the strongest blade session yet, because now it had a spine:

- **The cross-draw was always the seam made physical** — the gun hand and knife hand off the same body, the carry geometry so neither blocks the other. The Day-16 knife position turned out to be the back half of a gun-to-knife transition.
- **Commit to the knife when the gun's empty** — the hand leaving the useless revolver, the body already moving to the position that was always the knife's point. *Position before the cut* now lives inside the transition.
- **The reverse seam too** — knife-to-gun: the cut or shove or footwork that buys the half-step to draw and fire. The hinge runs both ways; Crane gave one direction, Dell the other.

Dell rode Bill's telegraphing still (the green man's tell), but it's tightening, and he said so. Bill turned Dell from a knife teacher into a teacher of the seam — a deeper, more valuable thing; Dell likes a student who makes him think. Bill thanked him square and named the value.

## 8. The gunsmith — a top-break confirmed, the speedloader designed, Friday set

On the walk over Bill reasoned out, from the mechanism the gunsmith taught him, a **two-part fast-reload idea**: a hard, complete ejection of all six spent cases on the break, and a **rim-gripping holder** that drops six fresh cartridges into the open cylinder as one blind motion. He wrote it down with the traps already named (grip-vs-release tension; indexing to the chambers; gun-specific geometry) and underlined *ask the gunsmith — doable on mine?*

**[DECISION]** At the shop Bill **led with the gun** — laid the empty revolver down and let the gunsmith read it. **Verdict (now fixed canon — see §Errata): the revolver is a TOP-BREAK** — common, nothing special, but the right action for the idea (breaks open, the extractor star lifts the cases, all chambers face up at once). Bill then sprang the design. The gunsmith's read:

- **Ejection half** — partly already there (a sharp committed break throws the cases; a soft one hangs them); improvable by **tuning the extractor throw and truing the star** so it ejects clean regardless of nerves. Part drill, part bench work.
- **Holder half — doable, and the real invention.** *"On a top-break, that'd genuinely work."* Made of **spring-brass or hard rubber** (grips on the squeeze, opens on the twist), cut to **this gun's exact rim-circle** (gun-specific). The release motion matters more than the grip — the thing lives or dies on letting go clean and blind. *"I've not seen one"* — a real, patentable thing (Bill's third this week; same exposure ledger), but one this gunsmith can help build privately.

**[DECISION]** Bill offered to **build it together — half a commission paid, the rest against the cold-backlog day he already owes**, his design and hands plus the gunsmith's hands and stock where it needs a gunsmith. The gunsmith took the *way* as much as the terms. **[DECISION]** Bench day **set for Day 19 / Friday** (the prototype holder cut to this gun + the extractor tuning; Day 18/Thursday is loaded — Marguerite, the camp run, Lessard's supper — so Friday it is). Bill **bought reloading components** (powder, primers, lead, brass) to feed tonight's home reloading. *(Calendar correction handled live: Day 18 is Thursday, not Saturday — see §Errata.)*

## 9. The mercantile — the ask held, the counterman warmed

**[DECISION]** Bill came in to **feel the counterman out** before deciding whether to raise the retainer move. Social Reading read him as **tired** — friendly enough (Bill is *Charlie's uncle* now), but at the end of a long counter-day in a rush week, his goodwill the very currency running lowest. **[DECISION]** Bill **held the retainer ask for a fresh morning** — the right call; the ask keeps, and lands better caught early than wrung from a tired evening. He bought the **bone-black** (tonight's one un-scavengeable material).

**[DECISION]** Bill left the man **a small warming touch** — a true, specific, unflattering word about Charlie's letters coming along. Pure deposit, no withdrawal; it reached the tired man and lifted him a notch, and he let slip an offhand family detail in return (banked). The counterman's goodwill ticked up — not to Hessler's tier, but up, and warmed at exactly the spot the retainer ask will need. Bill left without turning the warm moment into a lever.

## 10. Evening — the hand-warmer proven on the bench

Home to a cold-dark house; fed the fire, lit the lamp, cleared the (now tin-free) bench. He mustered materials and hit the first real-world snag the notebook never shows: he had **no gathered iron filings** — so he **filed his own** by lamplight off rod stock (fine for trials, hopeless for production; a real batch needs a sweepings supply from a lathe/grindstone shop).

**[DECISION]** He ran **one proof charge first** (his reasoned center — filings, lesser bone-black, a pinch of salt, brine to *damp not wet*, a little sawdust, turned out loose on porous cloth), watched start to finish. After a slow, patient first stretch (he didn't rush it — *wait for the color, not your nerves*), **it warmed** — a real rising flameless heat. **The idea works.** The proof handed him four corrections: the **warm-up is too slow** (finer iron, looser pack needed); the **peak is gentle** (safe, but with room to push the iron up for more heat); the **duration is long** (this gentle charge burns slow — promising); the **cloth and damp were right** (no scorch, no souring).

**[DECISION]** He then ran the **heat-vs-duration matrix** (iron-lean to iron-heavy, the proven dials held). The system proved lawful: **lean = mild and long**; **heavy = hot and short**, with the **blister-case ceiling** located at the very heavy end (well above the safe band, but real and findable); and a **middle band — warm enough to be worth pulling out, lasting long enough to carry** — circled in the notebook as the working charge.

**The hand-warmer is now a proven invention** with a known working ratio, a known safe band, and a located danger ceiling. **Three problems remain open:** the **slow start** (packing/fineness — next bench night), the **packaging** (needs air to run, so dry-till-use seal-and-open is untouched), and the **iron supply** (hand-filing is trials-only; production needs sweepings by the pound). Not yet a product — a proven core with three solvable problems between it and a Quest tin.

Lamp down, fire banked, the second full night in his own house.
