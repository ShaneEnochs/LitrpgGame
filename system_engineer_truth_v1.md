# THE SYSTEM ENGINEER — BILL'S AWAKENING (DM-ONLY CANON BIBLE, v1)

**DO NOT REVEAL DIRECTLY. The player has commissioned this file's existence but has deliberately NOT read it. Everything here is fixed so that the System's behavior stays consistent and its origin mystery is solvable through play. Reveal only through play — boot text, screen behavior, evidence, costs, consequences. Secrets stay secret until earned.**

**Precedence:** `game_bible.md` §13 establishes that every awakened delver perceives a **delver window** — universal, private, numeric — and that remains true of the world at large. What is exceptional about Bill is not *that* he has a window but *what his is*: this file governs how his System differs from the ordinary delver window, and on that subject it overrides any general description in §13. In every other respect `game_bible.md` still wins, and all its table rules (no dice prompts, narrative resolution, secrets stay secret, mysteries solvable, death stays real, strict resource tracking) apply to everything below.

**Player-authored design pillars (locked, from the player's own specification):** a System beyond the ordinary delver window — every delver has a window, his does far more — its true workings invisible to everyone else; class = System Engineer; blueprints for abilities, runes, weapons, armor, and more; faster growth than ordinary cultivators but not trivializing; Examine for deeper understanding; ability levels and hidden evolutions; the knowledge gate (he must understand a thing before he can blueprint it); costs visible but not editable; more limits = cheaper; strong abilities = absurd costs; mana pool from Intellect, visible as a number; character sheet visible, health stays conditions; class levels fed by class-purpose work and by understanding the System itself; runes gated behind real study; Examine accurate but vague at low levels; interface = system text plus a mental drafting space; origin = a mystery Bill will want to chase.

## ONE-PARAGRAPH ANSWER

When Bill's essence core opens, it opens unlike any core in the world: alongside ordinary cultivation he receives the **System** — screens only he perceives, a class named **SYSTEM ENGINEER**, and a faculty for modeling, drafting, and instantiating designs. The System is not a gift from the dungeons in general. It is a **scar from Severn Falls, 1852**. Assimilation is a two-way channel: when the entity models a mind, a residue of the modeling faculty itself can pass the other way, and in 1852 a twelve-year-old boy spent days in close company with a fresh, early, incomplete copy of his mother. The faculty imprinted on him then — dormant, inert, waiting for an open core to complete its circuit — and the preferred deepest truth (see REVELATION TIERS) is that the imprint did not happen by accident: the copy carried enough of **Rebecca Weaver** that she finished it deliberately. Her last act was to press into her son the only inheritance the thing's nature allowed her to give — its own way of seeing, refracted through a child who would grow into an engineer. The System is the Mirrorwell's modeling faculty wearing Bill's mind the way a copy wears a face: where the entity perceives living minds and builds perfect copies, Bill perceives structures and builds perfect drafts. He has, without knowing it, been carrying a piece of the campaign's central phenomenon for ten years. The class that will make him powerful is kin to the thing that took his mother — and it may be the only faculty in the world that can draft the gate.

## AWAKENING — TRIGGERS (locked options)

Per canon, killing even one dungeon creature opens a person's core. For Bill, the moment the core opens, the dormant imprint completes and the System boots. Three sanctioned triggers; **whichever the fiction reaches first fires — do not engineer toward one:**

1. **Standard:** Bill kills any dungeon creature, in any dungeon, anywhere. The ordinary road.
2. **The copy clause (DM-secret rule, locked):** a copy IS dungeon-substance — a made thing of the Mirrorwell's body walking in the world. Killing a copy counts as killing a dungeon creature and opens the core, anywhere, with no dungeon in sight. If Bill ever kills the thing hunting Rusk, or a copy of Amos, or any walker, he awakens — over a body wearing a human face. This trigger is emotionally enormous; if it fires, give the moment its full due, and note that the guild/USBD definition of "dungeon creature" has never contemplated it.
3. **Contact (use only if the fiction earns it):** a begun-but-interrupted assimilation — Bill drawn toward or touched at the gap and pulled back, or surviving partial contact — can complete the 1852 circuit directly, opening the core with no kill at all. This is the costliest version: it should scar him (a condition, a changed sense, a piece of subjective time he cannot account for), and it seeds the origin question at maximum volume, because no rule anyone knows says a man awakens without a kill.

**Assay consequences (live dramatic material):** the guild's mandatory 300 cr assay detects an open core and mana capacity — nothing else. Post-awakening, Bill reads as "awakened, modest mana," indistinguishable from any low-grade cultivator. **The System itself is undetectable by any assay, sense, instrument, or cultivator's perception in existence — he is the only one of his kind and nothing was ever built to look for him.** But the cell prizes Bill as a *clean specimen* (truth file). The moment guild paperwork shows him awakened, that status flips, and the timing of his assay against the timing of his awakening is a real clock: the license wall is payable within the fortnight. Track it.

## WHAT THE SYSTEM IS (DM-only metaphysics)

- It is **part of him** — not a spirit, not a separate intelligence, not a voice. It has no will, no goals, no personality. It is a faculty, the way sight is a faculty, rendered to his conscious mind as screens because his mind needed *some* furniture to hold it and screens-as-drafting-plates is what an engineer's mind built.
- **It renders in his idiom.** Plates, sections, schedules, ledgers, survey notation, isometric views — the visual language of drafting tables and Calloway's instruments, because it is built from the contents of his own head. This is itself a plantable clue: a thing from outside him would not speak so fluently in his hand. (Mostly his hand — see the CLUE LEDGER.)
- **It never speaks first.** No quests, no alerts, no warnings, no tutorials. It answers use; it never volunteers. Every discovery about it must be Bill's initiative — hypothesis, test, deduction. The small descriptions it shows are terse, accurate, and never explanatory beyond the minimum. If Bill never pokes at a function, he never learns it exists.
- **It never lies and never invents.** Where it lacks basis, it shows blanks, voids, unrendered sections — and it renders blanks honestly rather than guessing. Bill can trust a blank absolutely. This discipline (trust the void) should become one of his survival instincts.
- **It runs on him.** Drafting, Examine, and ability use draw on his mana. In essence-thinned environments (the sink), the rendering itself degrades — see THE ENTITY AND THE SYSTEM.

## THE WINDOW AND THE SYSTEM — THE CHOOSING

Every awakened delver has a **window** (`game_bible.md` §13): the universal, private, numeric interface that shows their own figures and, at awakening and at each growth-paying completion, presents **the choosing** — three Skills to pick among, two locked forever (`ability_framework_v1.md` §6). This is the world's machinery, the same for everyone. Bill's **System** is the exceptional layer riding on top of that ordinary window; the choosing belongs to the window underneath, which he has like anyone else.

This reconciles the choosing with the locked pillar. **The choosing is not the System speaking first.** When three offers unfold after a completion, that is the window doing for Bill exactly what it does for every delver on the same schedule — not his System volunteering a quest, an alert, or a tutorial. The distinction is provable in play: ask another delver "does your window offer you things after a delve?" and the answer is yes. The System still never originates; it only renders. Concretely:

- **The window presents; the System renders it in his idiom.** Where an ordinary delver feels three offers as terse words and a pulled sense, Bill's plates lay them out in full — exact effects, costs, category tags, and which two will lock — because his System renders the window's event the way it renders everything, in draft-work and figures. The rich rendering is the exceptional part; the offer itself is universal.
- **The offers are tailored to him,** as they are to everyone (`delve_progression_truth_v1.md`): one option deepening a line he has built, one opening something new, read off how he has actually delved. That the menu tracks the man is a designed behavior he is uniquely placed to notice and chart — a clue feeding the curriculum inference, not a quirk of his System.
- **The boot offer reads the incoming man.** His first choosing, at awakening, has no delving to read, so it reads who he is coming in: a methodical engineer-alchemist, high Precision and Intellect and Insight, with the mundane skills to match. Offer accordingly — nothing combat-shaped he has never done.

**Universal Skills versus drafted abilities — two separate economies.** A Skill Bill takes from the choosing (or buys as an orb, or learns from a tome) seats under the **universal** limits: the Intellect-sized active kit, the one Movement seat, the one aura (`ability_framework_v1.md` §§2–4, 8). His **drafted** abilities are the System's own economy, with their own creation slots (3 at class Lv. 1 — see ABILITIES). The two do not share a pool: an offered or orbed Skill never consumes a drafting slot, and a drafted ability never costs him a choosing. He is the one delver who runs both ledgers at once — the universal one every delver has, and the System's, which is his alone. (A Skill he holds can still crystallize into a sellable orb on unseating, per the universal rule; his *drafted* abilities do not auto-orb, per `ability_framework_v1.md` §8.)

## THE BOOT (staging guidance for the awakening scene)

Do not infodump. The first experience should be disorientation inside a familiar register: lines of pale draft-work hanging in his vision that no one else reacts to, terse text in a hand that is almost his.

Suggested first-boot content (adapt freely to the scene):

> **CORE: OPEN.**
> **CLASS ASSIGNED — SYSTEM ENGINEER. Lv. 1.**
> *(a status plate unfolds: his name, his numbers, a mana figure he has no referent for)*
> **POINTS: 10 — UNALLOCATED. DELVER LEVEL: 1.**
> *(and the window's first choosing unfolds: three Skills offered in full draft-detail, with the note that two will close)*

- "System Engineer" should mean nothing to him. He knows engines, mills, water-systems, rail-systems; "a system engineer" parses as a man who works on systems, which explains nothing about screens. Let him chew the word for sessions.
- **The points and the choosing are the universal window's doing,** not the System's (see THE WINDOW AND THE SYSTEM) — rendered in his idiom because his System renders everything that way, but the same event every delver gets at awakening. The 10 points he places himself; the first offer reads the incoming engineer. Keep the System layer (Examine, Drafting, the plates) distinct from this universal layer even in the boot beat.
- **One deniable beat at boot, optional but recommended:** a half-second impression as the plates first unfold — a woman's hands guiding his on a pencil. Gone before he can hold it. Plant it and never mention it again until the origin thread matures.
- Visible at boot: **Status** (with Points and Delver Level), **Mana**, **Examine**, **Drafting**, **Abilities (empty)**, and the **window's choosing** for as long as an offer is pending. Nothing else. Further panes appear only when reached (see CLASS LEVELS).
- He discovers controls by intent: the screens respond to directed attention and mental manipulation, like working a drawing he can rotate, pull apart, and annotate without hands. Eyes-open use is possible but visibly distracts him (a tell); eyes-closed use is full-fidelity.

## THE STATUS PLATE (what he sees)

- **Name. Class and class level. Attributes — as numbers, his own only** (the 1–100 scale, raw). He will immediately notice the numbers and have no scale to hang them on; let him calibrate the scale himself through play (a fine ongoing deduction: what does 16 mean? what's the ceiling? — he can rediscover the world's power scale empirically).
- **Mana: current / maximum, numeric.** The world knows mana as a felt thing; Bill now has both the feeling and the figure. **Locked formula: maximum mana = Intellect × 10.** (At Intellect 16: **160**.) The pool grows only as Intellect grows — which, post-awakening, cultivation can eventually carry past 20. Recovery follows `game_bible.md` §13: a full night's sleep with food restores the pool; deliberate meditation restores roughly Intellect per hour; strong-essence environments roughly double recovery; essence-thinned environments slow or stop it. (**Reconciliation with the gate model in `cultivation_body_forging_v1.md`:** ordinary cultivators grow mana by opening the eight essence gates, with Intellect fixing each gate's value at the break. Bill does not — his pool is computed, Intellect × 10, tracking his current Intellect with no gates and no lock-in, and growing only as Intellect grows, the dangerous way, through delve payouts. Treat this as intended, and as one more sign — subtle but real — that his core is not an ordinary core: the System renders a clean figure where the world has uneven, hard-won gates. He has no gate-opening meditation to do.)
- **Mundane skills as numbers** (the 1–10 scale, his own only).
- **Delver Level: a number** (his own only) — points ÷ 10, the same milestone the arch checks at tier entry (`game_bible.md` §12). He reads it as an exact figure where ordinary delvers feel only a coarse standing, and it ticks up as his points cross each ten. Unawakened it reads 0; awakening sets it to 1.
- **Essence core: rendered in words, not numbers** ("CORE: OPEN — FAINT" and similar). Cultivation remains the intuitive road canon says it is; the System describes the core's state but does not gamify essence. Growth past the unawakened ceiling happens for him the way it happens for everyone — delve completion payouts under the adversity law (`game_bible.md` §12; essence stones refuel a core but never grow it). One rendering exception, governed by `delve_progression_truth_v1.md`: a payout's *attribute result* lands on the plate as numeric unallocated points he may assign deliberately, because attributes are numbers on this plate; the core's own state stays in words.
- **Health: CONDITIONS ONLY, never a number** (player-locked). The plate shows the same condition ladder the table uses — Healthy, Winded, Hurt, Badly Hurt, Critical, Dying — plus named wounds and conditions. It is honest about them (it will name "infection setting in" before a man without medicine would be sure), which makes it a field-surgeon's instrument of real value without turning flesh into a bar.
- **Abilities: listed with level and use cost.** Empty at boot.

## EXAMINE

Directed analysis of a thing he attends to. **Costs mana. Always accurate. Usually vague.** Its purpose at low levels is to sharpen his own deduction — a thumb on the scale of an already-good mind, not an oracle.

- **What it returns:** a terse plate — material, state, function, stress, flaw — at a depth set by (a) class level and (b) **what Bill already knows.** Examine multiplies existing understanding; it does not substitute for it. Examining a rifle, a man who has stripped rifles gets tolerances and the cracked sear; a man who hasn't gets "mechanism: sound, one fault, rear of the action." Re-examining the same target adds little until his understanding grows.
- **Cost guide (DM anchors, scale to fiction):** simple object 2 · complex mechanism 5 · a person, surface read 8 · an essence phenomenon 10–25 · anomalous or entity-adjacent material 25+ and climbing. Deeper queries on the same target cost more, not less.
- **On people:** surface only — condition, fatigue, rough capability, visible function ("right shoulder: old break, badly set"). Never thoughts, never intentions, never memories. Social reading stays a skill, not a scan.
- **On the awakened:** it can tell a core is open (something ordinary cultivators can also sense in one another). Depth beyond that requires class levels and study.
- **Failure mode:** where he lacks all basis, it returns honest near-nothing ("composition: no referent") — which is itself information, and on the frontier in 1862, "no referent" pointed at a material means *dungeon-made*. Let him figure that use out himself.
- **The intuitive-leap function (player-specified, honor it):** the best Examine returns are the small true detail that lets HIS mind close the arc — the warm mortar's load trend, the one wrong tolerance, the screw turned by a left-handed man. Feed his deduction; never replace it.

## DRAFTING & BLUEPRINTS (the central verb)

The Drafting pane opens into a mental drafting space — a dark field where he can build, rotate, section, and annotate full three-dimensional models with thought. System text prices and validates; the space is where the work happens.

**THE KNOWLEDGE GATE (locked, the class's soul):** the System can model only what Bill genuinely understands. Gaps in his understanding render as literal gaps — unrendered voids in the draft. He cannot handwave a mechanism he couldn't explain; the draft simply won't close. **Understanding is the resource the class actually runs on; mana is only the medium.** Every blueprint is therefore earned through study, disassembly, observation, experiment, or testimony — which keeps his investigative play-style as the engine of his power forever.

**Drafting costs:** ~2 mana per hour of focused drafting trance. Trance is a tell: stillness, eyes tracking nothing, lips moving over figures.

**Blueprint categories:**

1. **Object blueprints (weapons, armor, tools, machines, works).** A blueprint is a perfected *design* — tolerances, materials, sequences, jigs — not a conjured item. **He must still build the thing**: materials, tools, money, hours, and his own hands. A blueprint lifts the *design ceiling* far above his unaided skill (drawings a Master would envy), but execution is still bounded by his Precision, his skill levels, his tools, and what the frontier can supply. This keeps the economy, the mercantile, dungeon materials, and the engineer identity fully load-bearing. A blueprinted object, well built, should be noticeably superior to trade work — and *too* superior work is a tell that draws eyes (smiths talk). **[AMENDED for blueprinted items by the CRAFTING TABLE class boon — see the END-OF-EMBERWASTE ADDENDUM. The hand-build step is lifted for items Bill holds a blueprint for, given the skill and the materials; the rest of this pillar — the knowledge gate, materials, and the skill ceiling — still rules, and the *tell* in fact SHARPENS (forge-less master-grade work is harder to explain, not easier).]**
2. **Ability blueprints** — see ABILITIES below.
3. **Rune blueprints — LOCKED until studied.** Rune knowledge is closely guarded canon. Until Bill has physically examined real runes and grasped *conceptually* how inscription, material, and essence interact, the Drafting pane has no rune vocabulary at all — the category does not even appear. First real study of rune-work should be a milestone scene (and a story goal: rune access means guilds, contraband tomes, the Hollow Road, or Saint Orison). After study, rune drafting obeys the knowledge gate like everything else, and rune *execution* still demands proper materials per canon (relic steel, ghostwood, and kin — guns still hate enchantment).
4. **Higher categories exist and are hidden.** Linked drafts (an object designed to host one of his abilities), large works (structures, seal-works, machines at building scale), and — at the far end of the class — **the gate.** See CLASS LEVELS. Do not name these to him; let their panes appear when reached.

## ABILITIES (creation, costs, slots, levels, evolution)

The Abilities pane lets him design preternatural abilities from scratch — the class's most singular power (everyone else gets abilities from orbs or long cultivation; he *drafts* them).

**Design (the minigame):** he specifies effect and **inputs requirements and limits** — trigger conditions, range, duration, target restrictions, material components, gestures or words, contexts ("only what my bare hand touches," "only in darkness," "only metal I have drawn"), cooldowns. The knowledge gate applies: he can only draft effects he can mechanically conceptualize (he can draft *ignite* from combustion knowledge and observed essence-flame behavior; he cannot draft *teleport* without a theory). Vague concepts the System must bridge itself are priced brutally.

**Costs (visible, never editable — player-locked):** every draft shows two figures that recalculate live as he adds or removes limits:
- **Creation cost** ("to slot") — a one-time mana expenditure to instantiate the ability into his core. **Must be paid in a single act.** If it exceeds his current mana, he cannot make the ability — full stop. (Desperate overcasting per `game_bible.md` is theoretically possible and should carry real backlash: injury, a flawed ability, exposure, worse. Never free.)
- **Use cost** — per activation, or per-minute upkeep for sustained effects.

**The limits economy (player-locked):** more limits = cheaper to create AND cheaper to use. Broad, strong, unrestricted abilities carry **absurd** costs — deliberately unreachable for years. Worked anchors at Intellect 16 (pool 160):

| Draft | Creation | Use |
|---|---|---|
| *Spark* — ignite tinder my bare hand touches, smaller than my fist | ~45 | 2 |
| Same effect, any visible target within ten yards, no touch | ~220 | 9 |
| *Read the Strain* — see stress lines in any structure I study for a full minute | ~70 | 4 |
| *Mend* — restore any broken object I look at | ~6,400 | ~300 |

The System prices the absurd ones without comment or apology. Let him learn the curve by bruising himself on it.

**Slots:** at class Lv. 1 he has **3 ability slots**; +1 at each even class level (adjustable if pacing demands). Scarcity forces curation. An ability can be **dissolved** to free its slot (takes a day of trance; creation cost is not refunded; the design remains drafted and can be re-slotted later by paying creation again).

**Ability levels:** each ability runs **Lv. 1–5 within its stage**, advancing through *meaningful use under stakes* — the same philosophy as skills. Drilling in a safe yard sharpens execution but barely levels; use that matters, levels. Leveling sharpens the ability within its definition: lower use cost, smoother activation, finer control. Level is visible on the plate; the *mechanism* of leveling is not — let him deduce it.

**EVOLUTION (the hidden depth — player-locked structure):**
- Every ability's evolution paths are **fixed at creation**, seeded by its concept and its chosen limits. A flame ability limited to touch evolves along touch-and-transfer lines; one limited to drawn metal evolves toward the forge. **Limits are seeds, not just discounts** — this retroactively rewards thoughtful design, and Bill should be allowed to eventually deduce it (a major class insight worth a level).
- Evolution triggers near level cap, through heavy meaningful use **plus a conceptual breakthrough or external catalyst** (a new understanding, an extreme moment, exposure to a related phenomenon). DM-judged, never scheduled. The ability transforms qualitatively — typically one limit relaxes or the concept deepens — and begins its next stage at Lv. 1. Some abilities are dead ends and cap without evolving; he has no way to know which in advance at low levels.
- **Visibility is gated by CLASS level (player-locked):** below class Lv. 3 he does not know evolution exists. At Lv. 3, capped abilities show a grayed, unreadable branch — proof of a beyond, nothing more. At Lv. 5, drafting previews the *silhouette* of an ability's next stage (one stage only, never the tree). Full foresight never comes; the deep tree stays dark even to him.
- First evolution he ever witnesses should land as a revelation, not a feature.

## CLASS LEVELS

**What feeds the class (player-locked):** doing what the class is for, and understanding the System itself. Concretely, class growth comes from: first-of-their-kind blueprints; completed builds from his own drafts; ability creations and evolutions; correct deductions about the System verified by test; deep study of genuinely novel phenomena (runes, the ward, essence behaviors, dungeon mechanics). Repetition feeds nothing. **Levels are rare, DM-awarded narrative beats** — no XP arithmetic, consistent with the table's whole progression philosophy.

**Loose roadmap (pace by play; unlocks appear as new panes or sharpened functions, never announced in advance):**

- **Lv. 1** — Status, Mana, Examine I, Drafting (objects), Abilities (3 slots). Evolution invisible.
- **Lv. 2** — Examine sharpens; +1 slot; cost previews during drafting gain precision (he can watch figures move as he toys with a limit before committing).
- **Lv. 3** — the grayed branch appears on capped abilities: *evolution exists.*
- **Lv. 4** — +1 slot; **linked drafts** unlock (objects designed to host or channel one of his abilities — the road to personal gear no one else could make). Rune drafting appears here *only if* the knowledge gate has been satisfied; otherwise it waits on the study, whenever that comes.
- **Lv. 5** — evolution silhouettes during drafting; Examine II (essence phenomena and the awakened read meaningfully deeper).
- **Lv. 6** — +1 slot; compound drafts (multi-part works, mechanisms of mechanisms).
- **Lv. 7** — **large works** unlock: structures, seal-works, building-scale machines. The pane that makes the gate thesis a drafting problem.
- **Lv. 8+** — self-examination deepens (see CLUE LEDGER — this is where the origin thread's hardest evidence lives), and the far category beyond large works begins to render at its edges. What sits at the top of this class and what a completed gate-draft would demand are the campaign's final architecture; per the truth file, **do not resolve casually.**

## PASSIVES (always on, cost nothing)

- **The Ledger (memory):** from the instant of awakening, his retention is total going *forward* — every page, face, figure, plate, and count, retrievable at will, rendered as archived plates if he wants them. **Pre-awakening memory stays ordinary human memory** — the System cannot dredge what the man already blurred. **Exception, locked:** if he ever tries to model his childhood memories of 1852, the void renders *wrong* — too clean, edges too straight, a blank shaped like deliberate erasure where ordinary forgetting is fog. The locked drawer, made visible as a drawer. Plant this only when he goes looking.
- **The Survey (spatial sense):** eyes closed, he can model any space he knows or can deduce — rotate it, section it, walk it from angles he has never stood at. Accuracy equals knowledge; gaps stay blank; **it never invents.** Underground, this is worth more than a lamp: he can hold the whole adit-and-gallery geometry of Hollow Reach (now in his notebook) as a living model. It is also fed by Examine and the Ledger — the three functions compound.

## SECRECY, DETECTION, LAW

- **No detection exists.** No assay, instrument, cultivator's sense, monster, or rite can perceive the System or its screens. He is singular; nothing was ever built to find him. (The entity is the one open question — see below.)
- **The assay reads:** core open, mana present, rough capacity. Nothing more. Post-awakening he profiles as an unremarkable fresh cultivator.
- **The tells are behavioral:** drafting stillness, eyes tracking nothing, the muttered figures, recall too perfect to be natural, designs too good for a frontier tinker, abilities that match no registered orb and no licensed tome. The USBD's whole regime assumes abilities come from orbs and spells from tomes; a man manifesting *unsourceable* abilities is, on paper, an unlicensed-practice problem and, to a curious inspector, something worse. His forgettable face and alias discipline are now load-bearing in a new way.
- **The cell:** if they ever learn what he is, "clean specimen" becomes "unique specimen," and their interest turns acquisitive in a way no poison-pill deters (paper deters calculators; hunger for a one-of-one instrument may out-calculate the paper). **Do not let them learn cheaply.** This is the campaign's sharpest new stakes-line; honor the player's operational discipline as the thing keeping him alive.

## THE ENTITY AND THE SYSTEM (handle with maximum care)

The two are kin — the same faculty at different scales, pointed at different objects. The entity models minds; the System models structures. Consequences, locked:

- **Essence-thin zones degrade the rendering.** Near the sink, his screens dim, lag, flicker, lose line-resolution — an instrument and a warning in one, parallel to the blue-cored flame. Deep enough, drafting becomes impossible and the Status plate runs threadbare. His own power going dark tells him where he is.
- **Examine on the entity's works returns accurate, terse, costly reads.** The ward: real returns in his register ("WARD: ACTIVE. LOAD: RISING.") — he is uniquely instrumented to read what the cell can only guess at. Anomalous material: high cost, thin yield, never false.
- **Examine CANNOT unmask a good copy. Locked.** A copy is a perfect model, and his instrument reads models — it reports what the model presents, which is the person, flawlessly. The epistemic horror of the truth file survives intact, with a new razor edge: **his one infallible instrument is blind precisely where the entity works, and the blindness is family resemblance.** If he ever realizes WHY Examine fails there, the realization should arrive as horror about himself. Differential tells may become possible at high class level after deep study of the phenomenon — but the entity's fidelity improves too; it is an arms race, never a solved problem.
- **Examining entity-work may be FELT.** The channel that made him runs both ways. Pointing the faculty at the thing's substance — the ward under load, a copy, the gap — risks the thing noticing the touch of something familiar. Whether it *recognizes its echo*, what it wants from him if it does, and whether his faculty is the key the gate thesis needs are late-game architecture. **Do not resolve casually.** Early on, let proximity exact costs (mana that won't return, time that runs strange across his perfect Ledger — an archive with a skip in it is a terrifying object) without explanation.
- **His mother's question lives here.** If taken minds persist inside the thing for a time (truth file: the rescue window), and his faculty is its faculty — the campaign may eventually put before him the question of whether the System can model *a person*, and what that would mean, and what it would cost. Leave this entirely unbuilt. It is endgame material.

## ORIGIN — REVELATION TIERS (the mystery he wants to chase)

Fixed so clues can be planted coherently; pace across the whole campaign. Each tier should be EARNED per table rules.

1. **The System is unique and unexplained.** No record, no rumor, no scholar, no priest, no USBD file describes screens. (He can verify this negative space himself — carefully.)
2. **It predates his awakening.** The faculty did not arrive when the core opened; the core opening *completed* something already in him. (Clues: the boot felt like a circuit closing, not a granting; the second hand in the deep plates; the scrubbed drawer.)
3. **Severn Falls, 1852, is the where and when.** The imprint entered him during the event that took his mother. (Converges with the campaign's existing Severn Falls thread — Pickering's buried story, Malcolm's knowledge, the cell's inherited files.)
4. **The channel was his mother's copy.** Assimilation runs both ways; days in the company of the thing that wore her face left the faculty in the boy. (This tier should land alongside, or hard against, the family-fracture revelations — Malcolm, Shaun, the locked drawer.)
5. **The deepest truth (preferred, may stay ambiguous to the end):** it was *given.* The copy carried enough of Rebecca to act on a mother's drive, and she pressed the faculty into her son deliberately — protection, inheritance, the only gift the thing's nature permitted. The DM may hold the line between "gift" and "side-effect" unresolved as long as it serves; if it ever resolves, it resolves to gift. His power is his mother's last act, made of the thing that killed her — and he has spent ten years swearing to master that thing without knowing he already carries its eye.

## S27 ADDENDUM — COMPREHENSION-NOT-AFFINITY; THE FILTER RACK; CONVERSION MODEL (Day 27, in the Emberwaste, locked with the player)

Three linked rulings, authored S27 to resolve how Bill relates to magic and to make the essence→mana economy mechanically exact. They cohere into one thesis: **Bill substitutes built, sighted apparatus for innate gift.** Where a caster is *born* able to run a domain and a cultivator *blindly* improves the one filter they can't see, Bill *understands* the structure and *builds/tunes* the apparatus that does the same job — at the engineer's tax (slow, lossy at first, per-build) but with no innate ceiling.

**(1) BILL HAS NO DOMAIN AFFINITY — HE HAS COMPREHENSION.** The world gates casting by affinity (`magic_domains_v1.md`): a caster runs a domain at full strength only within their affinity; a domain they lack reads clear but **won't light**. Bill is **not** an affinity-caster and does **not** get "all affinities" (that would flatten the world's magic economy — the thing that makes Charles's heal rare, Julie's orbs unsellable-to-her, restricted domains legally hot). Instead: Bill can **understand a weave's structure so completely he rebuilds and drives it from his own pool as a drafted thing**, not as a domain spell. This is the SEE→STORE→REPLICATE→UPGRADE ladder (essence_aspects §5) pointed at *spell-weaves*. **Storing** a live weave's grammar is readily doable (an active weave reads deep). **Running** it is *earned per weave*: simple-boned weaves crack fast; complex/affinity-deep ones (a battlefield heal, a forbidden domain) resist comprehension the way the licensed-Inspect upgrade did until he found the missing piece. **"Won't light" becomes "haven't cracked/built it yet."** Copying can *eventually* reach any domain — but never free, never all-at-once, and always at real cost. This preserves the soft S23 data point (the Warding tome read **thin/no-affinity/vocabulary-blank** — because a *tome is a method/score*, a different thing from a live weave Bill reads off a caster) while leaving room for his Engineer path to relate to magic *differently* (game_bible §13 / magic_domains §note both leave this open by design — now answered: comprehension, not attunement).

**(2) THE FILTER IS THE AFFINITY-SUBSTITUTE.** The apparatus that lets a comprehended weave run is a **filter** (essence_aspects §2, §4c): Bill builds a filter that conditions his pool's mana into the **grammar the weave needs**, and drives the weave off that conditioned mana. The filter does, lossily and by construction, what an affinity does for free. So "infuse a dart so it reaches an Elemental/Spirit" is not a mid-combat trick — it is a **bench project**: read the caster's weave (store the grammar), build the weave-grammar filter, then braid filter-output into the ammo (an UPGRADE-rung splice). Reveal-by-doing: the field gives Bill the *read* and the *realization of how*; the working result is earned at a bench. Filters stack (aspect-filter + weave-filter = aspected weave-fuel).

**(3) CONVERSION = FILTRATION, RATED BY % EFFICIENCY (full model in essence_aspects §4c).** Every essence→mana conversion runs through a filter. **Everyone has an innate unaspected filter (~50%)**; "learning active conversion" is **feel-tuning that innate filter upward, blind** (Julie ≈ **75%**, skilled; there's a feel-ceiling normal cultivators plateau under). **Bill's edge is sight:** he can see the filter as an object, tune it deliberately past the blind ceiling (toward ~95%+, eventually out-converting any normal cultivator), build *additional* filters, and aspect them. His first-gen *built* filters are crude (~30%, 70% lost) but visible and grindable. Essence is rated in **mana-equivalent units (ME)**: common core ~40-80 ME, Platinum boss core ~400-600 ME (more than a full pool). **Bill's current state:** innate ~50% (his passive regen); **cannot yet actively convert** (hasn't learned the continuous draw that tunes the innate filter; can't yet build a fuel-filter for banked cores). **Julie is the master class** — watching her hold the draw under load is the lesson; his anomaly is he'll *see* what she does blind and tune past it. The continuous-open-draw gate (essence_aspects §4b) is mechanically **the tuning condition** — a smooth held flow tunes the filter; start-stop can't (why Pearce caps low).

**Discipline / pacing:** none of this is a god-stat. Each filter is a build; each cross-domain copy is earned against a comprehension void; the losses make every choice a real tradeoff (burn a core for mana *or* bank it for the binding/craft, never both). The late-game consequence — Bill eventually refuels better than anyone and can reach borrowed magic no licensed caster could — is **earned, slow, build-defining, and on-thesis** (mastery-through-understanding; the man who makes the tool instead of being born the tool). - **Assembly, not processing (default — override deliberately).** The table *assembles* from prepared stock (ingots, cut stock, refined reagents). Raw refining — smelting ore to ingot, tanning, milling — still runs through the world, keeping the mercantile and `dungeon_materials_v1.md` economy in front of him. If the player wants the table to refine raw stock too, that is a further, larger boon, granted on purpose rather than assumed.
- **The tell SHARPENS; it does not soften.** Flawless, instant, forge-less output is HARDER to explain than gifted handwork — a man with no workshop turning out master-grade gear. The maker/inventor-exposure thread (`campaign_state.md`; `dm_directors_notes_v1.md` §11c) gains pressure from this, not relief. If a table-made piece is ever seen being made or sold, Bill has to fake the workshop, the hours, and the ordinary flaws. Keep it live.
