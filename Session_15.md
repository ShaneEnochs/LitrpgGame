## The day in order

**Morning — the stove, the coffee, the redraw.** Bill's first full morning under his own roof, the cold deep in the floor and the banked coal still live. He brought the stove up to real heat, set the coffee on, and finished inking the lift-catch redraw at the dry window bench — the weight-driven wedge catch (cam-faces angled so the cage's own fall drives the dog tighter into the rail, self-feeding; the spring dropped to a nudge; the taper shaped to bite progressively; fails safe on rope-slack). The bearing surfaces marked where steel quality bites. A page a smith can read and steel can be bought against.

**The wire to Malcolm — shape and a caught error.** Bill chose the **alert-plus-personal-line** shape: the patent substance held for the mailed plans, the wire carrying only a keyed alert and one father-and-son beat. First draft signed **"T."** (for "Tuesday") — caught and corrected before sending, since "Tuesday" is Pickering's working name and the runner-word to Calloway's, not anything Malcolm would read as Bill. Re-signed **Q**, the family word, the way Malcolm had signed his reply. Bill set the sequence: send the alert today, **hold the mailed plans until after Pearce reads the steel**, so the patent packet carries the version proven against real metal.

**Calloway's — the lease signed.** Bill chose the clauses-that-bite walkthrough over the full recital. Calloway named three: (1) the house is a **trust holding** — Wyatt tenant, trust owner, no Weaver anywhere — so Bill cannot prove ownership without unspooling the cover; (2) **upkeep and taxes run behind privilege**, so Bill cannot visibly improve the place past what a quiet workingman could afford; (3) a **termination clause** letting Calloway unwind the tenancy without Bill's signature if the cover ever burns — protection, and a key Calloway holds to a door Bill lives behind. Bill signed as Wyatt. A man of property under a name not his own.

**The wire taken down and sent.** Calloway wrote the keyed wire himself for the receiving channel (his office the envelope; he writes, he does not decode). Final count 44 words including both Q's; at the post-upgrade rate ~140 for the wire + ~5 handling = **~145 all-in, drawn from the trust.** Down to the depot inside the hour under the channel's name, never Bill's or Wyatt's. The mailed plans to follow the same way when ready.

**Calloway's — the hook: an anonymous letter.** Two things had come through the channel since Saturday; Bill took the hooked one first. A letter inside a letter — outer addressed to Calloway in trust, inner unnamed, the word **QUEST** on the fold. Calloway read only the outer (privilege). Bill read the inner, then handed it to Calloway to read.

The inner sheet: an unsigned note from someone who knows small-room facts — Doss filing Bill into a drawer instead of onto a list, the surface-assessment cover, the gradient finding, the three watchers by their *functions* (Sands, the awning man, the freight-window man), and the receiving channel itself. It states plainly it is **not** Sands or the watchers and positions itself above them ("they watch because they are told to; I do not need to be told"). It knows QUEST is **Bill's father's** word and how Bill keys with it. It urges Bill to keep measuring — quietly, not for the office that capped his scope — and offers contact, on Bill's terms, "the way your father taught you, not the depot; you are sitting in the cleaner road now." It frames itself as a friend doing "a version of your work longer than you have been alive," and signs only **"a friend of the work."** It tells Bill to burn it.

Calloway's read (in piles): three classes of knowledge, no single known party holds all three — family knowledge (the word and that it is the father's), present local knowledge (drawer, channel, watchers, learned *here*, recently), and the voice of an older "trade" that thinks of Bill's real work as a craft with elders. The letter's function is to **show reach and decline to use it** — a card laid face-up to be remembered. Calloway flagged the danger as the gentleness: it tells a careful, isolated man "I see you and they don't," the exact key to his lock.

**Bill names a brother.** Bill told Calloway he has a brother, **Shaun**, who knows QUEST. Left ~four years ago after a degree in **advanced mathematics** (past Bill's own understanding). No known destination; asked the family **not** to look for him and said he would find them when ready. Calloway's read: the *wait-for-me* shape fits an ordinary falling-out *and* a brilliant man quietly acquired by something nameless — the same coat on both, unreadable from outside; a mathematician's mind is exactly what powerful parties recruit. The trace would look both ways (ordinary registers vs. a clean four-year hole that is its own answer).

**Two instructions to Calloway.** (1) **Keep the letter locked in the box, not burned** — Bill will not take instructions from a man who reveals everything but his name. (2) **Trace Shaun, quiet and slow** — speed of mail and discretion, weeks not days; Bill gave the starting facts and can halt it anytime. Bill said he does not know what "trade" the letter means; Calloway declined to file the question closed.

**Calloway's — the simple good money news.** (1) The **gift cleared whole and is settled** — clean, quiet money, invisible to the watched window, the house drawn from it with much left; Bill is no longer a man counting pocket credits to decide whether to eat. (2) Malcolm's **structured remittance shape** came through (not money yet — the plan): several remittances, varied sizes, mixed origins, staggered ~two weeks, all routing to Calloway in trust, first due ~a week out. Calloway noted it is exactly what he would have advised — Malcolm structured it himself, which reads as a man who has moved money carefully before.

**The gunsmith — kit, components, and the trade.** Bill settled the kit's standing remainder in cash and took the reloading outfit outright (no clock); bought a **full starter component set** (pistol-grade powder, primers, cast lead in caliber, once-fired brass). Took the gunsmith's **free bench-side instruction** — telling a gun's fault from a shooter's, how mechanism and shot connect, loading safe on the new kit (never mix or eyeball a powder, read a case for pressure). Offered to take cold-weather backlog off him **another day** (not worked today). Warmest the relationship has been. *(The paid shooting teacher remains Crane — mornings, 50/session + ammo, gated to Doss's training condition; the gunsmith won't sell a trade that isn't his.)*

**Home — Tatch, the watch report, the errands routed.** Rather than run errands himself, Bill folded three things into one stop, sent Eli to fetch Tatch, and carried the kit home. He **read the watch report first** (his choice): the watch held clean all week through the rush; the awning man unchanged; the depot man's pattern unchanged but the wire window **busier** with rush traffic (Bill's own wire now in that stream, behind the channel's name). The flag: **three strangers this week that don't move like treasure men** — they don't ask the road or outfit; they **ask about people.** One asked a Garrity girl **who keeps the kids that run messages** (she gave nothing, reported it); one watched the freight lane over two chop-house meals; one known only secondhand. Tatch had already **pulled the littles back a half-step** and coached them (play dumb, report). Bill **left the watching to Tatch** — named it his call, his to bring the shape if it moves. Then handed off the two errands: **the mercantile lift-parts list** on the Quest account (at cost, monthly) delivered by Holt to the **Dunmore warehouse**; **the duplicate house key** cut on firm funds at the low-end farrier (Tatch to walk it himself). Neither the letter nor the brother crossed Tatch's line.

**Maddox House — notice given, the room cleared, the tie cut clean.** Bill went to give notice on the end room, thank Sarah Maddox for the arrangement, and clear out. He played it straight: gave up the room for the right reason (a held room in a full town is selfish), thanked her plainly for a fair deal extended before he'd proven himself. She took it without surprise or any pull to keep him; called the labor square against the notice — Bill walks out **owing nothing, owed nothing.** She gave her true word under the polite one: a rented room kept him hard to find, his own door puts him on a map, mind himself, and the children are met at her gate house or no house. Bill answered straight and appreciative and **cleared out clean** — the end room, the shed corner, and Tom's bench all surrendered (she routes the bench release through Hollis to Tom so Bill needn't chase it). He gathered his things the ordinary way, **drew the two ciphered notebooks and the Quest receipt slip out of the window casing** in the middle of the general gather (the casing drawing no eye), left the room swept, and carried everything home. She left his welcome at her table open as a man who lodged honestly.

**Home — the notebooks housed.** Bill ruled out the strongbox (first place a searcher opens, already holds Esther's list) and the trunk (obvious). He took the notebooks to his own room upstairs — the one part of the house the firm doesn't enter — and **seated them in the window casing there**, the old trick done better: eased the trim loose clean with his father's tools, set both notebooks into the hollow run between casing and rough frame, and seated the trim back flush and unmarked, no fresh-work tell. The receipt slip kept with them for now, flagged as the one item that ties keyed paper to a real transaction and is safest off in Calloway's box next visit. The first secret the house keeps for him.

**The notebooks solved; the afternoon squared; a meal.** Bill settled the notebooks, set the redrawn spec and working notebook by the door for the smith, drew **255 cr from the trust at Calloway's** (a second appearance in one day, noted and filed) so he could carry the steel and pay Pearce well by value without going thin, and ate a plain midday meal at his own table — the first at home at noon.

**The smith — Pearce reads the steel, and teaches.** Bill walked to the working blacksmith at the low end and sent Eli for Pearce as he went (Drovers' first, the shut assay office second). He gave the smith his business plain, named the consult up front, and **worked the rack himself while he waited** — sorting the bar stock by section and quantity against the redraw, squaring rough price, but stopping where his trade stops: he could pick the right *size*, not read which bar was true. Pearce came slow — fetched from the assay office, gathered and guarded, walking into a working smith's heat for the first time in a long while. He clocked that Bill had sorted by size and *stopped*, not played at judging the metal. Bill handed him the book open to the redraw, walked him through the load paths (what bears the life, what takes the grab, what to case-harden), named his own gap clean, and **asked Pearce to show him how the judging is done.**

Pearce taught — the ring on the anvil edge (good steel sings clean and holds the note; dead steel thuds), the grain at a filed break (fine silky grey draws and warns; coarse bright crystalline snaps cold and kills), the spark at the smith's wheel for the carbon. He sorted Bill's sized-right candidates into **true** and **don't**, naming why on each — including at least one bar that would have passed Bill's own eye clean and drawn a fatal set under a year's load. Somewhere in the middle he stopped being fetched-and-guarded and was just *working*, the most himself Bill had seen him; neither man named it.

**The buy, the fee, the next step set.** Bill bought the true bars — the right stock for each duty, Pearce's thumb on every one (~22 in steel, recoverable inside the Dunmore 265). He **paid Pearce 25** — deliberately more than the steel cost, a fee that says the reading was worth more than the metal it judged, handed as a master's fee earned, nothing that smelled of charity. Pearce did the arithmetic Bill intended (*that's over the steel*), accepted it by not making a thing of it, and the half-braced after-moment eased. Then Bill named his next gap honestly — he can shape the catch but can't judge the heat-treat — and **asked Pearce to teach him the hardening as he does it.** Pearce, having been asked to *teach* and not to *do*, twice in one afternoon, decided: he'll stand the heat-treat, read the fire while Bill does the hands, on his own terms (paid by value after, no flattery-discount, no charity) — *"You've been square twice. I'll take the third on faith."* He set the same summons (Drovers' first, office second) for when the metal's cut and ready for the fire, warned against the apprentices quenching anything unwatched, and walked out clean. Bill thanked him once and let him go.

**The warehouse — the build session (compressed).** Bill carried the steel to the Dunmore warehouse, where Holt had delivered and counter-checked the mercantile parts. Safety roles set first (cage blocked, drum chocked, no one under a movable load). Through the afternoon Bill cut the cam-faces to the redraw, shaped the dogs and the progressive taper, hung and trued the counterweight, and prepped the body for tomorrow's case-hardening — teaching Holt and Cobb the whole way (fault-to-cause, danger-discipline). By dusk the catch was **fabricated and fitted but not yet hard** — the geometry right, the fit to the cage worked out, everything waiting on Pearce's fire. The Dunmore brother saw forward motion where Day 14 there was a torn-down hoist and a promise. Bill called it as the light tipped.

**Player-set target:** the catch to be **hardened (with Pearce) and demonstrated (cut-rope, Bill under it) tomorrow, Day 16, ~3 PM.**
