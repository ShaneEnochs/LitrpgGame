## 1. THE TEAM — "THE PLATINUM FOUR" (need a 5th; want RANGE damage)

Established power: Myles, Jason, Julie did **Foundation** delves (growth item + starter intrinsic each, then 10 awakening points); the four have since cleared **3 Tier-1 Platinum** completions together (Charles joined on the 2nd Platinum, so Charles has **2**). At **10 points per Platinum**, Myles/Jason/Julie sit near **40 points each (~Delver Level 4)**, Charles near **30 (~Delver Level 3)** plus his Foundation ten — call the trio **DL 4**, Charles **DL 3**. All are **strong awakened for the frontier** (attributes concentrated in the 30s–low-40s in their lanes — comfortably past the 20 unawakened ceiling, per game_bible §5). They are an all-Platinum **purist** crew: they run only Platinum sites, refuse easier grades, and "barely make it back alive each time" (Hessler). This is why they need a clean fifth with **range** — they have melee, magic, and a healer, but no reliable ranged damage, and Platinum floor-bosses punish that gap.

**They are all friendly toward Bill.** They respect competence and don't care about cover stories; a quiet tinker with a strange silent gun who can shoot is exactly their missing piece.

### MYLES — tank / leader (DL ~4)
- **Person:** late 30s, broad, calm, the steady center the other three orbit. Ex-something-physical (smith's son / teamster). Talks little, decides fast, never panics. The one who calls the line and the retreat. Treats a new fifth as his responsibility to bring home — protective without being soft.
- **Build:** Foundation growth item = **a tower-style relic-steel shield** (grown with him; now bites back — a Foundation guard-class item). Starter intrinsic: **Bulwark** (Lv. ~3 — a hold-the-line discipline; amplifies his guard actives, anchors threat). High **Might** (~40), good **Presence**. Carries a **seated taunt/threat aura or active** that pulls monster aggression. Heavy and slow; immovable when planted.
- **Gear:** the relic-steel shield; a deep-iron arming sword; layered plate-and-mail (heat-treated — they run fire sites). Fire-resist salve.
- **In-delve role:** front wall. Plants, holds the boss's attention, calls Bill's shots ("range it, Wyatt — now").

### CHARLES — healer (DL ~3; newest)
- **Person:** 50s, dry, a little fatalistic, the conscience of the crew — the one who says "we should turn back" and gets overruled, and patches them up anyway. Former Church-adjacent or field-surgeon background (folds into the Life domain). Joined on their 2nd Platinum; even with him it's been dicey, which tells you how thin the margins are.
- **Build:** **Life-affinity caster** (the rare and valuable kind west). Foundation = a **focus/reliquary growth item** (boosts healing throughput). Spells: a battlefield mend, a stabilize, a cleanse (matters vs Infernal status — burns, brimstone-sickness). Low Might; he must be **protected**, which shapes every fight. Limited mana — he triages, he can't heal everything, which is the crew's real ceiling.
- **Gear:** Life focus; light warded robe; a satchel of his own elixirs (and the ones they buy/find).
- **In-delve role:** kept behind Myles. His mana is the clock — fights are won fast or lost slow.

### JASON — damage / fighter (DL ~4)
- **Person:** late 20s, hot, fast, grinning, the crew's edge and its risk. Julie's older brother; reckless in a way she manages. Loves the work, loves Platinum, the reason they only run Platinum (he'd be bored to death on Tin). Friendly and loud; will like Bill immediately if Bill shoots straight.
- **Build:** **melee striker.** Foundation growth item = **a relic-steel war-axe or paired blades** (grown sharp). Starter intrinsic: a **momentum/berserk-lean discipline** (Lv. ~3 — amplifies his strikes as a fight runs hot). High **Might + Precision** (~38/~35). A **Movement Skill** (a dash/leap-strike) seated — he closes distance fast and dives in, which is exactly when he needs Bill covering his back.
- **Gear:** the relic-steel weapon(s); a fire-aspected blade-edge enchant (bought with Platinum money — bites Infernal flesh harder); medium armor he's outgrown the caution of.
- **In-delve role:** the spear-tip. Dives bosses' flanks; gets in trouble; Bill and Myles get him out.

### JULIE — damage / mage (DL ~4)
- **Person:** mid-20s, controlled, precise, the one who actually plans the fights Jason throws himself into. Jason's younger sister; the brains/temper-brake of the pair. Cooler to a stranger than Jason at first, warms once Bill proves he listens. The closest in temperament to Bill (a planner) — a natural rapport if the player wants it.
- **Build:** **offensive caster** — most likely **Arcane or Transmutation affinity** (NOT Life — Charles holds that). Foundation = a **catalyst/wand growth item**. Spells: a ranged bolt/lance, an area effect she places carefully, a control/debuff. **Intellect-heavy** (~40 — big mana pool for her level). The crew's burst damage at range — but cast-time and mana-limited, so she and Bill's gun *complement* (he covers her cast windows; she opens bosses for his shots). **Note the aspect interaction:** in a fire/earth site her offense may run *worse* if her affinity clashes, *better* if it leans earth/transmutation — flavor her competence around it.
- **Gear:** the catalyst; warded light robe; essence stones (she carries them — the on-ramp to active conversion, a thing Bill will *recognize* now that he's read the conversion canon; a possible learning seed).
- **In-delve role:** ranged burst + control. Pairs with Bill as the crew's two ranged guns.

**Team tactics (so in-delve runs as "follow orders"):** Myles plants and holds aggro; Charles heals from cover behind Myles; Jason dives the flank; Julie and Bill range from the back, covering casts and divers. Standard call order is Myles's. Bill's job is **discipline**: shoot what he's told, reload fast (the speedloader and the coil gun both earn their keep), don't freelance. This is by design the delve where Bill is a **soldier, not a commander** — the player asked for minimal in-delve decisions.

## 3. THE EMBERWASTE — the delve (5 floors, open hellish exterior, Infernal, fire+earth)

Through the arch is **not a cave** — it opens onto a burning country under a bruised, smokeless red sky: cracked black hardpan, rivers of slow fire in the seams, ash drifting like snow, heat that tests the lungs while the air gauges fine (essence-thick, fire-aspected — the blue-flame logic in reverse: flames here burn *too* hot and bright). The "floors" are **traversal layers** — each gated by a **threshold** (a ridge of slag, a fire-river crossing, a collapsed arch) that won't pass until that layer's **floor boss** falls; crossing a threshold drops the party into a deeper, worse layer of the same hellscape. One arch in, one arch out (back through the entry).

**Loot/economy weighting (Bill's fuel run):** monsters drop **fire-aspected and earth-aspected cores** in quantity (Infernal flesh + the earth bones of the wasteland) — exactly what feeds the starving earth-filter and banks toward the maker-core draught. Per-person completion chest (graded by contribution) on the dungeon boss. Safe rooms hold **potions/elixirs** (heal, fire-ward, stamina, a rare mana-restorative) and the occasional cached core. Essence stones appear as drops (relevant to Julie, and to Bill's latent active-conversion arc).

### Floor 1 — THE CINDER FLATS
- **Terrain:** open ash plain, low fire-seams, scattered slag pillars for cover.
- **Mobs:** **Ashhounds** (Beast/Infernal — fast pack predators, fire-aspected, brittle to range — Bill's intro; he picks them off as Jason wades in); **Cinder-crawlers** (small earth-aspected Construct vermin — drop earth chips). Easy warm-up by Platinum standards; teaches the crew's rhythm and Bill's role.
- **Floor boss — THE ASH-MAW HOUND** (area boss): a pack alpha the size of a horse, fire-breath cone, summons two ashhounds mid-fight. Myles eats the cone on the shield; Bill and Julie range the adds; Jason flanks. Drops a **fire-aspected core (clean)** + the threshold opens.

### Floor 2 — THE SLAGWORKS
- **Terrain:** a broken industrial-looking expanse of cooled lava terraces and standing furnaces (the wasteland *looks* made here — an Infernal echo of a forge-country; quietly rhymes with Bill's maker-core, DM-only flavor).
- **Mobs:** **Slag-golems** (Earth/Construct — slow, armored, weak to precise shots at the molten seams — Bill's Precision shines: "hit the glow, not the rock"); **Emberwisps** (Elemental — float, resist physical, need Julie's casts or a fire-ward — teaches the crew's division of labor).
- **Floor boss — THE FURNACE-WARDEN** (floor boss): a towering slag-and-iron construct with a molten core visible through chest-vents; smashes and vents fire-bursts. **The fight Bill's gun is *for*** — its core only opens for a beat after it vents, and a clean ranged shot into the vent staggers it. Myles holds, Julie cracks the vents, **Bill puts the staggering shots in**, Jason finishes. Drops a **strong earth-aspected core** + a **fire-ward elixir** + the threshold.

### Floor 3 — THE BRIMSTONE REACH (safe room before)
- **Safe room** here: a wind-sheltered slag hollow, breathable, where the crew rests, splits found elixirs, and Charles recovers mana. **Roleplay beat** — the crew talks, Bill listens (and reads them); a natural place for Julie's essence-stones / active-conversion seed, or Jason's reckless-Platinum philosophy, or Charles's fatalism. Bill banks cores here, feels the filter's hunger ease as earth cores accumulate.
- **Terrain:** sulfur fields, geysers of fire on timers (a hazard-traversal layer — read the timing, cross between bursts).
- **Mobs:** **Brimstone imps** (Infernal — small, fast, cackling, throw fire — annoying swarmers, Bill's reload discipline tested); **Tar-lurkers** (Ooze/Infernal — resist physical, immobilize — Julie/Charles problem). Status pressure (burns, brimstone-sickness) makes Charles's cleanse matter; his mana clock tightens.
- **Floor boss — THE IMP-PRINCE** (floor boss): a larger imp commander that buffs the swarm and teleports short hops (hard to pin — Bill's ranged tracking vs a blinking target, a real skill check). Drops a **fire-aspected core** + an **essence stone** + the threshold.

### Floor 4 — THE OBSIDIAN STAIR
- **Terrain:** the worst traversal — a rising shelf of black glass over a lake of fire, narrow, exposed, no cover. The crew is strung out; this is where "barely make it back" earns its name. A **fall** here is lethal.
- **Mobs:** **Glass-wraiths** (Spirit/Infernal — resist physical, drain heat/essence as they pass — Bill *feels* the essence-drain with his open core, a nasty echo of the Reach pull, DM-only resonance to note); **Magma-serpents** (Beast/Elemental — erupt from the fire-lake, snap, submerge — ambush predators, Myles can't tank what he can't reach, so Bill/Julie must watch the lake).
- **Floor boss — THE CINDER-DRAKE** (near-dungeon-boss-class floor boss): a lesser fire-drake, flies, strafes the stair with fire, lands to bite. **The crew's hardest fight before the boss** — it punishes the no-cover terrain, forces Charles to overspend, and is exactly why they wanted a fifth gun (someone to *hit a flier* while Julie controls and Jason can't reach). Bill's discipline and the coil gun's clean ranged punch are the difference. Drops a **strong fire-aspected core** + a **rare mana-restorative elixir** + the threshold to the final layer.

### Floor 5 — THE HEART OF THE WASTE (dungeon boss)
- **Terrain:** a vast caldera, a lake of slow fire ringed by black stone, the air shimmering, the essence so thick Bill's core *thrums* (rich fuel everywhere — the binding-draught environment).
- **DUNGEON BOSS — THE EMBER-COLOSSUS** (dungeon boss): a mountain-shouldered Giant/Infernal of fused slag and living fire, an earth body with a fire heart, slow but devastating — ground-slams (quake + fissures), a fire-nova on a timer (the *clear-the-area* mechanic — Myles can't tank it, everyone must use the terrain/safe-spots Bill can read), and an exposed **molten heart-core** that only opens after it slams (the **kill window**: it slams → heart exposed for a beat → coordinated burst). The whole fight is the crew's tactics in miniature: Myles holds and reads the slam tells, Charles triages the nova, Jason and Julie burst the window, **Bill puts the precise heart-shots in.** Winnable in 2–3 attempts of the window if the party is disciplined; a wipe if they're not (Platinum-real).
- **On the boss's death:** the win condition completes, the **per-person chests** appear (graded by contribution — Bill's range and discipline earn a full, real share), and the boss/site respawn. Heavy **earth + fire core** drops, an **Infernal-aspected material**, elixirs.

### The QUESTS BOX (new, on examining the entry arch — player-authored)
When the party examines the arch, the new window returns:
- **MAIN QUEST: Defeat the Ember-Colossus.** Reward (non-money, scales Platinum): **a Platinum completion's points (10) + a per-person graded chest.** *(Standard win; stated explicitly now under the new system.)*
- **BONUS QUEST 1 — "Unbroken": clear all five floor bosses without a party death.** Reward: **a guaranteed aspected core of choice (fire or earth) per delver** (a fuel bonus — *directly* serves Bill's filter/binding; a reason he quietly wants the clean run the crew already wants).
- **BONUS QUEST 2 — "The Furnace's Due": destroy the Furnace-Warden's core (Floor 2) rather than just defeating it.** Reward: **a fire-aspected crafting material** (Infernal forge-stock — rhymes with the maker-core/made-stock thread; a maker's prize).
- *(Rewards are never money. Higher tier/grade = better rewards — this is the Platinum table; lower grades give lesser non-money rewards. The bonus quests are the new system giving crews reasons to take on extra risk for loot, not coin.)*

**Bill's read on the box (DM):** the bonus rewards being *aspected cores and forge-stock* is, to Bill, almost pointed — the system handing him exactly the fuel his filter and maker-core hunger for. He won't know why. (DM-secret: the waking cores; the system reshaping itself; possibly his own great core's resonance leaking preference. Do not resolve.)

## 4. THE WAKING CORES (player-authored cosmology — DM-ONLY, guarded)

**Canon set by the player:** the delve cores — the obelisk-cores that *run* delves — are quietly becoming **more sapient.** No one in the world knows. The **Monday-night change** (party-size gates; the quests box; non-money scaling rewards) is an *outward symptom* of this — the system the cores administer is **reshaping itself as the cores wake**, growing more deliberate, more game-like, more *authored*. The cause is above any in-world investigation for now; leave every in-world explanation as guesswork (Bill's "was it my Monday delve?" included — a real and unanswerable suspicion).

**Bill's great forge-core is sapient too** — a recovered class-resonant growth item, and (uniquely) *his*. The arc:
1. **First faint impression** — fired S24 night (a wordless *noticed-you-noticing* feeling as he set it down after feeding it). **Reveal-by-doing, very slow.**
2. **Growing impressions** — as Bill uses/feeds/cycles it, brief wordless senses: preference (it "leans toward" certain fuel — aspected, forge-kin), mood (warmer when fed, dormant when starved), attention (a felt regard during deep work). Pace these over multiple sessions; never a word yet.
3. **Binding deepens the bond** — when the maker-core finally binds (after the fuel + level conditions are met), the impressions sharpen toward *intent*; the construct-building feels collaborative, not tool-use.
4. **Eventually it SPEAKS** — a **bonded familiar**, a voice/presence Bill can converse with. Far horizon. When it comes, it should feel *earned* and a little uncanny — the engineer's tool turning out to have been a *who* the whole time, which rhymes hard with the campaign's central question (understanding-as-mastery vs the thing understood looking back) and with the Mirrorwell's copies. Hold the line; do not rush to the voice.

**Discipline:** this is potent and central; keep it *quiet and slow*. Impressions for a long while; the voice is a major beat, not a near one. Tie its preferences to Bill's fuel economy (it *wants* aspected/forge-kin cores — which conveniently points Bill at exactly the delves and loot his build needs). Never let it solve problems for Bill or hand him answers; it's a presence and a relationship, not an oracle.
