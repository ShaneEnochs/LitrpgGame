## The throughline

Bill Weaver — true name held by very few — stepped off the rail two miles out and walked into New Abilene as **"Wyatt,"** a fix-it man. The cover was the work: understand the dungeon at **Hollow Reach** and the wrong machinery moving under this frontier without being seen doing it. Over five days he built a life dense enough to stand on, declined the trap that was dressed as a survey, and found a third way into the question that had nothing to do with going down a hole on someone else's terms.

He built standing the way an engineer does — by fixing what was visibly broken. Hollis Vane's wagon brake on the road in; Mrs. Maddox's stairs, window, and pump (work that outdid her late builder-husband's, and won the house for good); the mission's rotted sill. Competence, offered plainly and taught to whoever was nearby, spread his name ("Wyatt, fix-it man, Maddox House") down every teamster route in town.

**The names he wears** (full map in `bill_weaver_sheet.json` → identity): *Wyatt* to the town and trades; *John* to Esther and the mission (she knows it's false and approves); *John-Wyatt* to Mrs. Maddox; *Tuesday* to Pickering and as the runner-word to Calloway's. The true name went only to Hessler (the guild roll, alias noted), Calloway (sealed schedule), and — by deliberate choice, under a confidentiality promise — **Pickering**, along with the Malcolm/Combine connection, his mother's death in one of the first breaks, and the real purpose. Rusk was given no name at all, and was glad of it.

## Hollow Reach — the shape of the problem

The site is an ordinary old **adit mine** northwest of town, pre-Appearance by decades; a dungeon **mouth** manifested in its deep workings during the first wave twelve years ago and was ward-sealed shortly after. It sat sealed about a decade until the recent reopening. A 1852 seal-wall stands in the passage between the 2nd and 3rd galleries; the breach is in that wall.

What Bill assembled from Pem, Hollis, Esther, Pickering, Doss, and the survivor Rusk: a **rising kill-curve.** The 1st crew came back whole but silent (paid or scared off). The 2nd came back two short — **Amos Vane**, Esther's reckless younger brother, was one of the two. The 3rd went down and **none returned.** The danger escalates, which means a cause: something growing, waking, or being fed. A supply manifest of timber, lime, and lamp oil read as shoring, mortar, and light — going deep, possibly sealing or burying.

The machinery around the site mattered as much as the site. A **commissioning party** (the cell) controls the archive — every field document is collected under the writ; Garlan's log of the breached wall is gone from Doss's office. The hall has no alchemist and no runecrafter; anything essence-sensitive, Bill must supply himself. The placement contract carried a **spine clause** with a kill-switch ("render stable *or certify it cannot be done*") that only opens from the bottom of the hole — bait for honest officers — and a **hidden schedule of recovery** incorporated by reference that signers never see: a document, contractually required to exist, naming what the crews are really sent to fetch.

## The third path

Bill **declined the placement.** The chair was the contract, and the contract was the trap. Instead he took a route that reconciled license, money, and survival: **Pickering paid the license fee (~1,950 cr) outright**, and Bill entered as a paid provisional, signing only a fee receipt. Crucially, **Doss filed him UNNAMED** — retained under hall miscellany, off the return — owning the exposure herself rather than sending his name up the cell's channel. *"This isn't safety. It's time."* In exchange she granted a **surface-assessment of the mouth** (scope capped, findings to her office) and yard access. It was the warmest the relationship had been; it also left the central question of the campaign open — what Bill would go down for, and whether he'd go down at all.

Two instruments were built and lodged with Calloway, both still live in current canon:
- **The patronage contract** — Bill as Pickering's discreet, anonymous source, every duty gated on Pickering's performance and limited to lawful, post-return, truthful accounts. Signed by cipher, identity on a single sealed schedule. Pickering performed early and in full.
- **The poison-pill account** — nine pages sealed in Calloway's box, published to named parties on the death or disappearance of **Bill or Rusk.** This is the origin of the standing Rusk protection and Pickering's press-in-reserve.

Calloway's doctrine for the whole enterprise, stated here and load-bearing ever since: **"Be the works, never the purse"** — and the warning that Bill was becoming *arithmetic* to anyone keeping a ledger on the town.

## What got built

- **The firm.** Founded out of the Tatch network: **Tatch** coordinator, **Min** treasurer, **Cobb** on the box (and first journeyman, taught a sellable trade), with an outer circle — the **Garrity sisters**, **Holt**, **Bess** (proven on her first watcher-pass), **Ada**. **Eli Garner**, an uninvited boy, was sent to earn a proper name under Tatch. The **mercantile retainer** (25/mo, Thursday rounds) and the salve consignment at the guild cage (via Hessler) gave it clean income. *(Current roster + voice → `npcs/the_firm.md`; the original loot-share partner Pem is his own file, → `npcs/pem.md`.)*
- **Training arranged.** Marguerite (blade), Crane (revolver), Dr. Arrowood (field medicine + two books). **Pearce the assayer** was named as the clean road to chemistry texts — first mention; not yet met.

## The enemy, and the threads left running

- **The watcher** — a cell screener who worked the depot on train days, then the guild, then escalated to the mission's front door, asking after "men in town taking work who aren't what they say." Bill never once gave him his face; the discipline held. (Live → `campaign_state.md`.)
- **Mr. Sands** — the first enemy-side name. A clean-nailed man tried to "settle Amos's contract early" on Esther with 1,000 cr in paper she refused to sign, and left a depot-telegraph contact protocol for if Amos "turns up." Matches Rusk's hush man.
- **The NW land assembly** — via **Quill** the scrivener (who doesn't grasp its weight): someone is quietly perfecting title to dead claims northwest of town — five or six instruments in five weeks, different agents, **one drafter's hand under all of it** — over ground that may contain Hollow Reach. *"The first loud footstep doubles every price behind it. The quiet is the discount."* Quill entered Bill's protect column.
- **Mrs. Maddox's shed** — the **five strange boards** (denser than oak, a gray cast in the grain; suspected ghostwood, undefined) and the **lidded box** she took into the house with "I know what it is." Both her property; both still open.
- **Carried open:** the Vane kinship (Hollis ↔ Esther, unchecked); the **clock discrepancy** (a false all's-well report came up the hole after the 3rd crew was taken — discoverable); the hidden schedule of recovery; the fever thread at the mission and Sister Beatrice's withheld observation; the school seed (lodged with Beatrice; Father Lessard the slow gate); Doss's unsaid thing.
