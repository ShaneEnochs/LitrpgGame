# MYSTERY ANCHORS (DM-ONLY CANON, v1)

**DO NOT REVEAL DIRECTLY — AND PLAYER: STOP READING HERE.** You commissioned this file in the campaign audit so the minor mysteries have fixed answers and clues can converge on something real. None of it is meant to reach you except through Bill's own work. Add it to the project unread.

**What this file is.** The threads list in `campaign_state.md` carries several live mysteries whose answers were never fixed anywhere — they were marked "undefined in play, develop per secrets rules" in the session archives. "Mysteries stay solvable" (game_bible §3) requires the answer to exist *before* the clues land, or improvisation eventually contradicts itself. This file fixes those answers.

**Precedence and scope.** `game_bible.md` wins on all surface facts. The four truth files win absolutely on their subjects — where an anchor touches the central mystery, it elaborates inside that file's stated flexibility and never overrides it. `living_world_truth_v1.md` tracks outward footprints only and defers here for the answers behind them. This file is **static**: it changes only when a new mystery needs an answer fixed, never as part of normal play. Each entry gives the answer, the discovery roads, and what the thing is NOT — the negative matters, because not every mystery should lead to the cell.

## 1. THE NW LAND ASSEMBLY — Coulter & Reese (defers to `hollow_reach_truth_v3.md` on the cell)

**The answer, locked:** Coulter & Reese Land Co. is a front retained by the cell at one remove — Ohio shell paper, agents under recorded powers, all of it drafted by a single Saint Orison hand the cell's counsel uses for work that must not carry a Bureau name. The purpose: control of the ground around the adit, so that access, witnesses, supply, and eventually the keystone parcel all run through ground the commissioning party owns. The reclassification (the cell's own cover move) was always going to pull strangers up the NW road; the perimeter had to exist before they came.

**The haste-tell, explained:** the assembly's patient phase and crew 4's descent were two limbs of one upstream decision — the active phase. When the order came down, the land agents were told to close the surrounding parcels before the standardization wire reached print and moved prices and attention. Quill saw the seam: patient men engrossing filings in haste the morning the wagons rolled, because both ran on the same clock and only one of them was visible.

**The keystone race (now live):** the adit parcel is unpatented public ground and must be taken loudly — county process or patent. The public rush gives rivals a reason to file first, so the front must move on the keystone within weeks. This is the pressure that produces Quill's grievance moment (an agent leaning on the land-office counter), and it is the window for Bill's banked question — *what loud true thing, on what morning* — which remains his to answer, never the DM's.

**Discovery roads:** the recurring Saint Orison drafting hand across otherwise unrelated paper (Calloway can see the pattern once he has two documents to lay side by side); a locally hired parcel guard who talks for a drink; Quill handing over the keystone filing as a grievance or not at all (his protect-column status is absolute); no-trespass friction with the rush, printed in the *Clarion* as ordinary land trouble.

**NOT:** a Combine play; not a windfall speculator. The discoverable tell is exactly that — the buying began before any treasure story existed. Patient money that knew before the public did.

## 2. THE DAY-10 CREW-CALL — the Aldine hole

**The answer, locked:** a clearance contract on a privately owned **Tier 2 Iron** site in the Cinder foothills — local name **the Aldine hole**, owner of record *Aldine Mineral & Land* of Cleveland, absentee eastern paper. The agent prices the work thin against Tier 2 danger and has standing instructions to keep the owner's name off frontier postings, which is why the principal goes unnamed. The site's six-month clearance window runs out **near the end of December**.

**The clock:** if the posting keeps sitting — and the rush is pulling exactly the experienced crews it needs toward the Special hole instead — the guild escalates pay, then the Bureau gets involved, and the territory gets a live break-risk on a Tier 2 site in mid-winter, when the passes are worst. This is the mundane disaster the young regime permits: ordinary absentee-capital negligence, not malice.

**Discovery roads:** Hessler grumbling the arithmetic at the counter; Bly's crew having passed on it ("Tier 2 risk at Tier 1 rates"); a *Clarion* notice if the window lapses. **NOT:** the cell. Keep it cleanly mundane — its function is to show the clearance law's economy working badly, and to hang a real regional danger in the background that nobody is conspiring to cause.

## 3. THE PALACE ANNEX SECOND ROOM

**The answer, locked:** the room stands ready for **someone expected**. The screener was instructed to hold a second room — paid weekly, made up, never slept in — for a man who comes when the crew-4 result lands: the settlement function staged in advance. (Whether the arrival is the hush man himself or another hand serving the same body stays flexible; `hollow_reach_truth_v3.md` governs who they serve.)

**Pem's product:** the Day-6 division of ground has had a week to work. His outfit can now establish exactly the room's shape — paid, made up, the maid never needed — and that shape is tradeable product under the Compact's terms. **The room filling is the town-side tell that the cell's posture has changed.** Spend it as such.

## 4. THE HIDDEN SCHEDULE OF RECOVERY (defers to `hollow_reach_truth_v3.md`)

**The contents, locked.** The writ's working annex — a copy traveled down in Garlan's pack; the master sits in the writ-holder's files; a clerk's draft may survive in cage paperwork (Hessler's untaken manifest offer is adjacent ground). What it requires of each descent:

- Mortar warmth logged at marked stations, morning and evening.
- Lamp-flame height at fixed distances, in fractions against a sound lamp.
- Paired watches, mechanical against subjective, drift logged daily.
- The gap measured each day, in the same hand, on the same form.
- Samples up: mortar, timber, air in stoppered bottles.
- The men: each man's sleep, appetite, and temper noted daily; any change examined in private and his account taken down before he speaks to the others.

**What damns it:** no relics line. No assay line. Nothing a loot writ would ask for — and questions no honest holder of a "worthless hole" would know to ask. It reads like survey instructions written by someone who already knew what the hole would do. To Bill specifically, it reads as a colleague's instrument program — which is its own horror, because it means the principal measures the way he does.

## 5. DOSS'S UNSAID THING

**The answer, locked:** she offered Bill the sixth chair before she understood what she was staffing. She has since worked out what the two-day reversal of her suspension means — that the people above the writ want the descents to continue, and that the site was never survey work — and she has carried, every day since, the knowledge that she nearly fed him to it. The unsaid thing is an apology and a warning in one.

**When it lands:** at the moment his data formally enters the writ channel, or the first time someone above her reaches toward him — whichever the player's choices produce. Her line, hers to deliver in her own words, to this effect: *"I put your name on a chair at that hole before I understood what I was staffing. You said no, and I've been glad of it every day since. Don't let anyone put you down it. Me included."* This recolors the Session 2–3 offer, the unnamed filing, the winch-pawl cover, and "lay eyes on you after" as one continuous act of protection. It confirms no mechanism and names no employer.

## 6. THE FIVE STRANGE BOARDS and THE LIDDED BOX (Mrs. Maddox)

**The boards, locked:** genuine **ghostwood** (per `dungeon_materials_v1.md`). Tom Maddox took the five boards in part-payment off a first-wave hauler in the early years, when "dungeon wood" was a curiosity without a market. He meant to build her a sewing cabinet of it and never did. She knows what it is, knows roughly what it would fetch now, and will not sell Tom's cabinet, built or not.

**The box, locked:** Tom's effects. His teamster's license, his pipe, letters in his hand — and a small **starfall glass** paperweight he overpaid for at a depot stall, the one impractical thing the careful man ever brought home. "I know what it is" meant exactly that.

**NOT:** cell material, not a plot device, not treasure. The payoff is Maddox herself — what it means the day she chooses to show Bill either one. If Bill ever identifies the boards aloud to her, what it earns him is the story, not the lumber. Late-game practical note: ghostwood takes enchantment safely (catalog); if Quest ever needs a small piece of dungeon material for sound work, the boards exist — and that road runs through her trust only, at her say, never through a price.

## 10. THE POISON PILL — DETONATION MECHANICS

**The mechanics, locked.** Trigger: credible word of Bill's death OR Rusk's death reaches Calloway (the Rusk watch's standing rule — "if I go quiet, the watch ends that day, one runner to the lawyer" — is one arming wire). On trigger, Calloway releases Pickering's copy and sends word to the diocese to open theirs (the altar-stone leg, already in place since Day 4).

**Effects, fixed so they play consistently:**
- Pickering prints. The instrument instructs it and his instincts agree; the *Clarion* runs the nine pages' substance as an accusation of a covered site and paid silences.
- The diocese acts on its own clock — slower, institutional, harder to intimidate, with its own stake (the fever wing, the mission, Amos's free Sundays).
- The cell's countermeasures land on the paper and the marshal, not on the truth; the site becomes briefly impossible to staff; an inquiry convenes in some form; if Bill is the trigger, parts of his alias web surface in the reading.

**The hard note:** nobody hostile knows the pill exists, so **it deters no one — it avenges.** It does not protect Rusk; it answers for him. If it is ever to deter, someone has to be told it exists, and telling is itself a dangerous play (it informs the cell they were documented). That choice is Bill's, never the DM's; this entry exists so a Rusk-hunt beat is played with the detonation consequences already decided.

## 11. THE GAUGE HEARTS — what fouls a sensing element (the bench as listening post)

**The physics, locked (small, surface-consistent):** an essence-gauge's sensing element takes a **set** from sustained exposure to strongly abnormal essence:
- **Strong-set** — from over-strong environments (deep dungeon environs, strong-essence chambers). The element darkens faintly, reads high, won't settle. Common enough that makers know it.
- **Thin-set** — from abnormally **thin** essence. The element bleaches, reads low or dead-flat, won't settle downward. **Nearly unheard of out west**; almost no frontier maker has seen one, because the only thin-essence ground in the territory is the kind nobody works.

**Bly's gauge (Day 10), fixed:** strong-set — fouled in an ordinary strong-essence chamber on a close site weeks back. Mundane. The pattern channel starts honest.

**The listening-post discipline:** Bill's bench now sees the territory's instruments. Log, DM-side, every heart symptom and any *volunteered* "where it was working" — Bill never asks twice (asking maps his interest, and the player knows it). The pattern accumulates slowly across sessions. A thin-set heart arriving someday, off gear that worked near the Reach, is the rare anomaly his two-data-point diagnostic (catch = body; won't-settle = heart) is already built to catch. No shortcuts: the bench data informs, it never confirms — `hollow_reach_truth_v3.md` governs everything it points at.

## 12. THE FEVER WING, IN WINTER (defers to `hollow_reach_truth_v3.md`)

**The rule, locked:** the wing fills every winter and **nearly all of it is ordinary fever** — that is the cover and it is true. The residue, never confirmed and never explained on screen: a case or two each season whose course fits no fever Arrowood knows — recovered men wrong-footed for weeks after, sleeping badly, wanting lamps. Beatrice's withheld observation stays hers; her door opens on her clock or not at all. The ambiguity is the point; play the residue at one or two details a season, no more.
