## MARGUERITE — BLADE LESSON #4 (the last)

At the slaughterhouse on a killing morning. Marguerite set the fourth lesson as the one words can't teach: driving a blade through a joint that resists — *committing*, not easing off at the refusal. Bill made the cut. The skin gave, the meat dragged, the joint said no — and he didn't stop; he put his shoulder behind the hand and followed the line through, the joint opening like the hinge it was, and caught the overrun (the Precision that can't be taught). Marguerite, watching how he caught it, called his hand finished for what she had to give: *"Your hand's better than mine, steadier. I don't think there's much left I can give you that your own hands won't teach you faster."* She closed the lessons — **this was the last** — with a standing open-counter offer: she'll tell him how any particular beast comes apart, free, across the counter.
- **Melee (Blades) 3 → 4** (the "means-it" / live-resisting-cut gate crossed).
- **New subskill — Butchery / anatomy of the cut 4** (joints, hinges, weak seams; transfers partway to where a thing comes apart in a fight).

## THE ARGENT HALL — THE BOARD, THE PICKLE, HESSLER, THE GHOST-CORE

The hall was loud — the whole territory in at once making their numbers add up, the contract board thick as a notice-wall. The Platinum crew's pin sat apart, careful even hand: *WANTED — ONE RELIABLE RANGE. Established crew, all-Platinum… Ask the cage for Myles.*

**The pickle run.** Bill grabbed a runner outside: 5 cr up front for two pickles, 5 more if the boy beat Bill to the front of the cage line. The boy beat it; paid off (10 cr total). One pickle to Hessler, who eased at the gesture.

**Hessler's warning (player-godhanded earlier; run straight).** Asked to sign for Myles's crew, Hessler spent his standing to warn first: the Platinum Four are the real article — good, honest, pay square — but all-Platinum purists who "barely make it back." Their healer earns his pay in the field, hours out; what Hessler sees is the *coming back* — the crew limping through the door days later, drawn white, strapped up, the healer hollowed out, calling it a good run because nobody died *that* time. They need a fifth with range because the four keep nearly not making it back. Bill heard it, took it to heart, and stepped over it with eyes open: *"I came across the continent to do this. I want to hit the final tier. I want the secrets of these delves. Tin won't get me there; Platinum might."* Hessler re-filed him (not the wandering fix-it man — a man who said *final tier* out loud and meant it), respected the choice, and signed him **forward to the tryout** rather than *to* Myles (a crew that turns men away looks a man over first). Bill filled the crew sign-on form (working name **"Wyatt"**, standing **Provisional**, brings **Range**). Hessler will send Bill's word + the cage's vouch to Myles today; a runner brings a where-and-when (tonight or tomorrow); tryout in town tomorrow late afternoon, shoot at targets. *"That crew reads late as a character flaw."*

**The weave-reading practice + the ghost-core.** While in line, Bill ran a soft wide passive Forceread cone (the dull suppression leak as cover) across the crowded hall — the richest live reading since the auction, dozens of working weaves at once (movement, passive, suppression of many makes, reading-family), sharpening his SEE rung by volume. One thing snagged: a core *quieter than it should be* — a genuinely clean suppression, seams tight, deep in the hall. Bill's Intellect worked it carefully: the cell hides *inside* the Bureau apparatus, so the hall is exactly its habitat — there's no rule it can't be here; Doss cleared only the *depot man*, not a roster. He filed it *worth a face, not flagged*. Leaning the cone (soft, not a hard probe) toward it resolved a reading-family weave under the suppression — the profile of a watcher *and* of a dozen harmless trades. Hessler (asked quiet) guessed the most ordinary answer: the licensed reader who works the assay counter, who suppresses for her trade. Bill drifted back (cone low, no ping) and the bearing walked him to the **assay counter** — the ghost was the **licensed assayer**, a plain severe woman in her forties, spectacles, ink-stained, brass credential, bent over a core, reading. He filed the face (the Ledger), read her live Inspect against his own Forceread (the formal trained version of what he does — modest reach, clean graded return, where his reaches deeper and returns messier), and confirmed: the most alarming silhouette resolved into the most innocent person. Filed, not flagged. (A small private aside: a town "starved for a licensed assayer" with a licensed reader at the guild counter — could be auction-day only, could be the Pearce thesis is about the *private* trade; noted, not today's problem.)

## THE GUNSMITH — COIL GUN BUILD BEGINS; SPEEDLOADER FINISHED

**The speedloader/revolver, finished & proven.** The gunsmith finished it that morning, bench-tested and ran it live out back before handing it over ("wasn't going to hand you a thing I hadn't fired"). Works perfectly — charges all six, drops them home clean, cuts the reload to a fraction. An old hand watching wanted to know where to buy one.
- **Engineering · firearm mechanism 2 → 4** (the banked bump lands — fabricated and proven; catches the firearm-mech branch up to where Bill's other branches live). Engineering parent holds at 7.

**The coil gun, build begins.** Split the work: the gunsmith cuts steel to Bill's tight tolerances (barrel roughed, chamber-work started, coil-stop spacing laid out); Bill goes into the Drafting trance to bridge the drive (complete in design, unslotted until there's a finished gun to slot against tomorrow) to the physical bore. **A bench-side epiphany:** watching the actual steel, Bill saw the first coil-stop was set a hair too far from the chamber mouth — a few thousandths of dead travel before the drive grabs the slug. He pencilled the correction onto the plans (move the stop in toward the mouth; kill the dead travel; cleaner first-stage bite → a fraction less essence per shot), explained the *why* in shop language the gunsmith could act on without the essence theory. The gunsmith got it, hadn't cut past it, took it in — the coil gun's design ceiling nudged up before it's even finished. Bill **left all the plans** with the gunsmith to study overnight (a real, deepening trust; the part that's only in Bill never went on paper). **Build finishes & test-fires Friday → slot-and-fire = the own-draft built-and-proven that awards class Lv.2.**

## CRANE'S RANGE — DRILLING THE SPEEDLOADER

Cold near-empty range. Crane framed the reload as the thing that keeps men alive: not the bench reload, the *blind, under-threat, hands-not-eyes* reload. Crane named the rest as the part the range can't give: real pressure, a real threat. Bill stopped at the diminishing return.
- **Shooting holds at 5** — the named gate (blind-reload-under-threat) wants the threat the range can't supply; tomorrow's tryout and Saturday's hole are the live reps. Bill's hands now run the speedloader by feel, eyes-up (a real concrete improvement carried into both).

## CALLOWAY — THE PEARCE PARTNERSHIP SET FOR FRIDAY 10:00

At lamp-lighting, Professional Street. Calloway already knows Pearce (has done legal business with him as an assayer) and has spent the month counseling Bill on the courtship; Pearce already knows Calloway is Bill's lawyer (told up front; Pearce trusts him). Bill reported the office meeting worked — Pearce named the partnership himself, waived his fee, handed the portfolio back — and asked Calloway to draw it, the three at his table, but wanted it heard cold first. Calloway, genuinely pleased his counsel bore out, gave the things-you-didn't-think-to-ask:
- **Even-handed drafting is what makes it hold** — he acts for the partnership, straight and fair both ways (already open between all three; not a reveal).
- **Bind his fortune to your silence, not his tongue** — equity forfeited if he talks, money clawed back, his licensed name on every filing beside Bill's, so betrayal razes him too. Strong with Pearce specifically: his deepest fear is being broke-and-shelved again; a structure that puts him back there if he talks holds tighter than any oath.

- **Timing — Bill's Insight read it:** Pearce's habit is afternoon, but he asked Bill to his office *first thing* for the office meeting — the *eager* hour, not the comfortable one. The morning is where his enthusiasm lives; the comfortable afternoon gives his pride room to find the clever objection. **Set firm at 10:00 Friday** (rides the eager hour, threads between the gunsmith first-thing and the afternoon tryout). Calloway sends Pearce the note tonight on his own letterhead — *requests the pleasure… to settle the articles of Quest Instruments* — a formal summons that makes it real and gives the pride no slack. Calloway draws the bones by the sitting; the fine clauses he finishes while Bill is away at the delve; Bill signs when back. (Calloway's one sober note: a Platinum hole is the one room his paper can't protect — get the protectable things squared first, which is half of what Friday is for anyway; a man settling his affairs before danger is the sober thing.)

**The lamps studied (intact, resaleable).** Bill finally put his hands in the auction lamp lot before returning it to Tasker (due Fri/Sat). The eastern fixtures and bedstone resolved as *weave + runework-anchor + stored-essence chip*: the **weave** (light, heat) reads plain and is replicable; the **runework** that anchors a weave into dead metal so it runs for a coreless buyer reads **blank** (the rune gate — no vocabulary until the rune road). **The merged-Inspect attempt (a real next-step earned).** Bill tried to draft a *better* reading-Skill by laying his Forceread, Pearce's licensed Inspect, and the guild assayer's Inspect side by side in the drafting space and finding the pattern under the three. He found the insight (all share intake/fold/sample/return; his reaches deep and returns messy, the formal ones reach shallow and return clean — *the better weave reaches like his and returns like theirs*) — but the draft **wouldn't close**: the formal *return* mechanism rendered as a partial void (read-from-outside twice, never built one, never grasped *how* it sorts cleanly). The knowledge gate doing its job. **Identified next step: a deeper study of a formal Inspect's return — i.e. Pearce**, his partner as of ten tomorrow. **The essence-into-the-draft attempt (a clean ruling).** Bill tried to power the trance off the cores (pull essence, cycle it, push it into the drafting space) instead of his own mana. **It doesn't take** — the System spends *mana*, not raw essence; raw essence must be *converted* first, and active conversion stays latent and unlearned (Bill felt its absence again, from the other side). The trance ran on his own pool, the ordinary light draw.

**Warmstone + Cleardraw — SEEN, STORED, REPLICATED.** Bill finally read both Lv.1 utility orbs at rest. **Warmstone** (radiant heat) and **Cleardraw** (identified for the first time: **air/draft-clearing** — not sight) — both simple-boned Lv.1 utility weaves, fully comprehensible, **replicated clean by comprehension** (no void, unlike the formal Inspect).
