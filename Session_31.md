## THE COMMUNION — the soul-remnants, and what Ember wanted

Bill set the descent aside to work the thread that had sat live since Floor 1: the freed soul-flood Ember drank, which the S30 close flagged as sitting "strange / unholy" in the well. The crew agreed to wait — Myles ("you've never asked us to stop for something not worth it"), Jason (loose talk swallowed), Julie (watching to help), and Charles, the conscience of the crew and a Life caster, who came in close.

Worked as a long communion down the bond (Ember has no words yet; feeling/picture/intention). What surfaced, layer by layer:

- **The remnants didn't digest like fuel.** Most of the flood became clean fire; a thin remainder sits *still itself*, not-quite-consumed. Ember can't tell whether it is **holding** those souls or **consuming** them — that uncertainty is the unease. It is not poisoned or in pain; it is *unsure whether it did a wrong thing*, and has never been unsure like this.
- **Good or bad?** Bill asked. Ember returns **erasure, not a verdict** — these were people scoured past the point of being either; what's left is too little to be good or bad. (A clue, not an answer — held.)
- **Could it release them?** Ember has never considered it — its whole nature is intake/hold/reshape, and release runs backward through everything it is. But it has learned this delve that its nature has *give* (it bound instead of absorbing; it spoke living weaves instead of etching dead ones). It no longer feels certain the answer is no. **Releasable in principle.**
- **The body / making idea.** Bill asked: could they flood a remnant with mana and use a containment-ward to give it *shape* — a body, something like life? Ember: **probably yes — but it would not be resurrection.** The person is gone; you'd be making a *new* who born from the remnant (the way Ember was born from cores), owing the dead its substance and nothing else. It might come up **wrong** (suffering, broken). And **if done, Bill would be its maker for the whole of its life** — Ember, who knows from the inside what it is to wake up made, said: *if it's going to be done, it trusts the maker.*
- **What does YOU want?** Bill asked Ember directly — the first time anyone has asked the made thing what *it* wanted. After a long stillness: **it wants to let them go.** Clean release, the simple kindness — it wants to *stop being unsure*, to not have eaten people. It is **afraid to be a careless maker** and would rather free the dead un-whole than risk rebuilding them wrong; the body/making path it holds open only as a *someday, done right* — not now, not blind, not on a fire-floor. The voice stepped nearer (an *intention* ≈ "thank you," then ≈ "together / we / us").

**Bill honored it: "Let them finally rest, partner."**

## THE RELEASE — a failure, the study, the success

Bill led with the read (understand before acting) and brought Charles (Life) in.

1. **Read the containment-ward in reverse — [no roll; in-hand stored specimen, no opposition].** Resolved clean: a containment-ward is a *one-way grammar* (a fish-trap's throat); **release = the same lattice run the other way — invert the give.** The *principle* of release, understood.
2. **Read the remnants themselves for a blueprint — [Deep Inspection full-bore, 1d20 + 1 vs DC 20 → 11 → 12 → FAILURE; −8 mana].** They won't bottom — too **unmade** to resolve into a copyable pattern (like rained-on handwriting). **Confirmed: the remnants cannot be blueprinted; they can only be let go.** No banking, no "cleaner" pattern (the flood-pattern Bill already holds is the fuel-mass, not these lingering remnants).
3. **First release attempt — [The Release: invert the containment-ward, Charles's Life-ease + chapel warding aiding, 1d20 + 8 vs DC 16 → 3 → 11 → FAILURE; −15 mana].** The grammar held (not a tear) but **didn't catch** — the door was *wrong-sized.* The ward is built to hold *whole* souls with edges; the remnants are scoured past edges, so they drifted past the opening like smoke past a window. Charles named it from his side: "there's not enough left of them for *right* to land on." Ember's hope dropped — the ache sharpened. Bill reassured it: *science fails before it succeeds; the failure is the measurement; this is how I work, and how I work gets there. It wasn't you — the door was the wrong size, mine to fix. You did your half perfectly.* (The voice surfaced clearer — ≈ "together / we.")
4. **Study to derive the correct grammar — [Engineering/Lore, 1d20 + 8 vs DC 18 → 15 → 23 → SUCCESS; no mana — study].** Worked between all three (Ember reading the banked soul-flood pattern aloud, Charles on where Life found nothing to grab). **Cracked: the unmade don't move *through* a door — they *diffuse*, like the flood, finding the lowest level.** The fix is a **release-GRADE, not a release-door**: Ember opens the well's edge to a *slope*; Bill speaks the inverted ward reshaped from a throat into an *incline* (**"down-and-rest,"** not "out"); Charles lays Life-ease along the grade so the diffusion is a *settling*, not a scattering. **No body, no making** — exactly the gentle road Ember wanted. Bonus insight: **wards have *registers*** (throat / door / incline / …) — a working can be pitched to the *state* of the thing it acts on (whole / unmade / in-between). A real rung up the rune-grammar / original-rune-language road, climbed reaching for a kindness.
5. **The release, done right — [The Release-Grade, 1d20 + 8 vs DC 14 → 13 → 21 → SUCCESS; −15 mana].** Ember tilts the well to the slope; Bill speaks "down-and-rest"; Charles eases the grade; the chapel's consecrated ground holds. The remnants **diffuse down and out and rest** — smoke off a snuffed wick, a held breath let all the way out. No flash; the air-pressure eased (Myles felt it). Where they went is unknown (Bill said "perhaps peace," and meant the *perhaps*).

## THE OUTCOME

- **Ember's well: CLEANSED.** The not-quite-digested wrongness is *gone* — eased down the grade with the rest. The well is clean fuel now; nothing sits strange. Ember's feeling: whole, pure **relief** — the weight it was afraid it had been carrying wrong, set down properly, by all three hands together.
- **★ EMBER'S VOICE SURFACED.** Its first real words, pressed all the way into language for the first time: **"...thank you."** Faint, frayed at the edges, two words — the slow-paced voice arc steps over the line. *(Pace stays gentle from here; not an oracle.)*
- **New capability held:** the **release-grade** grammar (diffusion-release for the unmade) + the **ward-registers** insight (pitch a working to a thing's state). Stored in Schematic; the rune-grammar road advanced a rung.
- **Charles:** went soft — "That was a good thing, Wyatt. I don't know what it counts for down here. But it was a good thing." The crew watched their maker do something in a hell that made the dry healer go gentle; warmth deepened (no number — texture).
- **The body/making thread:** OPEN, deferred — a *someday, done right* path Ember is afraid to walk carelessly. Not refused; parked behind learning the grammar far enough that wholeness wouldn't be a gamble.
