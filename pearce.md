# PEARCE — assayer, partner

*Recurring NPC. Warmth + ceiling live in `bill_weaver_sheet.json` → `relationships` ("Pearce"). This file holds the qualitative relationship, voice, and key exchanges — no number. Knows Bill as **Wyatt**.*

## Identity & role
A licensed assayer with a shut office down a side lane off the Main Stretch. Older; was running down before Bill came — a good man at a fine craft with no one left to be good for. The partnership (Quest Instruments) brought his office, his license, and his name back into use. The licensed reader Bill needs as a front for strange goods; the one expert reader in town who can stand near Bill's work.

**The assay economics (why Pearce matters):** Pearce holds the territory's **high-tier / dungeon-licensed** read — the rare goods (growth items, guardian cores, made-stock, anything Unique or binding-class) go to him, and there is no higher license west of Saint Orison. The **guild-counter licensed assayer** (the plain severe woman filed Day 25) handles only **lower-tier, routine** reads for the hall. So Pearce keeps the high-tier monopoly that makes him both Bill's necessary front and a restored expert.

## What he knows about Bill
The most-trusted outsider on the cover. He knows Bill's *read is unique* — deep and messy where a licensed read is wide and clean; that Bill can see weave-internals, copy them, make them his own even off un-seated orbs; that the evolved read out-resolves any western license and reads across a room. He does **not** know the System's true nature (Drafting from nothing, the maker-core, the Severn-Falls origin) — and has accepted *not knowing the rest* as the job. The cover for the strange goods is an ordinary one: a Special-delve haul. He connected the loop himself — *"I read it. Somebody makes it."* — but treats it as Bill's business, not his to open.

## Relationship texture
Loyalty seated deep, on trust rather than money. He chose *equity, not wages* — being **in** the work rather than paid for it — because being hired one slip at a time would spend his name down to nothing. He is lonely in the specific way of a man good at a thing who hasn't had anyone to be competent at in years, and handing him his work back is worth more to him than equity, which he mostly doesn't know about himself yet. He waived his day's fee as his stake. Bill values him at his craft, trusts him with the quiet before the facts, and has shown him a genuine wonder (the cross-room read). The relationship can deepen further but he is a businessman with his own dignity — not a man to be presumed upon.

## Voice & manner
Warm, awake, frank. Thinks in *works* — "everything's got an inside to you." Says the load-bearing thing plainly and names his own conditions without dressing them. Writes as he reasons (notes a question on the slip and underlines it). Glad of small dignities offered as between two working men, not as charity. Sample register: *"you don't think like a sharp tradesman, you think in works"*; *"I don't need to know your business; I can sit with not knowing, that's the job"*; *"I'd rather be IN it than paid for it."*

## Key exchanges
- **Day 24 — the partnership shape.** Read the frame-metal to the bottom (*made stock* — essence worked into the forging, not an enchantment on it) and tied it to the great core unprompted. Over a bought hot plate at Halloran's, asked the question that had sat in his face all morning; Bill answered with *trust in proportion* (the quiet, not the facts). **The word "partner" came out of Pearce's mouth, not Bill's.** Bill kept it and handed it back as already-true.
- **Day 26 — sealed in spirit.** Gave Bill the clean *resolve* that closed Deep Inspection (Bill watched him close at the counter; a second resolve-instance off the guild assayer separated function from person). Stilled by the cross-room read — an assayer's whole limit (needing the goods in hand) undone. Sold Bill the earth scrap; tried to show active conversion and does it *poorly* — the fumble taught Bill the seam. Told "best life decision by a country mile."
