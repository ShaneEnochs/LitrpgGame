### Floor 1 — the Cinder Flats
- A bruised-red hellscape; the great core in his pack **woke and leaned** in the essence-thick fire/earth air for the first time. Fight one: a **6-ashhound pack**. Bill split nothing yet — dropped both his **flankers** off the line before they reached Charles, put a clean assist into the hound on Jason's back, and on the last (locked on Myles, Jason already swinging) ran a deliberate **directed read-pulse** experiment: confirmed a **dungeon beast FEELS a directed probe** — it broke rhythm and turned to stare at him, pulling its own attention off the wall. A **distraction tool**, found by doing.
- Floor boss: **the ASH-MAW HOUND** (+2 summoned adds). Bill called the field himself ("Julie, I've got left") — carved the threat, dropped both flankers, then on Myles's call put the **boss-stagger throat-shot** in (2-sec aim into the exposed throat-seam mid-lunge), and the crew finished it in the window he bought. Myles: "You're not a gun. You're a *marksman*." Bill shared the credit to the wall ("if you hadn't immobilized it I'd never have hit that spot").
- **THE TRUTH TOLD.** After the boss, Bill laid his ability out for the crew — not just the silent gun and the read-pulse, but that Deep Inspection lets him **understand, COPY, change, and COMBINE skills** (the hound-pulse = his Foundation intrinsic braided with the assayer's licensed read), and that he can **copy skill orbs without consuming them** (read + copy, orb still sellable) and **read any core/orb/material to grade it**. Offered to try copying Charles's heal (Charles: yes, in a safe room — affinity may wall it) and Julie's magic (with permission). His reason, the one that landed on Myles: *"You're the leader; you can't make the right tactical call without knowing every tool you have. Better you know my full range now than later when it could've helped."* **Myles accepted it as the only right reason** and folded Bill into the crew's deep trust ("what happens down here stays down here; you bought in with the truth"), one rule: **capabilities told on the RIDE, not sprung mid-fight.** The crew now knows Bill is genuinely rare; loyal-discreet, indifferent to his cover. *(Exposure: a deep relational trust spend — four named Platinum delvers now know Bill copies abilities. Tracked.)*

### Floor 2 — the Slagworks
