## HOLLOW REACH (the central mystery — current state of knowledge)

The northwest site now has a name: **Hollow Reach** — an old sealed dungeon mouth, recently cracked back open, northwest of New Abilene.

**The kill curve (via Pickering through Esther; rhymes with Hollis + Pem + platform mutter):**
- **1st crew:** returned WHOLE but won't talk — paid off or scared off (per Pickering).
- **2nd crew:** returned TWO SHORT. **Amos Vane was one of the two.** (Hollis personally hauled this crew out ~10 days ago and returned two short.)
- **3rd crew:** went down **5 days ago. NONE returned.** Still down there or dead.
- Result: Doss can't fill the slot; the guild stonewalls the press; locals steer clear; a platform voice muttered "Doss still ain't filled it, three now shy of it" (ambiguous — three gone, or three refusals?).
- **Supply manifest (Hollis, firsthand): timber, lime, lamp oil** = shoring, mortar/corpse-lime, light. Going deep; possibly sealing or burying something.

**Bill's hypothesis framework (Intellect 16 work — in his notebook; honestly held, none confirmed):**
Key anomaly: the danger ESCALATES (whole → two short → all gone). A rising curve has a cause: growing/multiplying/waking/spreading/being fed.
1. **Bad information** — silenced 1st crew corrupted the guild's own intel; Doss may not know. Hole: a fair officer losing crews should close the site — unless overruled from above.
2. **Site kept active on purpose; deaths acceptable** — something down there worth more than delvers. The sealing manifest cuts against pure looting; fork: extract-and-contain (failing), or "sealing" is cover.
3. **(Ugliest)** Low-rank delvers are **expendable instruments spent for data** on a new lethal phenomenon. Cheap bodies, records kept, mouths shut. The escalation IS the measurement.
4. **Feedback loop** — delving itself winds the site toward a BREAK; lime+timber a doomed re-seal. *Rhymes with how Rebecca Weaver died.*
**Bill's named bias (player-acknowledged):** he WANTS this contract — it's the phenomenon he came west to study — and knows a man who badly wants a door talks himself past warnings.
**Test plan:** #1 predicts Doss is straight with him; #2/#3 predict contract terms that are off (overpay for a provisional, question-discouraging structure, "report" about the site's state rather than recovery); #4 predicts physical signs at the site. Best discriminating sources: the silent 1st crew, **Pickering's files/names (tomorrow noon)**, and Doss's pitch measured against depot truth (tomorrow late).
**Prepared Pickering questions:** Who was the 1st crew and why won't they talk? What was the supply manifest really for? (Plus his collected names of the missing.)

## THE PICKERING PATRONAGE PLAY (drafted, unsigned, contingent)

**The deal Bill wants:** Pickering funds Bill's full guild entry (~$19.50 wall) **plus an ongoing training stake** ("in, and trained enough to live" — Bill's stated floor), by any lawful means (payment/bond/sponsorship/etc.). In return Bill becomes a **discreet anonymous source inside the guild**, providing truthful accounts of what he lawfully witnesses, **only to the extent lawful and permitted**, only after safe return, with no obligation until Pickering has performed.
**Bill's hard line (stated):** won't break the law; won't disclose guild confidences/oaths/restricted matters.

**The draft contract EXISTS as a physical document** — written by Bill in Calloway's office, behind privilege, unsigned and unnamed. (The player wrote the actual full text; it's titled *"Private Agreement of Lawful Patronage and Discreet Correspondence."* Its key architecture, all Calloway-endorsed: patron's performance gates all duty; staged support = each stage secured before performance owed; "lawful and permitted" limits on all disclosure; explicit no-stealing/no-oath-breaking/no-confidence-breaching carve-outs; truthful-account-only, no guaranteed results; sweeping anonymity terms including agents/printers/runners; information received as private correspondence; **split-instrument structure** with identifying terms/payment records/enforcement held by a mutually trusted third party so no document in Pickering's possession identifies Bill; severability of any unlawful term.)

**Calloway's three REQUIRED fixes before signing (the $2 job, not yet done):**
1. **Enforcement teeth:** as drafted, enforcing it means suing publicly = self-destruction. Add **liquidated damages held in escrow**, forfeited to the third-party holder on breach, no public trial needed.
2. **Pin the money:** "secure access by any lawful means" lets a clever patron satisfy it with a worthless introduction. Standard must be **the full fee actually paid/bonded such that Bill is ADMITTED.**
3. **Bar identification-by-inference:** name-anonymity is worthless if published facts are so specific they finger the only man who could have known them. Add a clause against publishing detail that identifies the source by inference.
**Open design question: WHO is the trusted third party?** Must be someone neither party can lean on. (Calloway himself is a candidate the table hasn't discussed.)
**Sequence (Calloway's counsel, adopted):** confirm at noon that Pickering can actually FUND it → only then pay Calloway $2 to finalize → names go only on the third-party copy.
**Known risks (named in play, player-acknowledged):** Pickering may want it and not afford it; the deal sells guild-adjacent access while needing guild goodwill — exposure = plausible license revocation ("embarrassing powerful patrons") or worse; the document's existence is itself the danger; the watcher implies someone already minds who takes an interest.
