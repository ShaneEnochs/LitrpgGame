## What happened

A full day on Bill's own sequencing — fire and coffee at home, then Calloway's, the gunsmith, a newspaper, the guild hall, and Dell's yard — that turned, at the hall, into the heaviest decision of the campaign so far. **The crew-4 surfacing spine finally fired**, folded as texture through Doss's writ channel, and Bill answered it by choosing to put his name on paper about Hollow Reach.

**Morning — home.** Woke to the hardest cold yet (the whole lower windowpane furred white, ice skin on the basin). Fed the fire, made coffee, sequenced the day: Calloway first (deposit the receipt slip), then a newspaper, the gunsmith, the hall.

**Calloway's office — the receipt slip, the conflict beat, and the custody decision.** Lodged the Quest receipt slip in the iron box (errand done; the slip is now out of the upstairs window casing for good). Calloway then delivered the **NW-land conflict beat**: a party sought him as local counsel for "land matters northwest"; he declined, conflicted, telling Bill only what ethics allow — no name, no side, no parcel — with the unprompted caution that land money is looking northwest, he's not the only lawyer on the street, and the ones who'd take it are less careful whose business sits next to whose. Bill read it (Intellect) as a watch-direction with no actionable specifics, and as a flag on his single-point-of-failure: everything that could hang him runs through one office. He raised exactly that with Calloway, who counseled **splitting custody, not counsel** — keep him as whole counsel (the whole-board sight is worth more than redundancy; he proved it by arguing himself *out* of the more lucrative second-lawyer option), and get the poison-pile *originals* out of the single office. **Decided:** open a **safe-deposit box at the New Abilene bank under the QUEST TRUST's name** (trust-owned, so it survives anything happening to Calloway), move the danger-is-the-paper-itself originals into it, draft a custody instruction. Calloway is arranging it (his work, not Bill's; box fee ~20–25/yr; his time <150 all-in, exact figure quoted before he starts). **Held open per Bill's choice:** splitting the sealed schedule into two useless halves — to be decided *per-document* once Calloway sends the sorted poison list (word via the usual runner, ~1–2 weeks).

**The newspaper (mercantile counter).** Bought the *Oregon Clarion*. Mostly stale furniture (the same weary-caution NW-rush lead, the Saint Orison backlog, cartridges-firming), but one fresh item, set higher than a week ago would have warranted: **a rush party was turned back Thursday off the Coulter & Reese parcels by armed company men.** The editor did the arithmetic out loud — men don't post rifles on worthless scrub — and pointed his skepticism at the *land* for the first time. Same company, same direction as Calloway's morning warning; banked as a watch, no handle to act on.

**The gunsmith (Main Stretch) — the extractor finished, the speedloader founded.** Bill reported yesterday's *solo* Crane session (news to the gunsmith): the reserved extractor late-pickup confirmed real on a hard, fast, fouled break. He **decided to take the reserved timing cut**, and the two of them did it slow and careful — a hair at a time, reassemble-test-look, deliberately fouling the star to test the true failure condition — until all six rims lift together even fast and dirty. **The extractor fault is now closed for good** (lift, return, and timing all true). Then onto the **speedloader real build**: good spring stock pulled (the real thing, with memory the mule's cheap brass never had); they worked the design forward (replaceable fingers on *positively-indexed* honest seats — two surfaces, a flat for angle and a shoulder for depth, so a swapped finger sits identical; all-six-matched applies). Bill chose to **cut the body and the six seats** this session and leave the finger temper/bend/all-six-matching for a fresh-wrist follow-up morning (Insight+Intellect read: both men good but on the downslope after food; temper work punishes a softened hand; clean seam to stop at). Sent a runner for food mid-build (two plates, eaten at the bench). **Outcome:** the holder now exists as a real object — body cut clean, six seats matched and indexing — foundation laid right; **the banked Engineering firearm-mechanism bump still gates on the *fingers being fabricated and the holder working*, not on this foundation.** Half-commission settled from Bill's own pocket per the rule.

**The guild hall — the spine fires.** Caught the contract board (Aldine call still open; Cinder caravan-escort at rising pre-pass rates; river-road Beast bounty; NW guide/outfitting notices) and an awakened-setpiece detail in the background (a bounty crew waiting to weigh a tarped carcass; one man carrying the heavy load too easily — an orb-strength man, the room reading him without looking). At Doss's desk:
- **The re-measure cover is set** — Bill is covered to walk the Reach ground again under guild survey paper, reading as guild work, not a private man at a sealed mouth.
- **The crew-4 surfacing landed** (writ channel, as texture, never cold, mechanism never confirmed): a status came up the channel — *progress, resupply requested, crew sound* — dated sound the day before it was sent, two days back. Doss read it flat and then named her standing unease: **the clocks on this hole have never sat right** (a report of a crew well on a day that doesn't square with descent/last-word dates). She nearly said the thing aloud — *someone keeps reporting good news on a schedule from that hole* — and stopped herself, a careful officer not saying it to a civilian. She also named the **Quint pressure**: an eastern charter's advance man pressing for site access under the new classification, who'd love a clean recent survey calling the site quiet.
- **The filing-form question:** what Bill found on the re-measure (the creep, the site not at equilibrium after a descent) can go up the channel **verbal-only** (dies with her discretion), **written-unnamed** (real paper, the channel starts hunting the hand), or **written-named** (most protection for the finding, most exposure for the man). She laid the cost straight and did not steer him.

**Bill's decision — the turn.** Bill named the company-buying-land as a coming problem unprompted (it landed on Coulter & Reese, already on Doss's desk) and pushed for **sooner**. Then he chose, eyes open, to **put his name on the paper** — "anonymity isn't worth the lives of others," spending nineteen days of dull-fixer cover and a town's goodwill deliberately. Doss visibly revised him upward ("I had you wrong, a little — most men spend a town's goodwill on themselves"). She tied it to her vise: a **named engineer's filed evidence that a sealed mouth is moving** forces the people above her to act or be on record ignoring it — which tells her something she's wanted to know about who keeps this hole open. **Set: a guild survey tomorrow (Day 21, Sunday, first light), under her paper cut today, on the same Sunday frame as the Day-7 baseline, the finding filed *named* on return.**

**The monster-fighting teacher — Doss says yes, and gives the hour.** Bill asked her to teach him to fight monsters; he asked it *right* (the survival craft, not the greenhorn's heroics), and she agreed — framing what she has (a former working delver's knowledge: reading a monster, knowing when not to fight, knowing your own gut) versus what she doesn't (the close duel — that's Dell). She gave the **first hour on the spot and ran well past it**, missing teaching: the monster categories field-sorted by *what kills it / what it's about to do* (Beasts readable by weight; **Constructs as mechanisms — a man who reads machines reads them best**; oozes/spirits/elementals where the gun fails); lessons-with-bodies-attached; and the leaving — *know your line to the door, spend more attention on it than on the thing in front of you.* The method she handed Bill is one he already owns (read a machine by what it can't do); she aimed it somewhere new. No charge — "owed, for what you're spending tomorrow." Next: come sit again after the survey (a sealed mouth is its own thing to read).

**The guild transport.** Bill asked, since it's sanctioned, to ride on a furnished, guild-paid conveyance rather than his own. Doss: of course — guild work goes out *as* guild work. **Set for the cage yard before first light:** a furnished, provisioned guild conveyance; **a driver and a hand** (two more sets of eyes and guns for the rush road); survey instruments from the cage stores against the paper; warm gear and rations; the whole of it on the guild's account, nothing from Bill's pocket but the morning. Doss named the edge honestly: a guild rig rolling to the Reach at first light is a thing the town sees — the same loud-and-covered trade Bill already chose, now with wheels under it. Stay inside the frame and the frame holds.

**Dell's yard — settling the hands, not the gut.** Last of the light. Bill drilled the gun-knife seam; the snap-shot work showed (gun-hand and knife-hand no longer arguing; cleaner commit), but Dell caught the same thing as ever — the fractional *think* in the seam — and told him straight: the yard's given what the yard gives; the seam is hard not because the moves are hard but because a real opponent *means it*, and the gut under that can't be drilled, only lived once. "Practice settles the hands, not the gut. Don't go finding out about the gut on purpose — the men who go looking are the men I bury." (Honor: this is the same live-resistance gate that stands on Melee — no tick from drilling.)

**Home, and sleep.** Bill went home through the deep cold, settled the small night-before things (warm gear laid out, revolver clean/full/running perfect), and slept ahead of the first-light wagon.
