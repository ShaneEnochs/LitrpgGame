# CULTIVATION — CORES, GATES, AND SPIRITUAL PRESSURE (v1)

*Worldbuilding reference for the Frontier Fantasy Solo RPG. Consistent with `game_bible.md` (the canon and tiebreaker, esp. §5's 1–100 attribute scale, §12 delve growth, and §13) and the Bible's voice rules. Reworked from the earlier body-forging version: cultivation now affects mana only. It does nothing to the body. Physical stats come solely from delve payouts (§12).*

---

## What cultivation is, and isn't

Cultivation is the practice of opening and developing the essence core so it holds and moves more mana. That is all it does. It does not change the body, grant physical strength, or alter how a person looks.

Raw power — strength, speed, toughness, the things that put a person past the human ceiling — comes only from delve payouts (§12), never from cultivation. A heavily cultivated person and an uncultivated one with the same physical stats are equally strong; the cultivated one simply has far more mana and far better command of it.

The two growth tracks are fully separate: **delves raise stats; gate-work raises mana.** A dangerous delver usually has both — stats earned in the holes, mana earned at the gates.

---

## The core

Every awakened person has an essence core in two parts.

- **Inner core** — holds eight **essence gates**. Each gate is a closed barrier; opening one permanently raises mana capacity. Eight is the ceiling. The inner gates are the main answer to *how much* mana a person has.
- **Outer core** — developed by ongoing work rather than discrete breakthroughs. Strengthening it adds still more capacity, sharpens fine control, and lets a person expel mana faster. The inner gates set how big the tank is; the outer core is what makes that mana usable at speed and with precision.

### How a gate opens

A core grows by pulling in essence — from delves, essence-rich ground, and essence stones — and compacting it. The more compact the core, the less the gates resist. In meditation, a cultivator pushes mana against a gate until it breaks. It is not guaranteed: a push can fail, and opening a gate usually takes a fair amount of training with compacted essence behind it. When it works, the gate stays open for good.

### Intellect sets the size of a gate

Intellect is the mana attribute. The capacity a newly opened gate adds is fixed by the cultivator's **Intellect at the moment the gate breaks**, and locks there — break a gate with high Intellect and it adds a lot; break it with low Intellect and it adds little, permanently.

The practical consequence, which no ordinary cultivator can see because there are no screens: the patient play is to raise Intellect first and open gates after, and a gate opened early at low Intellect is a permanently smaller gate. Most cultivators stumble into this by feel; a few work it out and time their breakthroughs.

### Mana regenerates

A spent pool refills over time — faster with rest, food, safety, meditation, or strong-essence surroundings, slower under strain. Capacity (gates plus outer core) sets the ceiling; regeneration sets how fast a person returns to it.

## Spiritual pressure

An open core gives off **spiritual pressure** — a felt sense of a person's power that other awakened cores can read. It is the replacement for any visible mark of cultivation: a strong person looks ordinary but feels strong to anyone awakened nearby. Spiritual pressure is, in effect, the aura every awakened core carries — and an aura ability (below) is that same field shaped to do something.

- **It reads overall power** — the sum of stats and core development — so a read tells you roughly where someone sits on the scale, how dangerous they are, without a number. (Bill's System is the sole exception to "without a number"; governed by `system_engineer_truth_v1.md`.)
- **Awakened-only.** Unawakened people feel nothing, the same way they can't sense essence or read an orb. Pressure is a sense the open core grants.
- **Monsters have it too.** A delver can feel a monster's presence before seeing it, which makes pressure a real tool for sensing danger in the dark. But some monsters rein their pressure in to hunt by ambush, and a suppressed predator gives no warning. Natural suppressors are among the frontier's worst surprises.
- **Suppression is control, not a separate gift.** Reining in your own pressure draws on the same fine control the outer core builds. A developed cultivator can damp their pressure to pass unread; a young or careless one leaks, and a furious or straining one floods the area whether they mean to or not.
- **Range and familiarity.** A good read needs you fairly close. The exception is people you know well — someone you have run many delves beside, you can feel from a fair distance, well enough to know roughly where they are and how they are doing. Acquaintance attunes the sense.
- **Some can see it.** A few abilities and skills let a cultivator see spiritual pressure rather than only feel it — a read by eye instead of by instinct.

### Pressure and auras

An **aura** (§13, `auras_v1.md`) is not a separate field. It is this same spiritual-pressure field shaped to *do* something to the area around the cultivator — steadiness, heat, fear, a slowing heaviness. Passive pressure only tells another awakened how strong you are; an aura ability makes that field act. The assay still measures the core directly; pressure is the in-the-room version a person carries everywhere, and an aura is that pressure put to work.
