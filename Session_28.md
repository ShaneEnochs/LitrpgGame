### Floor-2 rest → the FURNACE-WARDEN
- Pre-Warden, Bill turned Deep Inspection **inward** and read his own innate filter as a machinable object (rough-walled, snags = the ~50%; high spots *visible* to him). Topped to 210 on his own draw (first solo active conversion). Rebuilt the coil-gun clip **wedge → broadhead → bodkins**, resolving to use the gun only twice on the Warden.
- **Hard pre-aggro read:** Furnace-Warden — Construct, ~11 ft, ~2 tons; molten Fire core; immune pain/fear/poison/bleed/fatigue + **fire**; resists physical (iron plate)/heat/concussion; **weak at the exposed core (wide, takes wrecking) + pryable vent-seams.** Vents fire (telegraphed by seam-flare + draft) → core bare ~1 beat after.
- **The fight:** Myles held aggro; the first vent came up **tight** (warped louver) → Julie's lance loaded it, **Bill's wedge pried it wide**, **broadhead wrecked the bare core** → Jason finished it. **Core DESTROYED — the Furnace's Due, paid.** No party damage.
- **★ The owed failure-beat LANDED — by Bill's choice.** The wedge-pry + the broadhead were two hard mana-pulls back to back; Bill *felt* the rail shudder on the second and **chose to fire anyway rather than send Jason's hands into the open furnace** (*"I don't want anyone to get hurt, even if there's a chance I wreck my gun"*). The shot was perfect — the **rail-throat went COMPROMISED** (hairline split, trueness gone): soft-pull-only, fragile, Myles's-call-only for the rest of the delve; the revolver became Bill's working gun. Competence-priced, chosen, fail-forward — never a miss. **Counter reset to 0.**
- **CREW NAMED — "FORTUNE'S FOLLY."** Off Bill's line to Charles ("fortune never accounted for this crew"). Myles named the crew for the first time in three Platinum runs and welcomed Bill on *for real* — *"You're not the fifth man. You're one of the five."*
- **Drops:** the **Infernal forge-stock** (the Due reward — frame-metal, the cure-metal for the gun); Fire-Platinum-dense **core fragments** (the Due wrecked the core); a dense clean **Earth core**; a **fire-ward elixir** (Charles tossed it to Bill).
- **Corpse-reads:** completed the **cerberus construct-blueprint** (full body/plate/vent/seat + the fire-channeling grammar, now whole); salvaged the **vent-louver mechanism** + a **seated-grammar fragment** (a fire-weave anchored in dead metal — the hinge between the construct-road and the rune-road; its *anchoring* read shape-only).

- **Skill-creation is NOT bench-gated** (established): weave-comprehension and authoring happen in Bill's head via the read + logged structures. Bill braided **Julie's density-knot (compression) + the Warden fire-grammar (payload) + active conversion (the per-second feed)** into a new family, then **split it deliberately into two skills:**
  - **HELL GRENADE Lv.1** — placed/thrown charge, detonate-on-release; **fuel-agnostic casing** (ambient, or seated in a stone/core = a bigger grenade; junk-core economy confirmed). Tested core-seated → real crater.
  - **SPIRIT GUN Lv.1** — launched orb (finger-gun pose), thrown to a point in eye-line, brief flight-hold, detonates on arrival; crude aim at Lv.1. Tested on the corpse. **The flight-hold = the first brick of the tied-off-knot road.**

The crew-distributable / tied-off grenade is crackable via **essence-signature stepped-commands** (write a conditional into the essence) or **self-taught anchoring** — discovered through play, never deferred to an offscreen calendar.

Soft wide read (geysers = readable timing; imps = herd-into-fire threat; lurkers = route-around Oozes), then a **hard probe on the watcher: the IMP-PRINCE** (Infernal commander; teleports; buffs/drives the swarm; squishy — mobility *is* its defense). - **The fight (Bill = bait-and-gun):** crew held the lip chokepoint. Bill drank the fire-ward, **read the teleport tell** (the core *flares* a half-beat before each blink; it only blinks to lull-vents that open an angle on Bill), called it to Julie → **she denied lanes to funnel it** while Bill **pre-aimed with Steadyhand** → the Prince blinked into the one lane left → **clean revolver head-shot, instant kill. Swarm broke.** No party hurt.
- **The crossing:** Bill held a **wide sustained Deep-Inspection bubble** and *conducted* all five + the corpse across the geyser field — zero burns, zero grabs.
- **At the Floor-4 arch:** looted the Prince (intact Fire-Platinum boss core + a fire core + an essence stone); **logged the Prince's TELEPORT** — a short-range **spatial-fold grammar**, fire-powered (captured whole; a brand-new spatial domain = the hardest crack Bill's logged). **First-ever read of a threshold arch:** a conditional release-gate (*holds shut until cleared, then passes*); **no runes — the gate-logic is seated in the ESSENCE-FIELD**, confirming the **essence-signature road is REAL** and distinct from the licensed rune-road.

### Floor 4 — THE OBSIDIAN STAIR (the "barely walk out" floor — cleared clean)
- Four threats deep-read from the lip: the **essence-hardened obsidian stair** (sharp, sound, conductive; narrow & exposed over a **lethal fire-lake**; **formation-breaking — a fall = death**), **magma-serpents** (breach-to-strike; lake domes a beat before), **glass-wraiths** (physical-IMMUNE; vulnerable to essence/force + Life/holy; **drain heat and essence** — a specific risk to Bill's open draw), and the boss **CINDER-DRAKE** (flier, strafes the no-cover stair).
- **Read-geometry inventions:** Bill ran the read as a **downward cone on the lake**, then invented a **weighted spherical needle-array** (deriving the safe needle-spacing with Intellect — provably catches every dome; dense toward the lethal threat, loose elsewhere), holding it under stakes to **complete the drake read**, then downshifting on a pre-set rule. Later used a **1-second pulse** to catch inbound wraiths.
- **Serpents** died in the breach-window (revolver); Bill used Intellect to **calculate the serpent-reach envelope** and knew the height above which the lake stopped being a threat. **Wraiths** died to the proven Julie-slow + **Charles-Life-burn** plan (Charles's domain as *offense* — his floor-beat). *(A Charles "fifteen years" line is RETCONNED — see errata; delving is young on this frontier and Charles is the crew's newest. He self-corrected it in-fiction.)*
- **The drake:** Bill **provoked the launch** (advanced to force its commit), caught the **wing-spread window**, put **Spirit Gun + Julie's bolt into the same (left) wing** → the wounds connected, the wing failed, **it crash-landed grounded**; then a **maxed chip-seated Hell Grenade** to the crippled flank → Myles + Jason finished it on the glass. *Both new skills proven under combat load.*
- **★ ACTIVE DRAIN — REALIZED.** Two wraiths inbound during the finish. Bill drove a **full-power Deep-Inspection probe** at the nearer wraith to read its drain-mechanism — and it **reached back and drained him through the channel.** Then it snapped into place: *the directed-drain isn't a new skill — it's active conversion + the read, tuned OUTWARD; the only missing piece was the knowledge that the draw can pull from essence-creatures, which the wraith just demonstrated.* Bill **opened his own draw down the channel and reversed the flow** — sighted draw beating blind draw — and **drained the wraith to nothing**, pool climbing as the Spirit came apart (*"I'm giving it the Fortune's Folly treatment"*). Julie + Charles took the second. **Earned clean off ~6 beats of in-play setup.**
- **Cinder-Drake DOWN. Both wraiths dead. NO party casualties — the floor that turned this crew back twice, walked out of clean.**

### The safe room (Day 28) — rest, forge, the stakes named
- Looted the drake: **Fire-Platinum FLOOR-BOSS core** (~800–1,400, forge-kin), a **rare mana-restorative elixir** (Charles gave it to Bill for the Colossus), a **fire-aspected smithing nodule**, and a few **drake-scales**.

The road up is mapped: sighted tuning beats blind, eventually past Julie toward the master ceiling.
- **The stakes named:** Bill asked how far the crew had gotten before — *they never cleared the Emberwaste*; turned back at the Stair twice; **today is the furthest any of them has ever been.** The Colossus is **unknown ground** for all five — Bill's read tomorrow is the first real intelligence anyone in this crew has had on the dungeon boss.

A marksman's problem; the reason the coil gun matters.
- **★ THE HELLFIRE FORGE — LIT.** Bill's idea: the dungeon *is* a forge — free hellfire, essence-thick air. He designed the apparatus (slag firebox tapped into a fire-seam, Julie warding containment, a slag anvil), the crew built to the drawing, and they **tapped the seam and lit it** — concentrated contained hellfire, hot enough for essence-smithing. **The great core surged — the strongest waking-response yet** (the maker making a forge stirred the maker-core). Bill **worked the forge-stock into a proper brace of the rail-throat**: not a full re-forge, but **the coil gun is now SERVICEABLE for the Colossus** — several reliable hard pulls, precise shooting restored for the fight (full fix still owed on return). Myles enforced the sleep the moment the gun tested true.
