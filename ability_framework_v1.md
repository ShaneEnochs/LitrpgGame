# ABILITY FRAMEWORK — the four kinds of Skill (v1)

*Worldbuilding reference for the Frontier Fantasy Solo RPG. Consistent with `game_bible.md` (the canon and tiebreaker, including its voice rules). This file organizes the supernatural-ability layer — a delver's **Skills** — sitting above `ability_orbs_v1.md` (the orb catalog), `auras_v1.md` (the passive-field catalog), and `magic_domains_v1.md` (spell access), and explains how a delver's Skills fit together into a build. It elaborates `game_bible.md` §13 and §6 and never overrides them. Voice follows §2: plain and literal.*

*A note on the two words. The awakened, supernatural layer is **Skills** (capital S): the four categories below. The mundane 1–10 competencies anyone can train are **mundane skills** (§6). "Abilities" survives as an everyday prose word for the Skills layer. Keep the two layers distinct on the sheet.*

---

## 1. The four kinds

Every awakened delver's Skills sort into four categories:

- **Active** — the Skills you trigger and pay mana for. Cast spells (from tomes, domain-gated) and the active or toggled ability orbs.
- **Passive** — Skills that run on their own once held. Auras, and a small number of rare always-on boons.
- **Movement** — Skills whose main purpose is getting around: dashes, blinks, climbs, controlled falls, surefooted travel. One held ready at a time, separate from the Active kit.
- **Intrinsic** — trained, scaling disciplines that strengthen their own lane, amplify your Skills in that lane, and evolve at thresholds.

Every Skill, in whatever category, climbs the same ability-level track (1–5, §6) and can evolve. They enter a build the same handful of ways: the **choosing** (§6), ability orbs, tomes (Active spells, domain-gated), auras, and intrinsics grown from teachers, tomes, or practice — and, for Bill alone, drafting.

---

## 2. Active

**What they are.** The do-something Skills: a spell cast from a tome, an orb triggered or held active (Burst, Iron Skin, Darksight, and their kin in `ability_orbs_v1.md`). Each costs mana per use or per moment sustained.

**The seated kit, and how Intellect sizes it.** A core and mind hold only so many Active Skills at full readiness. A delver runs a focused kit, not every orb and tome they own — overstuffing dulls everything, so a person keeps sharp what they actually use and lets the rest sit idle. **The size of that kit scales with Intellect:** a sharper mind holds more tools ready. As a working figure the seat count is about one plus a tenth of Intellect — two seats at the low end, three around the unawakened ceiling, and climbing slowly from there as Intellect grows — but the number stays **felt, not published**: folk delvers know it as "you can only keep so much sharp at once," and most never count it. (A delver whose window quantifies their own figures reads the seat count as a number where others feel a ceiling, and watches it tick up as their mind sharpens — see §11.)

**A consequence worth seeing.** Because Intellect sizes the kit and most delvers' points settle by struggle (§12), a brute who fought his way to high Might and low Intellect holds few Skills ready however many orbs he owns, while a thinker runs a broad kit and breaks easier in a fight. The build punishes a one-attribute dump twice over — once in the thin kit, once at the market, where Intellect-keyed orbs sit out of reach (§8). This is the system policing itself; no hard cap is needed.

**Re-seating is slow.** Swapping what a delver keeps ready is possible but deliberate — it takes real time and focused effort to drop one pattern and bring another up to readiness. A kit is a commitment across a delving career, not a loadout swapped before each job. (What happens to a Skill when it leaves the kit is in §8.)

**Levels.** An Active climbs the ability-level track (1–5, §6) through hard use, sharpening and sometimes evolving. A spell deepens through casting; an orb's Skill through use.

---

## 3. Passive

**What they are.** Skills that run on their own once held. Two ends:

- **Auras** are the main passive and the costly end — sustained, draining mana continuously while projected (`auras_v1.md`). One aura at a time by default, per that catalog.
- **Always-on boons** are the free, bedrock end — rare constant effects, from deep cultivation or a particular piece of loot, that need no upkeep: a standing toughness of the flesh, a constant low sense, a body that no longer feels a certain harm. These are scarce; nobody carries a stack of them.

**Few and defining.** A passive shapes a whole build — what a delver always has changes how they fight and travel more than any single triggered trick. There is **no fixed slot count**; the scarcity is the loot and the core's own limits, not a published cap. The one-aura rule stands; always-on boons are rarer still.

---

## 4. Movement

**What they are.** Skills whose main purpose is locomotion or repositioning — a dash, a blink through matter, a climb up sheer rock, a controlled fall, surefooted travel over bad ground. In the orb catalog these are Surefoot, Slow Fall, Light Step, Adhesion, Phase Step, and Burst; more come from tomes and the choosing.

**One ready at a time, separate from the Active kit.** A delver keeps **one Movement Skill seated**, on its own, parallel to the one-aura rule and apart from the Intellect-sized Active kit. Holding Surefoot means not holding Phase Step. This is what makes a good Movement Skill prized loot: every delver wants the one that fits how they delve, and can carry only one.

**Levels and evolutions.** A Movement Skill climbs the 1–5 track and can evolve like any other — a Slow Fall deepening into a longer or steerable descent, a Phase Step into a longer reach. An evolution can ease the pressure of the one-seated rule in flavor (a faster re-seat, a wider use) but **never grants a second Movement seat**; the single seat is the rule the category is built on.

---

## 5. Intrinsics

**What an intrinsic is.** A **discipline** a delver cultivates that does three things at once:

1. **Strengthens its own lane** on its own — a growing benefit that needs no activation. A blade discipline makes every cut land harder; a warding discipline makes every seal hold longer.
2. **Amplifies the delver's Skills in that lane** — the same discipline that sharpens a delver's strikes also makes a blade-keyed orb or an Arcane spell hit harder. The intrinsic is as much a part of a Skill's power as the attribute behind it.
3. **Evolves at thresholds** — at certain levels it takes a step-change, not just a small gain.

**Why it matters.** This is the difference between high stats and a real build. Might swings a hammer; a blunt-weapon discipline swings it better, and makes the delver's concussion spell and their hammer-orb hit harder too. A delver with a bag of unconnected orbs and no intrinsic behind them is weak however many orbs it is; a delver with two deep disciplines that everything in their kit feeds through is dangerous.

**The time bottleneck.** Cultivating a discipline is slow — slower than gaining a point. So a delver's near-term goal before stepping up to harder delves is usually an intrinsic threshold, not more points. This is the main reason **builds concentrate and delvers diverge** (§12, §21): no one has time to deepen everything, so they pick.

**Where they come from.** Open-ended, grown in play, not a master list (house style, per §6 and §21). Some are seeded by a found tome, orb, or a teacher who has the discipline; some grow out of a mundane skill once an awakened core feeds it past the human ceiling; and a delver who clears a **Foundation delve** receives one **starter intrinsic** outright, at level 1, keyed to their class, affinity, and the person they are (§12). They are **awakened-only** — an unawakened person has mundane skills (1–10), not intrinsics. Name an intrinsic when play produces one; do not pre-build a catalog of them.

**Levels and evolutions.** An intrinsic climbs the ability-level track (1–5, §6) — the same scale orbs, auras, and every other Skill use, so the campaign keeps one evolution idea, not several. Each level deepens the lane and the amplification. At certain levels the intrinsic **evolves**: a step-change that may sharpen the whole build, hand the delver a new Active that does **not** count against the seated-kit limit, or open a capability that was not there before. Evolutions are what a delver chases when they commit to a discipline.

*Thematic note, not a table rule: a delver's milestones in a discipline tend to land near the same career stages as the attribute bands at 20 / 40 / 70 / 100 in §5. The resonance is real; the world has not noticed it, and a mind that charts it is doing the kind of work the campaign rewards.*

**Intrinsics do not orb out.** Unlike a found or granted Skill, a grown discipline cannot be crystallized and sold (§8). It is part of the delver, not an object they hold.

## 6. The choosing

**What it is.** At awakening, and once at each growth-paying completion thereafter (§12), the window offers the delver **three Skills to choose among**. The delver takes one; the other two are closed to them for good.

**How offers are generated (DM rules).** Tailor each offer to the delver in front of you:

- Read demonstrated play — the recent axis of struggle, the existing build, and a real gap. At least one option should deepen an existing line, and at least one should open something new. Options may come from any of the four categories.
- **The first offer, at awakening, has no delving to read,** so it reads who the person is coming in — their mundane skills, attributes, class, and how the core opened. (Bill's first offer reads the methodical engineer-alchemist, not combat he has never done.)
- Never offer knowledge the world hasn't earned — no spoiler-Skills, nothing that resolves a truth-file mystery.
- **Log every offer and lock** in the director's-notes ledger, so a declined option stays declined forever.

**How offered Skills behave.** They are real Skills like any other: they seat under the category limits, level 1–5, and can evolve. An offered Active joins the Intellect-sized kit; an offered Movement Skill takes the one Movement seat; an offered intrinsic grows like a grown one.

**State of knowledge.** The choosing is public folk knowledge — "what did your window offer you?" is saloon talk. What no one has worked out is that the offers track the person so closely — that the menu is, in effect, reading the man. (That implication is DM material; see `delve_progression_truth_v1.md`.)

**The locked options are a fact of the world.** Across a full career a delver declines roughly two Skills for every one taken — most of what their window could have offered, it never will. The lock is permanent and one-way: the window will not re-offer a declined Skill, though the same Skill may still reach the delver by another road — an orb on the market, a tome — since those roads are not the window choosing for them.

## 7. Inspect

**What it is.** An awakened Skill family for reading what a thing is. Separate trained lines:

- **item-Inspect** — orbs, gear, materials, tomes, relics;
- **monster-Inspect** — type, traits, danger, the weak point;
- **delver-Inspect** — an open core, rough capability, condition.

A delver trains the line their work runs on; an assayer and a monster-hunter's spotter develop different ones.

**Higher reveals more.** A low item-Inspect reads category and rough grade; a high one reads exact level, every seating requirement, affinity keying, and any flaw. The same curve runs through the other lines. It needs an open core, costs a little mana, and climbs the 1–5 track like every Skill.

**The assayer's trade.** This is the licensed assayer's road to a real cultivation profession. An assayer who trains item-Inspect can tell a delver exactly what they hauled out and what it is worth — which is why a buyer pays for a trusted assayer's read before paying near-tome money for a high orb (§8), and why a respected assayer's word carries value of its own. It slots straight into the loot economy and the assay regime (§14, §17).

**Inspect on people stays surface.** Condition, fatigue, rough capability, an open core, at high skill an affinity — never thoughts, intentions, memories, or another person's private window figures. The window stays private; social reading stays a mundane skill, not a scan. Monster- and item-Inspect can go deep; delver-Inspect stops at what a careful look and spiritual pressure would show, sharpened.

**Gate examination is separate.** Examining a dungeon arch returns a window of the site's requirements to anyone awakened (§12) — that is the arch presenting itself, not an Inspect read, and it needs no Skill. A high item-Inspect may eventually sharpen what a delver makes of an arch's returns, but the base examination is free to every awakened person.

**Bill's parallel.** Bill's System Examine does privately and more deeply what Inspect does publicly and by training. The two overlap in function and differ in source; that he can hold his read against a master assayer's is itself a thread (DM material; the file that governs his window wins on it).

## 8. Holding and trading Skills (orbs and requirements)

**Skills crystallize when unseated.** When a delver drops a found or granted Skill out of their kit, it **crystallizes into an orb that carries its level** — kept in reserve or sold. The level goes with it, so a Skill trained to level 5 is worth far more as an orb than one at level 1; a delver selling it is selling the use-time someone already spent. This makes an unseated Skill a physical object — it can be kept, sold, lost, stolen, or confiscated — where a grown discipline in the body cannot.

**What crystallizes, and what doesn't.** Found or granted Skills orb out: Active, Passive, and Movement Skills from orbs, auras, and the choosing. Grown intrinsics do not (they are part of the delver, §5). Learned spells do not (they belong to the domain track — a sellable spell-orb would let a buyer skip their affinity gate, which the magic system forbids). And Bill's drafted abilities do not auto-orb; if he ever crystallizes one it is a deliberate, costly act, not income (the file that governs his window wins on it).

**Seating and use requirements.** Orbs and enchanted gear both carry **requirements that must be met to seat or use them**, fixed when the item is made or found and readable before purchase (item-Inspect, §7):

- **Attribute floors** — a heavy Active wants the Might to wield it, a fine one the Precision, a far-reaching one the Intellect. This is the main limit: a powerful orb can be bought and still sit useless until its floor is met.
- **A Delver Level floor** on the strong ones — the same number that gates tier entry (§12) also gates whether a core can hold a given orb, so the orb market can't shortcut the danger ladder.
- **Affinity or class keying**, where it fits — an aura or domain-flavored orb reads off the wearer's affinity; some orbs sit right only for a class.

As a rule of thumb, Common orbs ask little, Uncommon a modest attribute floor in their lane, and Rare a real attribute floor and often a Delver Level floor; the exact figure is set when the orb is found and read. Meeting the requirement is permission to hold; the Intellect-sized kit is still how many a delver runs at once — two separate limits, which is what lets the orb market stay open without breaking the curve.

**Gear works the same way, with one difference.** Enchanted gear carries use-requirements set at crafting (§13, §15), but gear is not seated in the Active kit — its only limits are the requirement, the mana it draws to run, and material scarcity. So strong combat gear should carry genuine attribute and Delver Level floors, not just a price, or it becomes a way to wear power a delver hasn't earned. Weak utility gear stays open to anyone.

Full catalog of orbs, their categories, and their legal status in `ability_orbs_v1.md`.

## 9. How the four make a build

Actives are what a delver does, passives are what they always have, a Movement Skill is how they get around, and intrinsics are how hard everything they do lands. A strong delver has a focused Active kit sized to their Intellect, one defining passive, the one Movement Skill that fits how they delve, and a few deep intrinsics that the kit feeds through. A weak delver has a pile of unconnected loot and nothing behind it.

With domains (`magic_domains_v1.md`): an intrinsic is often domain-flavored, and the two systems reinforce. Domain affinity says **what magic a caster can reach**; an intrinsic discipline says **how deep they have driven it**. An Arcane affinity plus an Arcane discipline is a real caster; an Arcane affinity with nothing behind it is broad and shallow.

The choosing (§6) adds to a build steadily — one offer per paying delve, shaped to how the delver has fought — while orbs, tomes, and auras are the loot that lets them reach past what the window offers, requirements permitting (§8).

## 10. How this runs at the table

- **Numbers are visible to the player (precise tracking).** The seated-kit count, the Intellect formula behind it, intrinsic levels, ability damage, and DCs are shown to the player — surfaced inline in bold-bracketed tags when mechanically in play (§3, `gm_operations.md` §4). The *characters* still experience their limits as felt rather than posted (a delver senses "I can't hold more sharp at once"), and Bill's window renders crisp figures as his anomaly — but the player always sees the exact numbers on the sheet. Questions like "is this seated," "has this evolved," "does this intrinsic carry this spell," "do I meet this orb's floor" are answered from the tracked values, not narrated vaguely.
- **Growth is earned** (§3, §12). Skills climb through real use under real danger, slowly; evolutions are earned beats, not handed out. Loot, teachers, and the choosing seed Skills; cultivation and use grow them. Nothing advances because time passed.
- **The seated kit is fiction, not bookkeeping.** Track what a delver actually keeps ready — the Active kit, the one aura, the one Movement Skill — as a short part of the sheet, the way gear is tracked. Do not turn it into a slot-management minigame; it exists to make "I can't be ready for everything at once" true, not to be optimized at a screen.
- **The choosing is a live beat, not a menu dump.** Present three offers in the fiction — terse words and a felt sense for an ordinary delver, full detail on a window that quantifies — let the player choose, and log the two locks (§6).

## 11. Bill

The framework here is the world's — it governs the delvers Bill meets, hires, fights, and competes with, whatever his own awakening turns out to be. His window renders this layer crisply where ordinary delvers feel it roughly: the seated Active kit as a list with its count, the Movement seat and the aura slot named, intrinsic levels and evolutions as figures, an orb's requirement floors and a Skill's level as numbers he can read and chart rather than only feel. His **choosing** arrives like everyone's, but rendered in full numeric detail — exact options, exact costs, category tags — where an ordinary delver gets terse words and a felt sense.

None of this touches his System's own creation slots: Skills he gains through the choosing, orbs, or tomes seat under the universal limits here; the abilities he **drafts** are a separate economy governed by the file that holds his window, and offered or orbed Skills never consume a drafting slot. Leave the rest to play; do not fix it here.

## 12. Decisions baked in (override freely)

- **Four categories,** Active / Passive / Movement / Intrinsic, with Movement carved out of the old Actives and held to one seat.
- **The Active kit scales with Intellect** (about one plus a tenth), felt and not published; the one-aura and one-Movement rules are flat, not scaled.
- **Open-ended intrinsics**, named as play produces them, not a fixed master list — matches the house style for skills (§6), archetypes (§21), and monster categories.
- **One evolution scale.** Every Skill uses the existing ability-level track (1–5), so the campaign keeps the scales it had — attributes (1–100), mundane skills (1–10), ability levels (1–5) — plus Delver Level (§12), and no new number line.
- **Felt caps, not published integers.** No hard "ten actives." The limits are real and in-fiction; the clean numbers stay off the page (and may render only on a window that quantifies).
- **Passives stay scarce.** Auras remain one-at-a-time; always-on boons are the rare bedrock.
- **The choosing's locks are permanent.** Two declined for every one taken, logged, never re-offered by the window.
- **Found Skills are tradeable, grown ones are not.** Unseated Skills crystallize into level-carrying orbs with seating requirements; intrinsics, spells, and Bill's drafts do not orb out.

*Where this file conflicts with `game_bible.md`, the Bible wins; on spell access it defers to `magic_domains_v1.md`; on the orb catalog and requirements detail it sits beside `ability_orbs_v1.md`; on Bill's faculty it defers to play and to the file that governs his window.*
