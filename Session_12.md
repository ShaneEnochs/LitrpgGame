## 1. Morning — the Compact answered (the session's main plot movement)

The pending two-outfit Compact with Pem (struck Day 6, never advanced) finally moved. **Pem sent the rules**, in Ada's copying hand, Pem's phrasing under. Tatch walked Bill through them from memory at the back door.

**The four base rules:** (1) **Patches** — Pem's ground is the awning man, the firm's is the depot man; neither outfit works the other's watch without the other boss saying yes first. (2) **Jobs/books** — solo work stays whole to that outfit, books separate; shared jobs go on the one set of shared books Min keeps, and the split is named *before* the job, not after the pile exists (Pem was hard on this). (3) **Crews don't deal crew-to-crew** — goods and word move boss-to-boss; neither outfit's kids take orders from the other's. (4) **Disputes** the two bosses can't settle go to **Calloway** — not to pick a winner, but to write down what was actually agreed so it stops being two memories.

**Pem's two hard lines:** **No buyout, ever** (neither outfit can buy out, absorb, or take over the other, at any price — *"I been somebody's property and I'm done bein' it; a paper that can be bought is a slower road to the same place"*). **Clean exit** (if it dissolves, each outfit walks out with what was theirs walking in — kids, ground, share of the shared books counted clean; no "we built it together so it's half mine").

**Bill's reading (Intellect):** the four base rules are clean (three he'd have asked for himself; crews-don't-deal-direct functions as mutual anti-poaching). Both hard lines are *defensive* — Pem negotiating not to be swallowed, not to grab — the most reassuring posture in a counterparty. The no-buyout is cheap to give and fatal to haggle. The one genuine gap: the clean-exit clause is clean for goods and cash but silent on **value built together during the season** (a trusted shared name, a returning customer, a kid taught a trade).

**Bill's answer:** yes to all four base rules (the split-named-first rule the one he'd have asked for); yes to both hard lines, as things Pem is *right* to want, not grudging. One fix requested: have Calloway write the *how* for the exit — a method to count or split what gets built together, so a dissolution settles cold in advance, same spirit as Pem's split-before-the-pile rule. Bill claims none of it; he wants the method named.

**Routing:** hold the whole thing until Calloway tightens the exit clause first, then Pem hears the yes and the clean marked paper all at once. Min carried it both ways on the noon trip. *(Advanced further this evening — see §6.)*

## 2. Morning — the medicine work (warm-weep fixed + proof run)

Bill set up in Maddox's scoured kitchen for the foot/hand line — staunch (yarrow) and knitbone (comfrey) — plus fixing the salve's **warm-weep defect**, holding Arrowood's line (comfrey and yarrow true actives, whole-skin only, clean tins/hands/base, never seal a dirty wound).

- **Lanolin correctly deferred** — a forward ingredient for the salve/balm base line, not needed for this morning's actives work; held for the line (Min to pin Dunphy's cleaned price, no money down). No errand sent.
- **Warm-weep diagnosis (Engineering/chemistry):** a base failure, not an actives failure — the carrier goes soft and weeps near skin heat. Fix = raise the melt point with a hard setter without killing spread.
- **Maddox's stores ruled out:** nothing that carries a smell (a pocket salve can't go rancid); and using her stores for Quest stock isn't within the kitchen arrangement (Bill brings his own makings, the stove is the courtesy).
- **Mercantile run #1 (morning):** bought yellow beeswax (by the portion) and a clean cheap working-man's oil, on the company account, retainer terms. Counterman's free advice: **test the measure by warming in the hand, not the stove** (stove runs hotter than a pocket); start stiff, cut back (softening a hard batch beats saving a runny one). Named two forward base candidates he'd send for: **spermaceti** (sets well, near odorless, dear) and **petrolatum / rock-oil jelly** (doesn't turn, near odorless, railroad men use it) — neither in stock.
- **The ratio test:** ran wax-to-oil ratios by hand-warmth, cut the wax back across small batches, found a **working ratio that holds at hand-warm without weeping and still spreads cold.** Ratio recorded, repeatable. **Engineer's epiphany:** the base is a *dial*, not one recipe — the same system gives a firmer warm-weather/summer tin and a softer deep-cold tin by moving the mapped ratio. A product *line* from one controlled base.
- **The proof run:** compounded a small batch on the fixed base — a couple of knitbone (comfrey) tins, a couple of staunch (yarrow) tins, plus plain base. ~4–5 hand-lettered test tins after the rent. A proof run, not sellable quantity (trial wax).
- **The rent + the staunch tin (Insight):** met the shed/bench rent exactly (topped Maddox's house tin with plain salve, no flourish) and separately set a staunch (yarrow) tin by the kitchen end with a flat word — for cuts, keep it by the kitchen. Maddox accepted it (kept it where he set it = it stays), called yarrow "soldier's woundwort," and gave a true free edge: **the hand-lettering won't do for strangers — mark it plainer before it goes to the cage.** (The seed of the struck-die plan.)

## 3. Afternoon — the gunsmith (fine bench work + discount pinned)

Fine fitting, after Day 11's rough stack. **The job:** a lever-action with a timing fault, owner collecting Saturday; the gunsmith set diagnosis-first as a test ("tell me what's wrong before you touch a file").

- **Diagnosis (Perception/Intellect):** catch coming back into battery just before the lever closes home; extra push where there shouldn't be one; a bright witness mark on the bolt face. Read: **carrier timing off** — the lifter arriving a hair late, so the bolt noses the carrier. Creed Bill articulated: **the witness mark is where the fault lands, not necessarily where it starts.** Took the sideplate off to confirm at the source (clean, no marks).
- **Confirmed:** the true high spot was the **lifter arm's bearing surface**, proud a few thousandths; the bolt-face shine only recorded the consequence.
- **The fix (taught as he worked):** fine file, light, one direction, checking between every stroke (remove the least metal that fixes it). Two inspirations: stone the bolt-face witness mark flush after, so the symptom surface doesn't become the next high spot; and note the part history on the job for the next man. Finished: fault fixed at cause, action cycles smooth.
- **The verdict + the number:** the gunsmith read Bill as a man with the bench who was missing the trade. **Pinned: the kit is 200 all-in; Bill's day of work cancels the lever-gun job (no cash either way) and buys the kit down to 100.** Path stated: another bench day like this and he'll talk the rest *toward cost* (unpinned until a second day). **No clock, no interest** on the remaining 100 — take the kit whenever Bill has the hundred. Bill said straight he's light on cash today and would rather work the number down than hand over a thin payment; the gunsmith was fine with it. **The 100 is not yet paid, the kit not yet taken** — a standing 100, no clock.
- **Shooting lessons asked and declined (steered):** the gunsmith won't sell a trade that isn't his; steered Bill to **Crane** (retired Guard sergeant, armory annex mornings, 50/session + ammunition). What he *will* fold in free on bench days: the gunsmith's side of shooting — how mechanism and shot connect, telling a gun's problem from a shooter's.
- **Next bench day set:** Day 13 (Saturday) **mid-morning**, after the owner collects the lever gun; real work, cold backlog.

## 4. Evening — the mercantile (branding settled + the kit-tin)

Back to the counter, full dark coming on. Bought **two cakes yellow beeswax** (~8 cr, Quest account, at cost) to scale past the proof run, and opened a **standing wax order** off the next valley freight. Then worked the counterman as the branding map.

- **The marking tier (counterman's straight counsel):** hand-lettering is done (reads like a kitchen). The Clarion printed-paper label is dear, recurring, and paper — peels in a delver's pack, and half of men read *swindle* off a patent-medicine label; hold it for a future parlor/townsfolk version. The **tinsmith's struck die** is the one — pay once to cut the die, every lid comes off it identical, cheap, metal (survives a pack), and uniform tins read like a real outfit made it. Bill's Insight read it as straight trade counsel (it points him at a one-time cost across town, taking the counterman out of the repeating sale); his Engineering agreed (a die is a tool you *own*).
- **The lid layout (Bill's design, refined with the counterman):** script *Quest* + maker's name across the **top band** (same on every product forever → the **struck die**, cut once, owned). Product name and instructions/warnings **beneath** — these change product to product, so they do **not** go in the steel; set cheap and changeable (an ink stamp per batch, a paper slip under the lid, or a small secondary die for the product word). The counterman's catch (which Bill's Engineering had already reached): cut the *whole* lid into one die and you've bought a die that only ever makes one product — every product a fresh die, a fresh charge, scrapped the day a word changes. **The fix is the same principle as the salve base: build the part that never changes once, keep the part that changes cheap to swap.** Brown-paper sketch of the layout pocketed for the tinsmith.
- **Product names settled (through several tries):** **Quest Muscle Balm** (comfrey/knitbone — the ache product, clean and strong-selling). The yarrow tin was the trouble: *Wound Ointment* markets the misuse Arrowood forbade (men packing it into open cuts); *Crackseal* smuggled *seal* back in; landed on **Quest Hand and Foot Balm** — points the tin at the body parts and the whole-skin use entirely inside the doctor's line, no misuse invited, no doctor's permission needed to sell it. (The yarrow tin is the foot/hand line by its own name.)
- **The first-aid / wound kit (NEW idea, parked):** standing at the counter, Bill's earlier first-aid-kit thought surfaced — sell *kits* to delvers at the cage. Asked after bigger containers; the counterman pulled a **flat hinged tin** (saddler's/teamster's, lays flat in a pack, lid snaps, hinged so a one-handed man doesn't drop it in the mud). **Bought one sample kit-tin** (trivial, account) to put on Arrowood's desk. The kit is a *system* sale — a cleaning agent (spirits or tincture of iodine — both period-real; the clean-first-then-protect two-step that fixes the wound-naming problem instead of dodging it), cloth/lint, the balms, whatever Arrowood says belongs — one container, several Quest products inside, refills after. **Two flags:** it's a high medical claim (Medicine is 2; **Arrowood's to bless or kill**, and the relationship matters as much as the blessing — bring it as *help me build this right under rules you set*, not as a layman teaching a doctor); and the counterman is a willing supplier but a useless medical advisor. **Parked for Arrowood, a daylight surgery-hours errand.**

## 5. Evening — the shed with Tatch and Min (coats funded, Compact routed)

Bill sent the kids to the **back door** (the trained channel), then took a portion of **his own supper** (bread, handheld — not two plates; sharing his own portion, not redistributing Maddox's table, and small enough to draw nothing from Maddox) out to the **shed**: out of the wind, his by rent, lamp up, the kids fed while they worked through the day's two deliveries.

**The coats (taken first — business waits):** Min reported the wool work. The hole stands and she found its bottom: between raw fleece and woven cloth sit **carding and spinning**, unpriced women's hearth-labor, and once you price those hours the from-scratch road climbs back up toward just buying the bolt. She had the **made-cloth and sewing** prices firm. The **Dunphy lever** (raw fleece + a carding hand under one favor) is a *maybe* leaning on a carding hand she couldn't confirm secondhand. Tatch closed his half: Pem approached person-to-person, the priority ranked by age and need as marks not names.

- **Decision: bolt-and-sew — fast over cheap-and-slow.** The cold is already here; the from-scratch saving would arrive after the winter it was meant to beat. **Per coat ~9 cr** (≈6 wool + ≈3 sewing; bigger kids more). **Eleven kids** on the priority top (the ones genuinely without a coat against this cold); more below. **≈100 total** to coat all eleven (buy the bolt whole and wool likely comes under 6/coat).
- **Bill gave Min 40 cr cash** to start it — wool bought first thing tomorrow, the first hands cutting, **youngest-and-coldest first** (~4 coated for sure off the 40, the rest rolled in as more money comes). The 40 is **Bill's own money, a person's act, not the firm's** — Min administers it.
- **Speed lever (Min's point):** the bottleneck is sewing hands, not wool (wool is an instant daylight buy). **Several hands at once** → first coats out in 2–3 days, all eleven inside a week — and paying *several* women to sew puts winter coin into the same households the effort is aimed at.
- **The Dunphy lever redirected** — off coats, onto **supply** (cleaned fleece + lanolin for the balm line). Min keeps it warm, pins Dunphy's prices direct, no money down.

**The Compact (Calloway's mark):** Calloway took the one exit-clause fix and did it lawyer-clean in three parts. (1) **Split built-together value into two kinds:** *divisible* (customer list, shared stock, shared-money tools/cash, a name's cash goodwill) — counted, split by each outfit's share, named before the dissolution, same as the shared-books rule; *indivisible* (a kid taught a trade, a reputation, a supplier relationship) — **not divided at all**; the skill follows the person, and **a taught kid owns the trade — neither boss may claim a child's hands as property** (written knowing Pem's history — the clause that will land hardest with him). (2) **Method for hard cases:** where the bosses can't agree what a built thing is worth or who it follows, it goes to **Calloway** to *record*, not pick a winner — the existing dispute rule extended to dissolution-value. (3) **A running record:** lasting shared-built things get **noted in Min's shared books as they're made** (not valued, just recorded), so a dissolution reads off a record instead of two memories — Pem's split-before-the-pile principle pushed one step earlier.

- **Bill's reading (Insight):** better than what he sent up — a method, a clean kind-split, a child-protection clause in Pem's own language, and a record-keeping habit that makes the exit clause enforceable instead of aspirational.
- **The team's read (asked):** Tatch — deliver it Pem's way (clean paper, his hand, his own time, no sit-down; a meeting makes him hunt for the catch); the kid-trade clause is the part Pem will like most. Min (the treasurer's catch, sharper than Bill's half-seen one) — the running record is *her* book tracking *Pem's* built-value too, and a man wary of being owned might balk; **write Pem a standing right to read the shared book any time.** Bill's Intellect: make it **both ways** (the rule binds him first or it binds nobody).
- **One amendment added:** mutual book-inspection right (either boss may read the shared book on demand, both directions).
- **Routing:** **back to Calloway** for the one amendment (Min carries up first thing Day 13), **then Tatch to Pem** with the whole clean paper to read on his own time, no sit-down. Pem answers when ready.
- **STATUS: still PENDING — answered, tightened by Calloway, one final amendment (mutual book-inspection) going up, then to Pem for execution. Close, not closed. Keep ⛔ DO NOT COMPRESS.**

## 6. Evening — the Drovers', a third pass at Pearce

Bill swung by the Drovers' for a **cheap drink** (3 cr) to see if a loosened Pearce talked. Read the room (Perception, ordinary tells): freight men, a quiet table of working delvers, Pearce at the bar-end. **Key read (Insight):** it was *late* — past Pearce's talkable first-hour-or-two window, deeper into the rye than Bill had ever caught him. That cut two ways: the pride-wire a little number (he might say the thing the sober man swallowed) *and* the maudlin edge right there, where pity/charity/the-rescue and *liking-him-and-getting-clumsy* (Calloway's warning) all become temptations that feel like kindness.

- **The lure:** Bill asked **Fenner** (light, not leaning on him) about catalogues and books on engineering, medicine, herbs. Fenner: the counterman's catalogue or Saint Orison; not his line. **Pearce reacted** — an exhale at the *gap*: an expert hearing a layman list the soft outsides and skip **chemistry**, the rigorous middle under all three and the heart of his dead trade.
- **Bill's move:** turned, chuckled, said if he'd known books were this hard to get out here he'd have brought his own — a rueful joke at his *own* expense, no reach, the easy thing for a proud man to pick up.
- **Pearce volunteered, unasked, three rye in:** he *did* bring his own — crates of them, the metallurgy, the chemistry, the assay tables, the old Bureau grading texts, **all boxed in the shuttered building going to damp**; named his wound himself (the Bureau made his trade worthless); *"it's hard to get anybody who wants the ones already here."* (He complained that nobody wants the books — he did **not** offer them.)
- **Bill met it peer-to-peer:** agreed with the *waste* — good knowledge, like good tools, rotting where the place got smaller than the thing built right. Wanted nothing, took nothing, pried nothing. Pearce repeated it back to himself ("a thing built right, left to rot... that's about the size of it") and **sat thinking instead of drinking** — the third time. Bill **left on the high note** (a plain word, no last line doing work). Pearce: *"Mister"* — a notch sorry the talk was done.
- **Deposit:** the deepest step yet, taken with no clumsy move. From *talked to me, not past me* → **this one sees what was actually lost.** The boxed library (chemistry/assay books), the microscope, and the building all sit one season closer; nothing pried for. Reagent-supply and the deferred microscope test both still route through this man.
