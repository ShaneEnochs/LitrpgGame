THE LIVING WORLD — OFFSTAGE TRACKER (DM-ONLY, LIVING FILE, v1)

DO NOT REVEAL DIRECTLY. This file tracks the world beyond Bill's reach so the frontier feels alive and causal rather than frozen. The player never reads it — but its Public items are what Bill finds when he picks up the Oregon Clarion, hears at the depot, or reads on a posted notice, and its Rumor items are what reaches him secondhand. Reveal only through those channels, in play.

This is the one secret file meant to change. Unlike the canon and the other truth files, the DM advances this one at session close. Discipline:

- Most sessions, little moves. A line in the paper, a rumor, a price drift. Big developments are rare; breakthroughs rarer still.
- Every change has a stated cause. Nothing happens because the plot wants it; it happens because someone with motive acted, or a young institution reacted, or a pressure already in canon played out. Record the cause in the update log.
- It defers to canon and to the other truth files. It never overrides game_bible.md, and it never resolves or accelerates the central mystery — on the cell's true nature it defers entirely to hollow_reach_truth_v3.md, and on fixed minor-mystery answers to mystery_anchors_v1.md. It tracks outward footprints, not secrets earned in play.
- Voice: the DM tracking is plain. Sample Clarion lines are in the paper's period voice (the in-character-document exception), the same register as the Day-9 wire article.
- Visibility tags: Public (printed/posted, Bill can read it) · Rumor (secondhand, uncertain) · Secret (DM-only; surfaces only if play digs it out).

TRACK 2 — The federal delve regime (USBD & government policy)
State (post-Day 11): Registration holds at an estimated 80%+ of delvers. The standing rules — mandatory assay, licensed assessment and government reporting of recovered dungeon goods — are on the books but unevenly enforced on the frontier, and a gray-to-black market in unassessed goods persists. A clarifying circular on Special Delve claims and access is being drafted in Saint Orison. Why: A young agency standardized to extend its reach; the standard immediately produced cases it has no procedure for. The Bureau answers the only way it knows — more forms. Visibility: Public (circulars, posted notices) + Rumor (what's coming). Visibility: Public (Clarion picks up Saint Orison news) + Rumor (the circuit's timing).
