## Story so far

Bill Weaver came to New Abilene alone, a 22-year-old engineer with an alchemist's streak, to get an adventuring license and chase a dream his father hates: understanding the dungeons well enough to control one instead of burying it. His mother died in the Severn Falls break in 1852, when he was twelve. He is careful, forgettable on purpose, and works through people. He wears different names in different rooms (see the sheet) and keeps two ciphered notebooks hidden in the casing of his own house's upstairs window.

Over thirteen days he built a foundation rather than delving: a patronage deal with the editor Pickering funded his licensing (now a clean provisional); he turned down the dangerous Hollow Reach placement and took a surface-assessment arrangement with the fair guild officer Doss (filed unnamed); he assembled a firm of street kids with a treasury and rules, struck a two-outfit Compact with the rival boss Pem, built alliances across town (Calloway, Maddox, Hessler, Arrowood, Beatrice, the mercantile counterman), started a salve line, and named the whole enterprise **Quest**. On Day 13 his father answered: a keyed reply wire, a 5,000-cr gift cleared whole into trust, and a furnished house. Bill resolved to collapse to his true name eventually but keep it for last, landing house, money, and name apart so no watcher sees them arrive together; for now a trust holds the deed and Wyatt is the dull tenant.

On **Day 14** he answered Sarah Maddox about the house face-to-face (ownership and risk his, the firm's only to use), and she accepted it while holding her gate. He read the firm's books with Min and found it not yet self-sustaining, then designed **Quest Academy** as the fix — a relay where he teaches the able kids who teach the rest, letters bound to the trade, the watch held by shifts, the cost carried as investment. At the guild he gave Hessler sample tins (a verdict due Tuesday), cleared the clerk's filing backlog for goodwill, set up a paid certification job for Monday, reconciled a books gap, and **set a new rule: his expert labor now pays his own pocket, the firm earns off the kids' trades.** He carried the **Hollow Reach re-measure** to Doss (she'll arrange cover and send for him) and opened the **Foundation-delve** road (the Pilgrim's Well; her conditional letter of introduction, gated on him getting trained first). He visited Esther, who handed over her **recognition list for Amos** and asked him to find out what became of her brother. He won the **Dunmore Brothers' lift-works job** — designing a fall-arrest safety catch, stacking a three-part standing partnership on it — and, at the Drovers', turned a real corner with the broken assayer **Pearce**, who critiqued the design's metallurgy, sparked Bill's redesign, and agreed to read the actual steel Monday as a paid consultant. First night in his own house.

On **Day 15** he ran the day in order: finished the lift-catch redraw, then at Calloway's signed the house lease (as Wyatt), sent the keyed patent alert wire to his father (signed Q, ~145 from the trust, plans held until the steel is read), and met the day's hook — an **anonymous letter** routed through the receiving channel, keyed QUEST on the fold, from someone who knows small-room facts (the drawer-filing, the channel, the three watchers by function) and that QUEST is his father's word, who positions himself **above** the cell's watchers, signs only "a friend of the work," and tells Bill to burn it. Bill ordered it **boxed, not burned**, named his brother **Shaun** (gone four years after an advanced-mathematics degree, asked not to be found), and set Calloway **tracing Shaun quiet and slow.** He learned the gift is settled clean money and the structured remittances are shaped to begin ~Day 17. He bought the gunsmith kit outright with a full component set and took the gunsmith's free trade instruction; routed the mercantile lift-parts and the duplicate-key errand through Tatch; read a clean watch report flagging **three strangers asking about people, one about the message-running children** (Tatch pulled the littles back). He gave notice at Maddox's and **cut that tie clean** (end room, shed, Tom's bench all surrendered, square), moved the **ciphered notebooks into his own upstairs window casing**, drew 255 from the trust for cushion, and ran the afternoon's build: at the smith **Pearce read the steel and taught the reading** (the relationship's deepest step — paid 25, a master's fee), agreed to **teach the heat-treat as Bill works it** ("I'll take the third on faith"), and at the Dunmore warehouse Bill **fabricated and fitted the catch** (hardening pending).

On **Day 16** he ran the build day. Morning: he set up shooting lessons with **Crane** (own reloads, mornings, weather permitting — Doss's shooting condition begun), collected the struck **Quest die** (80 cr) and ordered lids, took **Hessler's positive sample-tin verdict** (his wife's winter-cracked hands healed whole — she wants more), and spent his first real **knife hours with Dell** (Doss's yard-hours condition begun). He sent for **Pearce** the right way and at the Dunmore warehouse **hardened the lift-catch under Pearce's eye** (the master taught the heat-treat: color-reading, case-hardening, the quench, the temper). On the careful once-over Bill **caught a real fault** (the wedge seated a hair off-square) and **walked Cobb and Holt through the fault and the fix** before re-truing it. Then the **cut-rope demo HELD**: Bill stood under the full loaded cage, the rope was cut, the catch bit dead-level. The **Dunmore lift-works job closed** (265 to pocket) and the **three standing arrangements fired**. He **restructured the firm into the green** — the firm as an independent **subcontractor** of Quest, a standard work-for-hire form built from the kids' own side, Quest paying **1 cr/tin**. Min reported the **kids' coats** near done. That night Bill ran the **first full Remedies production** (58 tins).

On **Day 17** he ran a foundation day, no heavy spine fired. Morning: he handed the firm its **first distribution run** cleanly (tins to the tinsmith for lids — struck and ready today — then 30 to Dunmore, 20 to Hessler, 8 samples placed; Quest pays the firm 1 cr/tin; Tatch signs under the conflict guard, Bill the customer), and from the morning fire **reasoned out a mundane pocket hand-warmer** (hurried rust — iron filings + brine + bone-black + sawdust; a Quest Instruments product). At **Calloway's** the **first structured remittance landed** (to trust, the locked "exactly what I'd have advised" note delivered and read); the Shaun trace is slow with nothing back; the boxed letter sits untouched; Calloway will draft the retainer move (gated on the counterman's yes) and read the restructure as **sound and defensible but not invisible**. He shot a **full cold morning out to the last round with Crane** (Shooting up), and over lunch Crane turned his training strategic (the gun is the first word, not the whole answer; train the first shot from a bad start, blind speed-reloads, and the gun-knife seam). He brought that framing to **Dell**, who taught the **transition** (Melee up) — the cross-draw as the seam made physical, knife-when-the-gun's-empty, and the reverse. At the **gunsmith** he confirmed his revolver is a **top-break**, designed a **speedloader** (eject all six on the break + a rim-gripping holder that charges six blind), and set a **build day for Day 19/Friday** (half-commission + the owed cold-backlog day). At the **mercantile** he read the counterman tired and **wisely held the retainer ask**, bought bone-black, and **warmed the counterman** with a true word about Charlie. That evening at his own bench he **proved the hand-warmer real** — characterized the heat-vs-duration dial, found the working ratio, located the safe band and the danger ceiling — leaving three open problems (slow start, packaging, iron supply). He squared the notebook and slept, the second full night in his own house.

The central mystery is Hollow Reach, the sealed dungeon mouth northwest of town. Crew 4 went down Day 9; the re-measure afterward is the finding, owed to Doss first (active guild op). A land company (Coulter & Reese) holds the ground around the adit, posted private and guarded. The USBD's reclassification to "Special Delve" has drawn an ignorant rush northwest. An unnamed party ("a friend of the work") has demonstrated deep knowledge of Bill's situation and his family's cipher, positioning itself above the cell's watchers — a contact surface opened, not resolved.
