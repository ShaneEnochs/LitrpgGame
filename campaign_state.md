## Story so far (compressed)

Bill Weaver — true name held by very few — came to New Abilene as "Wyatt," a forgettable fix-it man, carrying a real purpose under the cover: he means to understand the dungeon at Hollow Reach, and the wrong machinery moving under this frontier, without being seen doing it. Over nineteen days he has built a life dense enough to stand on: a discreet children's firm (Tatch coordinating, Min on the books) that now earns its own keep on standing income; a furnished house held through a trust with no Weaver name on it; a lawyer (Calloway) holding everything that could hang him; a newspaperman (Pickering) bound by a confidentiality promise and a poison-pill account; and a quiet web of trades and teamsters who know him as competent and dull.

The Hollow Reach baseline — the only "before" that will ever exist — was measured on Day 7, surface only, never past the mouth. Crew 4 went down after; the surfacing of what comes back up is the heavy thing still owed. Bill's father Malcolm answered the wire in their idiom: a 5,000-cr gift (cleared whole, in trust) and a separate structured 5,000-cr loan now arriving in staggered parcels. The firm has begun branding and distributing Quest Remedies; the first distribution run and a camp run have shipped through the subcontractor structure. Bill has taken up the gun (Crane), the knife (Marguerite for the cut, Dell for the fight), and a string of inventions that carry his hand — a patented lift catch, a now-workable hand-warmer, a designed speedloader, a solved-on-paper self-feeding stove — against the standing discipline to *build dull*. The school wish, planted with Sister Beatrice, reached Father Lessard's table on Day 18 and became a real mission the priest will carry to his Bishop.

**Day 19 (this session):** a clean full day on Bill's own sequencing — the self-feeding stove solved on paper (a safety-first redesign that fails toward waste, not invisible gas); the gunsmith bench day, where a deliberate cheap-brass speedloader **mule** taught the real problem (spring fingers take a set and fatigue; grip-vs-release plus all-six-matched) and the holder was re-scoped for a proper build; the extractor tuned to *good* (bearings smoothed, the timing cut held in reserve); the home component lot run down on the gunsmith's press; a strict solo session at Crane's (snap-draw kept honest, the extractor's late-pickup confirmed real-but-marginal on a fouled fast break); and at home, the **hand-warmer brought to a workable prototype** (slow-start and packaging both solved, only the iron supply/scale left). No heavy spine fired — the crew-4 surfacing again had no natural channel and was correctly not forced.
