# Session 10 — Day 10, Wednesday, November 5, 1862

*Dated archive. Write-once. Current state lives in `campaign_state.md`; this is the record of what happened.*

New Abilene. First hard cold of the season setting in. Bill's fifth full night's sleep behind him; healthy, unwounded throughout.

## Money this day (reconciled)
Morning pocket 39 cr. Hollis paid +10 (the graphite-paste spring fix, "the paste and the hour"). Mid-day the pocket was set to **92 by player adjustment (godhand)** — this absorbed the earned Hollis 10, not counted twice. Then −50 (Quest curtain filing at Calloway's) and +25 (Bly, "for the hour"). **End-of-day pocket: 67 cr.** Quest tab still 18; firm's 60 still moving east with the wire.

## The day, in order

**Breakfast at Maddox House.** Mrs. Maddox set Bill's plate before the boarders' (as since the flue). Bill asked where to acquire coats for the firm's children against the coming winter; she named two doors — the mission poor-box (free, but a different look) and **Yost's used-clothes stall** (the door she'd use, cheap, asks no questions). Bill said he'd buy when the pocket was real, not on credit. He asked to use her kitchen for new medicine formulations; she refused *today* (wash-day eve, bread in, Hollis at midday) and set it for **Friday morning** — he confirms again tonight (done), she scours the good pot Thursday night, he brings his own makings, he holds the doctor's line (wax + safe simples only). Bill confirmed he'd cleared the line with Arrowood himself.

**Firm brief (Tatch, walking).** Three things handed over: (1) **Quest Academy** as a real plan — Tatch's input: don't call it a school (orphan-house connotations; runaways won't be "kept"), make it letters *and* paid work the same hour, and Bill should buy the room/books/time but not be the face at the front (Ada teaches the little ones). (2) The **product lines** — Tatch's read: foot-balm first, sell it cheap on Freight Lane (the firm's ground, not Hessler's counter), and fix the weeping/separation problem before selling a single tin (a bad tin on the Lane tells forty men). (3) The **coats** — Bill framed it as *a person's* act, not the firm's/guild's/fix-it man's: he'll outfit Pem's and Tatch's children first, asked Tatch to carry it to Pem person-to-person with no hook ("you can't tell him there's no hook; only not have one and let him not find it"). Coats handed out *through the firm* (Pem's to Pem's hand, Bill's name on no coat) — same principle as the school. Count held (no list of names — a danger if a wrong eye reads it); instead a **priority ranking by age and need**, kept as marks not names (3 = won't-last-a-bad-night down to 1 = has-somebody). Bill also floated the **wool idea**: buy raw wool from a sheep man (Dunphy) and pay a depot sewing-hand a wage to make coats — three people fed off one winter. Tatch to return inside a day with the sewing-hand lead and the wool-vs-Yost sum. *(No samples moved — the only finished product is the salve, wrong for the test market's feet/hands; the foot line doesn't exist until Friday. Word to Hessler: the line lands after Friday.)*

**Hollis's rig — the named debt, paid in full (depot freight end, midday).** Bill chose to chase the named fault first, then everything else. Diagnosis (Appraisal + Engineering): the near-side **spring pack**, not the hub — scoured dry, leaves sawing, U-bolt clip seated slightly loose. Offered two fixes honestly: the free plain re-grease (covered by the handshake, washes out by spring) or a **graphite-tallow paste** of Bill's own make (holds a year+, charged above the deal). Walked Hollis through cost-plus — 3 cr goods + 7 cr the extra hour = **10 even** — against the lost-afternoons-per-season it saves. Hollis took it ("a teamster's number"). The whole job run as a **teaching session for Tom** (Hollis's ~16-yr nephew), quizzing the *why* of every step: jack the axle not the spring; the dry-metal feel; even-loosening the clip; and the real lesson — graphite packs and *stays* where oil flows out. Then a full **walk-round taught as a method** (the three categories: do-now / watch / leave). It turned up the **off-front hub** dry-but-not-scored (the lesson job) and its **root cause** — a worn linchpin/dished washer letting the wheel wander, which dried that hub faster than its mate. Ran the **hub rebuild** as Tom's first front end: witness-marking before teardown, reading dry-vs-scored by feel, clean-and-repack (grease where steel meets steel), and dressing the dished washer flat + sound pin so the fix *holds*. Both fronts quiet and tight, most of it Tom's hands. Hollis: "a man with a trade," and he'll let Tom lead the back spring pack and the next hub himself.

**Calloway's (Professional Street).** Filed the **Quest trade-name curtain** — *Bill Weaver doing business as Quest*, true name on the second line, signed, clerk carried it to the justice of the peace; registered tomorrow, operating as Quest by week's end; the mercantile tab converts on registration. **−50 cr.** Calloway approved the name "Quest" (dull, expands without lying, harmlessly hopeful) and re-stressed: the curtain is a cover, not a separation — this month, legally, Quest *is* Bill; put nothing heavy under it. Bill gave the **Pearce report** (first contact, the pride read, leaving on the high note, not following him through the door). Calloway's counsel: don't lead with the gift; arrive at "I need an assayer and he is one," never rescue; "liking him is when men get clumsy." Bill asked, carefully and indirectly, whether the father-wire had a reply — **none yet**; Calloway noted a careful man doesn't answer a weighty sealed message same-day, and will send word by runner the hour anything lands. *(Calloway knows only that a sealed, ciphered message went east after he'd advised 3,000; he does not know the figure Bill settled on.)*

**Guild hall (late afternoon — the awakened world onstage at last).** Bill went in with his eyes open and made the deliberate choice to start being *known for the work* (Quest the trade, his face kept behind a runner-channel — banked the larger "protective prominence vs. invisibility" strategy for Calloway later). He eased Hessler's line by taking handheld repair off the room: **fixed and upgraded a delver's gauze lamp** (the owner unnamed, drinking off a bad delve, collects tomorrow) — diagnosed a worn raiser starving the wick, dressed the wick, and added a **sprung detent giving click-stops at known heights** (better than new). Worked it in full view, unhurried, no performance. Then a prickly **Iron-licensed crew boss** brought a **fouled essence-gauge** to the members' back bench. Bill drew the body/heart line honestly, opened the *body only* (with permission, narrating every move, hands visible), confirmed the **movement sound** and refused to touch the **sensing element** — which, by the symptoms (smooth swing to false readings, settles normally, fouled off a bad site, a faint wrong cast at the heart the owner confirmed is *new*), is past a tinker and a licensed maker's job. First essence-touched dungeon instrument Bill has had open. Closed it, handed it back. The crew boss named himself — **Tamsin Bly** — paid **+25 cr "for the hour"** (his pride won't carry a free ride; Bill took it square), and established standing trade: **Quest services the *bodies* of finer instruments**, word left via the firm's runner-channel, Bly will spread the name to other gear-carrying crews. Bly's standing invitation: leave word about the contract board's delve work too.

**The delve trade, mapped (Hessler).** Bill read the board with Bly's framework: Olles's supervised-descent posting from Day 6 is **filled/closing** (the two junior haul-spots taken — the road recurs, but that posting is spent). A **fresh crew call** went up today (experienced crew, harder site, short fill window; principal not named — noticed, nothing hung on it). Hessler's lay of supervised work: others run it besides Olles; much is arranged man-to-man off the board over a drink; **day-length supervised delves on close, tame, soft holes exist** and run quiet here, found by *asking the men who run them*, not reading paper. His conclusion (drawn himself, no names): the road for a man like Bill is being known to the right person — which loops to Bly, a small-crew boss who takes close cheap work. Bill weighed and **rejected** asking Doss to take him down (wrong person — an officer, not a guide; strains the relationship built on his caution; the awakening is a deliberate life-fork, not a favor; smells of his own named bias on the day the delving world got shiny). Bill ended the visit thanking Hessler; Hessler will point instrument body-work toward Quest when he hears of it.

**Maddox House, evening.** Quiet supper (Bill cleared his own plate, thanked her, reminded her of Friday again — said twice as asked). Maddox: gone early to Calloway's, won't be at breakfast; restated her trustee terms unprompted — she'll sign if the instrument matches Bill's speech, won't if it says something cleverer ("a woman who'd sign a thing she didn't read isn't one you'd want holding it for those children"). Told Bill to take the extra blanket.

**The letter to Malcolm (written and sealed, signed Q).** The warm half the cipher couldn't carry, to follow the wire east via Calloway. Plain, period-formal; owns the pride as a grown man's fault (not a boy's); names **Shaun** and the empty chairs (Rebecca gone, Shaun gone — Bill refuses to be the third); the West as a different country with a different arithmetic; the children and the destitution; the trades Malcolm taught him doing good; the money's *shape* (back into the people, raises more than one man) without specifics; "a safer hand than the mails" for the rest; "I love you, Father"; reply via Calloway. Goes to Calloway in the morning. *(Shaun's estrangement and Rebecca's name now sit in Bill's own written words to his father — consistent with the Session-7 road decision: Q, business letter, the name carries everything.)*

**Before bed:** half an hour with the dispensatory and book of simples (fever and wound-dressing chapters, read against the mission evidence — the texts' "why" still doesn't fit what he saw). Ordinary study; Medicine theory by inches.
