## ⚠⚠ HOLLOW REACH — THE BASELINE (site intelligence, Day 7, all canon)

Field book kept in piles — facts and impressions separated, sourced, timed, per Calloway's discipline.*

### The findings (facts pile)

- **THE DIVERGENCE IS REAL, MEASURABLE, AND QUANTIFIED.** 100 yards: flame standard, gas sound. 50 yards: flame standard, gas sound. **20 yards: flame at ~4/5 standard height, steady, faint cold cast at the core — gas tests SOUND, three times** (lime water clear, papers clean). **At the threshold (the mouth, never past it): flame ~2/3 standard height, visibly cold-cored — gas sound at his feet.** Flame height logged in fractions of an inch against sound air at known distance and time.
- **The effect releases with distance, no lag worth naming** — the carried lamp recovered to standard height by the bench edge, checked against its 100-yard sister.
- **Surface mortar/stone at the adit frame: AMBIENT.** Ordinary cool rock in shade. Inference (marked as inference): whatever warms mortar is deeper than daylight reaches — **the effect on stone is depth-graded; the effect on flame reaches the surface.**
- **THE DEAD-LIFE BOUNDARY: real, roughly centered ON THE ADIT** (not the bench, camps, or fire rings — the mouth is the bullseye). **It is NOT a circle: a teardrop**, long axis down the drainage — nearest fresh sign ~**300 yards** (uphill, over the rock shoulder); farthest dead ground ~**600 yards** (down the drainage, near the deadfall-pine mark Tom's "where'd the birds go?" first flagged from the wagon). Inference (marked): *a plume* — the way smoke or seep water moves from a source along the path of least resistance.
- **EMPTY, NOT KILLING:** inside the boundary, old sign everywhere — weathered pellets, prints baked into old mud, a years-old nest. **No carcasses, no bones, not one.** "Nothing died here. Things stopped coming."
- **STABLE ACROSS THE DAY:** the re-walk (4:35–4:50) found **no boundary movement in three hours**; the lamp line re-read unchanged. Whatever this is, on Day 7 it is in equilibrium — steady, not advancing. **That is the before. Crew 4 descends Tuesday. The re-measure after is the finding.**
- **THE TIMBER STACK IS DOWN BY A THIRD OR BETTER** (Hollis, firsthand — he tarped it to his own shoulder after hauling crew 2 out). Crew 3 carried orders to re-close the gap; the material went DOWN. **The re-seal work was at least started; materials were consumed below.** A physical fact about the state of the gap that nobody on the surface has known until now.
- Surface detail: fire rings cold and old; grass stops in patches with bare dirt nothing colonizes; no ants or beetles under turned rocks; the sawhorse carries no wasp nest, no spider silk; weathered wood that should be crawling is clean. The adit's air **smells of nothing at all** — no damp, no rot, no rock.
