## NEW CANON THIS SESSION (LOCKED — preserve)

1. **THE BASELINE WAS DELIVERED — and made invisible on paper.** Bill gave Doss the full Sunday baseline standing, from memory, piles separate (the recitation, not a summary). Then the two of them deliberately engineered what her office *officially received*: a true-but-useless receipt — *consultant's surface assessment, NW site, under sanction; surface works examined, no descent; site found quiet, no contact, no recovery; instrumentation to certification standard, equipment returned, reagents expended; verbal report received and accepted; no further action recommended pending scheduled guild operations.* Filed in the **ordinary cabinet**, not the locking drawer (nothing-happened paper). **The measurements exist nowhere in the building** — only in Bill's book and Doss's head. She named the cost out loud: she is trading the baseline's **legal standing today for its existence tomorrow**, because the writ's reporting clause took Garlan's log inside a day. (Consistent with `hollow_reach_truth_v3.md`: the cell controls the archive; Doss is a fair officer working around it.)
2. **DOSS'S NEW STANDING RULE (LOCKED):** the re-measure does **NOT** ride Sunday's permission. Sunday was lawful because the writ gave her office cause to assess the surface *before* guild operations. Once Garlan's crew is down, the site is an **active guild operation** and her paper thins. When the time comes, Bill **comes to her first** and they find out what permission exists. He agreed. (This is now the governing constraint on the campaign's central operational question — when/how/under what posture Bill gets back to the mouth.)
3. **Bill disclosed truthfully but minimally about the written record:** one copy, his own hand, piles separated, secured — and **stopped short of WHERE.** Doss noticed the omission, approved it with a single nod, and left it alone. (The location of the four books / the casing stays off every channel, including a trusted officer's.)
4. **THE NORTHWEST BUYER HAS A NAME: COULTER & REESE LAND COMPANY.** From Calloway's docket map (150 cr, delivered): **seven** recorded instruments now (two filed Monday morning in the land office's post-Sunday backlog), a **broken horseshoe** of parcels surrounding the old adit claim but not touching it. Registered back east — **Ohio incorporation** on the filing. No local office, no local officer; every instrument executed by agents under **recorded powers of attorney**. **All five powers drafted by one hand** (Quill's copyist tell holds — Calloway would swear to the habits himself) and **notarized in Saint Orison**, three by one notary, two by another. The surrounding work is **FINISHED as of Monday morning.**
5. **THE KEYSTONE (LOCKED reading of the map):** the adit claim — where Hollow Reach sits — is **unpatented public ground the locators abandoned owing the county.** It cannot be quitclaimed off heirs; it must be acquired **from the county or by patent**, a louder, slower, more public act than anything done so far. Coulter & Reese have bought every quiet parcel *around* it and saved the loud one for last — for a window when nobody's positioned to bid against them. The day they file on the adit (or the county posts a tax/sheriff's sale on it), the quiet window has shut, surface access ends (posted = trespass), and it is public record within the hour.
6. **⚠ QUILL'S HASTE-TELL (the session's quietly biggest fact):** at supper, unprompted, as a craftsman's grievance, Quill noted the **two filings recorded Monday morning were engrossed in HASTE** — the same good drafting hand, but moving fast, "like somebody told the lawyer get these in." Five weeks of patient, careful work, then sudden hurry — **the same morning crew 4's wagons rolled NW.** This is the thing Calloway said he couldn't price (the *when*): patient men got hasty over the weekend. **Something changed.** (DM-side: develop per the truth files. Quill does not grasp the significance and MUST NOT be taught to — see player discipline below.)
7. **PEARCE'S BUILDING — title result:** Pearce owns the shuttered assay office **outright, free of mortgage**, but there is a **three-year county tax lien**, ~**340 cr** to clear today and compounding. No other encumbrance. Two acquisition roads: from Pearce (whiskey + a season's plan, per Calloway's standing counsel) or **out from under him at a county tax sale** by anyone watching the delinquency rolls. Bill is "the second person Calloway knows of who knows the lien exists." Building still **last** — never the landlord before the works exist.

Her locked terms, now Bill's to honor in the drafting: (a) she answers to the **instrument**, not to Bill — the children are named owners, she is the hands, never answerable to his say-so; (b) a **named-succession clause** goes in or she won't sign (she buried a husband intestate on one bad mine paper and won't do it to children); (c) the day this stops being about the children and starts being about whatever Bill is at in this town, **she rules against him the way she ruled on the shed** — and he should want that. She knew Calloway already (Tom's lot line, '59 — "the only lawyer who got *less* friendly when the money got bigger").
