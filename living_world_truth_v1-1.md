THE LIVING WORLD — OFFSTAGE TRACKER (DM-ONLY, LIVING FILE, v1)

DO NOT REVEAL DIRECTLY. This file tracks the world beyond Bill's reach so the frontier feels alive and causal rather than frozen. The player never reads it — but its Public items are what Bill finds when he picks up the Oregon Clarion, hears at the depot, or reads on a posted notice, and its Rumor items are what reaches him secondhand. Reveal only through those channels, in play.

This is the one secret file meant to change. Unlike the canon and the other truth files, the DM advances this one at session close. Discipline:

- Most sessions, little moves. A line in the paper, a rumor, a price drift. Big developments are rare; breakthroughs rarer still.
- Every change has a stated cause. Nothing happens because the plot wants it; it happens because someone with motive acted, or a young institution reacted, or a pressure already in canon played out. Record the cause in the update log.
- It defers to canon and to the other truth files. It never overrides game_bible.md, and it never resolves or accelerates the central mystery — on the cell's true nature it defers entirely to hollow_reach_truth_v3.md, and on fixed minor-mystery answers to mystery_anchors_v1.md. It tracks outward footprints, not secrets earned in play.
- Voice: the DM tracking is plain. Sample Clarion lines are in the paper's period voice (the in-character-document exception), the same register as the Day-9 wire article.
- Visibility tags: Public (printed/posted, Bill can read it) · Rumor (secondhand, uncertain) · Secret (DM-only; surfaces only if play digs it out).

## STANDING FEATURE — LOCAL DELVE ECONOMY, THE GUILD HALL, & TEAM RECRUITMENT (added S21; expanded S22; market economics added S23)
The rush + the region's strangeness drive a busy LOCAL DELVE economy: **Tier-1 / Foundation holes are relatively plentiful**, surfacing and closing on their own clocks — treat a modest rotating roster of nearby low-tier delves as ALWAYS available background. Higher-tier holes are rarer, contested, and access-controlled (cf. Hollow Reach). **Team-recruitment notices are a constant, churning feature** of the guild hall and cage-yard boards: crews short a body, solos seeking parties, parties posting for a role (a healer, a front-line, a "reader"/surveyor, a tinker). This is Bill's organic on-ramp to leveling and to a CREW — he has no fighting core and the fiction has flagged he needs one; his rare engineer/"reader"/maker value makes him a sought non-combat asset even before the maker-core's constructs come online. Joining/forming a crew raises visibility and ties his alias to others — opportunity AND exposure; play both.

**THE ARGENT HALL — facilities (canon, expanded S22).** The New Abilene hall is a proper working guild hall with: the **cage** (Hessler's counter — registration, the buy book, member services, broken study-stock); the **contract board** (the rotating menu of postings, tier/grade marked); the **AUCTION HOUSE** (a dedicated room — **weekly Tuesday-morning auctions** of consigned + hall lots: cores, materials, orbs, infused gear, occasional tomes/relics, competitive bidding before eastern buyers' agents, **believed prices**, assayed lots drawing real money and unassayed lots drawing lowball/suspicion, consignors paying the hall a cut — PLUS a **daily direct buy/sell counter** every day for over-the-counter floor trade in common utility orbs/materials/leftovers; the good/combat/high-level goods move on Tuesday or ship east, which is why the daily shelf is thin); and the **CRAFTING FORGE** (a forge/bench-works **runically warded, Warding domain, against fire spreading** — the sanctioned, licensed site for essence-craft west, where the rune-maker road, the maker-core binding, and certified crafting have a legitimate warded home; working there means working where the guild can see you, so for anything truly private Bill's planned leased workshop serves instead). The hall is "thin on essence work this far west" and "would rather have a maker" (Hessler) — a standing reason the guild wants Bill on the maker road, and a hook for the Pearce partnership's guild leg (Doss would hear Pearce back on a payroll). The great forge-core is NOT auction/floor stock — it is a one-buyer / off-the-book item.

**THE AUCTION MARKET — believed-price economics (CANON, observed live Day 23).** The Tuesday auction is where the real delve market is made, and Day 23 established its behavior as standing canon:
- **The assay spread is most of the money.** Assayed/certified goods trade near the buy-book floor *confidently* (a known quantity trades tight and fair); identical-looking UNASSAYED goods trade at roughly a **third to a half**, grudgingly (buyers price in the risk, which eats the value). The struck assay-mark is worth far more than the metal — the economic proof of why a town starved for a licensed assayer leaves core-money on the table, and the foundation of the Pearce assay-and-craft thesis.
- **Aspected goods carry a premium and a use** — a clean aspect draws specialist money (essence-workers, crafters) above the plain-core floor.
- **The best goods leave the territory.** Combat orbs and domain tomes make hundreds-to-thousands and are bought by **eastern buyers' agents** to ship east the hour they sell. Observed figures (anchors, scale to fiction): **Lv.3 combat orb ≈ 525**; **certified Warding domain tome ≈ 2,200**; Lv.1 utility orbs ~tens (a pair ~60–100); **Lv.2 utility Passive ~150–220**; niche/common cores ~30–60 (aspected at the top of band or above); **unassayed unknowns ≈ nothing** (the room can't price what it can't read — which is the one place Bill's Forceread gives him an edge no buyer in the room has). This is *why* a western delver short a fighting Skill has no cheap road to one: the fighting orbs don't stay west.
- **Licensed assay FEES (Day-23 anchor):** a dungeon-licensed read on a guardian core runs ~tens of cr (a county-off licensed assayer would charge higher with a ~6-week backlog) — fee work that *creates* believed price, the partnership's revenue logic in miniature.
- **The daily counter** is thin by contrast (common utility orbs/materials/leftovers) because the good goods wait for Tuesday or ship east.
