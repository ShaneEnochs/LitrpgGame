## 1. ASPECTED vs UNASPECTED ESSENCE
- **Unaspected essence** is the default ambient essence of the world and of most delves — neutral, the stuff a core cultivates and a delver's mana runs on. Most of what Bill has absorbed (forge-shaft, ambient) is unaspected, which is why **his mana is neutral** (confirmed in play S23: a ward run on his plain mana comes out plain; to make an *aspected* effect he must aspect the fuel first — §2).
- **Aspected essence** carries a TYPE, from aspected SOURCES: a fire golem / fire-golem delve yields **fire essence**; established aspects include (non-exhaustive) **earth, fire, electric/storm, poison, beast, ghost** — list open (water, ice, decay, light, etc. as sources appear).
- Aspected essence is denser/specialized and does specialized work; unaspected is flexible but generic. A monster's nature usually sets its aspect; a delve's character sets its ambient aspect.
- **An aspect, from the inside (S23, Bill's Forceread read):** an aspect is a **disciplined fold in essence** — a structured bias in otherwise-neutral fuel. Reading the fold is how Bill learns to *impose* it (§2); it is also why aspected essence converts to mana *worse* than neutral (§4b — converting it back to generic mana throws the fold away).

## 2. ASPECT-FILTERING (a System-Engineer / cultivator faculty — **EXERCISED S23**)
- A skilled cultivator can build internal **filters** that aspect raw unaspected essence by running it through, converting it toward a usable aspected state. The filter is built by reading an aspect's *fold* off a specimen (a clean aspected core) and then shaping a structure that imposes that fold on neutral essence/mana run **against the grain** of the fold, pass after pass.
- **Conversion is LOSSY and takes practice** — the balance feature. Early on, essence must run MULTIPLE PASSES to reach usable aspected state, losing some each pass; yield improves with skill (a master loses little). Aspecting is never free alchemy.
- Bill (Forceread/Schematic) reads and DESIGNS his own filters far better than a feel-only cultivator — expect him to reach good yields unusually fast — but he still pays the practice/loss tax; it just shrinks quicker for him than for anyone else.
- A filter aspects toward ONE type; Bill can build/learn more over time. Having an aspect's essence on hand (from a source) eases building that aspect's filter — and **building the first filter teaches the concept**, so every later filter (any aspect, given a specimen) comes far faster.
- **[S23 — FIRST FILTER BUILT.]** Bill built a **first-generation EARTH-aspect filter**. Method: he meditated/cultivated to top his pool (passive conversion, §4b), broke a clean **earth-aspected niche core down as a TEMPLATE** (read the earth-fold against a neutral thread), and ran his neutral mana against the grain, pass after lossy pass, Schematic keeping each correction. **Cost:** the earth core was **spent WHOLE** as the template (a one-time template-consumption; it is now a dead stone), plus a hard draw on his pool. **Result:** a working but **LOSSY** earth filter (several units neutral in per usable unit earth out — a master's would lose little) that aspects his neutral mana toward earth. He grasped *aspect = a disciplined fold* and *filtering = running neutral against the grain* — the conceptual key, so the next filter (any aspect) will be far faster.
  - **Open wants this creates (live):** he has **no earth essence left** (the specimen was consumed) — the filter works but has nothing to chew until he gets more earth-aspected essence (a source: an earth delve/monster, a banked earth core). And an **earth-aspected effect is now buildable** — e.g. the drafted Standing Ward run through the filter for a denser/harder ward — pending creation mana, a seat/slot, and earth essence to feed the filter.

## 3. BANKED ESSENCE (the hard rule)
- Essence used to make a CORE or power essence-touched work must be **BANKED**, NOT drawn from one's own cultivated primary core.
- **Why:** essence compacted into the primary core is locked into the cultivator's own power (gates/Intellect). It can't be withdrawn to spend without un-making the cultivator. Banked essence sits in a SEPARATE, withdrawable reservoir. (This is the same rule that governs conversion's source restriction — §4b: you spend from your stores, never from your self.)
- **Where Bill banks:** primarily his **MAKER-CORE** (the bound forge-core — a withdrawable non-primary reservoir; see S21 maker-core canon — still inert/unbound as of S23, → Pearce Day 24 for the ID), plus ordinary external storage (essence vessels, held monster cores). Banked = withdrawable; primary-core = not.
- Keeps the primary-core gate economy sacred: cultivating yourself and banking for craft are DIFFERENT pools with different rules.
- **[S23 note]** Bill's held monster cores (the unread-core sack, the two niche cores, the gatekeeper core if not sold) are banked-essence candidates with **dual-then-triple value**: SELL (assayed, near floor), BANK for craft/constructs (maker-core fuel), or — once he learns active conversion (§4b) — **convert as field fuel for mana** (lossy). A core in the haversack is now potentially three things.

## 4. EXPELLING CORES & ESSENCE-TOUCHED CRAFTING
- Banked essence can be **expelled in the shape of a core**, like the cores that power monsters/constructs.
- Such cores **power essence-touched things**: enchanted armor, weapons, machines, fixtures — a core seats into an item like a construct's heart seats into cladding.
- **Aspect matters for efficiency.** The RIGHT aspect makes an essence-touched device markedly more efficient/powerful. Canon example: a **soul forge powered by FIRE essence** runs hotter, more efficient, more powerful than one fed unaspected essence. Matching aspect to function is the crafter's art.
- Bill's class (maker/fabrication/essence-craft) is built for this loop: study a thing → bank essence → aspect if needed (§2) → expel a core → power/enchant the made thing.
- **[S23 — the non-rune road clarified.]** Reading a rune-bound enchantment (the auction breastplate ward) confirmed: **runes anchor a weave into DEAD metal** — they are how you give a powered effect to a thing with **no core of its own**. Everything Bill can currently do lives in *his own core*, so until he walks the rune road (years out; the inscription license at Saint Orison) he **carries every weave himself**. The **essence-touched / expelled-core path above is his non-rune road to powered GEAR** — a seated essence-core does for an item what a rune-anchored weave does, without the inscription license. The rune road and the maker/essence-touched road are two different ways to put power into an object Bill doesn't have to hold.

## 4b. CONVERTING ESSENCE → MANA (cross-reference — parent rule in `game_bible.md` §13)
*Authored S23, player-directed. This section is the working cross-reference; the canonical statement is in `game_bible.md` §13. Resolves the essence-vs-mana relationship for play: **essence = raw fuel, mana = refined spendable pool, conversion = the refining step.***
- **Passive conversion — universal, slow, free.** Every awakened core continuously refines ambient/banked essence into mana on its own. This *is* ordinary mana regeneration — faster in strong essence, slow or stalled where essence is thin. No skill required. (Bill tops his pool this way when he meditates/cultivates before bench work — e.g. before building the earth filter, §2.)
- **Active conversion — deliberate, fast, TRAINED.** A learned cultivation technique that refills the pool far faster than passive regen (moments, not hours). It must be learned and practiced; **bare-handed** active conversion (drawing banked essence into mana by will) is the harder, later skill. The common on-ramp is an **essence stone**, which does the refining work for the user (why field casters/delvers carry them).
- **Hard limits (these preserve every locked growth rule):**
  - **Lossy** — never one-for-one. **Aspected essence converts WORSE than neutral** (converting a specialized fold back to generic mana throws the specialization away — §1). Neutral banked essence is the efficient mana fuel; save aspected essence for aspected *work* (§2/§4), not for conversion.
  - **Refuels, never grows** — conversion fills the pool faster but **never raises the Intellect-and-gate-set ceiling**. Same as the standing stone rule (stones refuel a core, never grow it; forcing growth with raw essence still risks rupture). Fill the battery faster; do not make it bigger.
  - **Source = banked or ambient essence, NEVER the primary core** — the §3 rule, restated: you refuel mana from your stores, not from your self. Primary-core essence is locked into your power and can't be drawn back out to spend.
- **Bill's status (S23 → updated S26):** he has **NOT** learned active conversion — but as of **Day 26 its MECHANISM is UNDERSTOOD** (no longer a black box). Reading a person cultivating actively for the first time (Pearce, who does it POORLY — the bad execution exposed the seam a clean one would hide), Bill resolved the structure: **intake → refine → meter**, and the **gate is the CONTINUOUS OPEN DRAW** — Pearce sips-refines-sips (start-stop), where the fast/clean version holds one unbroken intake while the refine runs. So Bill now owns the *shape* and the *named gate* but still **cannot DO it**: this is a trained body-cultivation reflex (§13 parent rule), not a thing his System can hand him — exactly the filtering/weave-copying pattern (understanding precedes, but does not grant, the trained skill). His Forceread/Schematic let him *reason into* it faster than a feel-only cultivator (he's already past the conceptual wall most never articulate), but the drill is still owed. **The master class is Julie** (a Platinum crewmate who carries essence-stones and does it well) — the Saturday delve is where he can watch a clean execution under load and begin to drill it. Once learned, his **banked monster cores become field fuel for mana** (lossy), and an essence stone would refuel him directly in a pinch. Until then, he runs on passive regen and rest. *(Reading a living, cultivating core at depth is expensive — the Day-26 person-read BOTTOMED his pool: a useful in-world ceiling on the faculty.)*

## 4c. THE FILTER MODEL — CONVERSION AS FILTRATION, MEASURED IN EFFICIENCY (player-authored canon, Day 27)

*This section makes the §4b conversion rule mechanically exact and unifies it with the aspect-filtering art (§2). Parent rule stays `game_bible.md` §13 (lossy, never raises the ceiling, banked/ambient source only); this extends it with the model and the numbers. Authored S27 in the Emberwaste, locked with the player.*

**The core idea: every essence→mana conversion runs through a FILTER, and "active conversion" is the act of improving the one filter you were born with.**

Everyone has an **innate, unaspected filter** — the thing their core does to turn essence into mana. A normal cultivator can never *see* it (the window doesn't render it; the body just does it). What a cultivator experiences as "learning active conversion" is, mechanically, **feel-tuning that innate filter upward** — they get better at refueling without ever knowing they're improving an apparatus. **Bill's anomaly is sight:** his System lets him *see* a filter as a constructed object, **tune it deliberately**, **build additional ones**, and **aspect them** (§2). Same act — improving a filter — done with eyes open instead of blind.

**Efficiency = % of essence retained as mana** (100% − % lost). The scale, anchored to the established numbers (pool max 210; common core value ~30-60 cr; Platinum boss core ~700-950 cr):

| Filter | Efficiency (retained) | Who / how |
|---|---|---|
| **Innate, untrained** | **~50%** | Everyone at awakening. Pure passive baseline; this *is* ordinary regen and the floor of active conversion. |
| **Innate, feel-tuned (= "active conversion")** | **~55–85%** | A normal cultivator improving by practice, blind, never seeing it's a filter. The continuous open draw (§4b gate) is the tuning condition — a smooth held flow tunes; start-stop can't (why Pearce's sip-refine-sip caps him low). **There is a feel-ceiling: most plateau; none reach lossless blind.** **JULIE ≈ 75%** — a skilled active-converter who's feel-tuned well past innate. |
| **Essence stone** | **~65–70%** | A pre-built clean *external* filter; refuels the pool directly, which is why field casters/delvers carry them. Better than untrained-innate, below a master's tuned filter. |
| **Bill's first-gen BUILT filter** | **~30% (70% lost)** | Crude — matches the earth-filter's "several units in per usable unit out" (§2). But it is *visible and tunable*, and it is a **second** filter, not his innate one. |
| **Bill's tuned filter (mastery)** | **~95%+** | Deliberate sighted tuning blows past the blind feel-ceiling. **Eventually out-converts any normal cultivator** (who is stuck near their innate plateau). Earned: lots of bench work per filter, build-defining, dead-on-thesis (the engineer's *apparatus* beats a born gift, at the cost of having to build and grind each one). |
| **Aspected filter (same craftsmanship)** | a notch below its unaspected equal | Imposing/preserving a fold is friction; an aspect-filter loses a bit more than a neutral one of equal skill. (And converting *aspected essence* to *generic mana* is worse still — the §4b rule — because the fold is thrown away.) |

**ESSENCE measured in mana-equivalent units (ME)** = how much mana a source would yield at a hypothetical 100% filter. Rough scale, tracking core value:
- **Common core (~30-60 cr):** ~40-80 ME
- **Uncommon core (boss-dense / ~140-300 cr):** ~150-300 ME
- **Platinum boss core (~700-950 cr):** ~400-600 ME — *a single boss core is more than a full pool's worth of fuel.*

**Worked example (the Ash-Maw boss core, ~500 ME):** through Bill's *innate* 50% → ~250 mana (already more than a full 210 pool); through a crude built filter at 30% → ~150; through a tuned 95% filter → ~475. The progression is visible and worth chasing — and it's also why Bill usually **banks** an aspected boss core (for the binding/craft) rather than burning it for mana: the value is higher banked, and aspected→generic conversion is lossy anyway.

**Bill's status (Day 27):** he has the **innate ~50% filter** like everyone (his passive regen). He **cannot yet actively convert** — he hasn't learned to hold the continuous draw that tunes the innate filter, and he can't yet *build* a fuel-filter for banked cores. **Julie (≈75%, sighted-by-feel) is his master class:** watching her hold the draw under load is where he learns the tuning act — and his edge is that he'll be able to *see* what she does blind, then tune deliberately past her plateau and build dedicated filters besides. Her 75% is both his lesson and his proof that the innate filter *moves*.

**Filters Bill can eventually hold (a growing rack, each a build):** the innate unaspected one (tunable); the earth-aspect filter (§2, first-gen, crude); future aspect-filters per specimen; and **weave-grammar filters** — see §5 / the comprehension-not-affinity rule in `system_engineer_truth_v1.md` — which condition his mana into the *grammar a borrowed weave needs to run*, the apparatus that substitutes for affinity.

## 5. READING & REPLICATING WEAVES (Bill's signature arc — paced, NOT brake-gated)
*This is potent and it is meant to be one of the things that makes Bill exceptional even among the exceptional. The rungs are PACING and FLAVOR — milestones to feel earned — not brakes. Bill moves through them faster and further than anyone else would, because reverse-engineering essence-structure is literally what he is. Let him be a special snowflake among special snowflakes.*
- **Rung 1 — SEE:** Bill perceives that a **weave** exists in an ability being used or an essence-touched item, and reads its shape (Forceread/Schematic applied to essence-structure, not just physical structure). Available EARLY and comes naturally; rough only on genuinely alien/high-tier weaves. *(S23: read live across many auction examples — a breathing sustained ward, a seated-idle active, an Inspect/reading weave, Movement and Passive weaves seating, a Lv.3 combat orb's staged force-dump.)*
- **Rung 2 — STORE:** Bill captures/holds a read weave (Schematic-held), as he held the suppression weave and his own optimal weave. Comes readily for him; only the most complex weaves make him work for it.
- **Rung 3 — REPLICATE:** Bill reproduces a stored weave. Lossy/imperfect at the very top of his reach; for anything near his level he reproduces it cleanly and sooner than expected. Far-above-tier weaves take real effort but are NOT walled off forever. *(S23: the **Standing Ward** — built from UNDERSTANDING a read ward, drafted clean, unslotted — is replicate-class work done by comprehension rather than rote copy, the engineer's way.)*
- **Rung 4 — UPGRADE (his edge):** Bill re-engineers a weave into a more efficient / more powerful / upgraded version — the thing almost no one else can do at all. Costs study + materials + sometimes levels per weave/item, but this is a CAPABILITY he genuinely has and uses, not a someday-maybe. This is the through-line: the engineer who reverse-engineers — and improves — magic itself.
- **Weave families (read confirmed across S22–S23):** weaves share a **grammar** but split into families — **converter** (suppression, the magnetism-cut: intake→fold→output-cut), **movement** (Surefoot: a different grammar), and **reading/Inspect** (reach-out-and-sample-back, returns *information* not force — **Forceread belongs to this family**, confirmed S23 reading Pearce's licensed Inspect). A weave splices most cleanly *within* a family; cross-family splices are the harder art (the "new Skill" project). **Spells/tomes are a fourth thing** — a *method/score* (spell-as-method, tome-as-teaching, domain-as-grammar), not a held weave; most of a tome's deep structure reads thin to a non-caster (no affinity; rune/domain vocabulary blanks), though the shape comes through (S23, the Warding tome).
- **Reading vs probing (S23 detectability rule):** a **soft, wide, passive** read (a cone, ambient) returns information and is nearly undetectable — even an assayer (the man likeliest to feel a probe) felt nothing off Bill's soft-cone Forceread. A hard, narrow **probe** is what another cultivator can catch. Bill's cover doctrine rides this line: run the soft read, let the leaky suppression weave be the noise that explains away any whisper of it (see `campaign_state.md` / dm-notes — the leak-as-alibi doctrine).
- **Guardrail (light):** don't let a single afternoon hand Bill a stranger's entire spellbook for free; treat notable weaves as quick projects, not zero-cost auto-acquires. But err toward letting him SUCCEED — the fantasy is competence, not gatekeeping. DM paces with a light hand.

## 8. RARITY GRADE — HOW CORES & MATERIALS ARE GRADED (player-authored canon, Day 25)
A standing classification for the *quality/scarcity* of a core or dungeon material, distinct from a dungeon's tier/grade (which rates *sites*, `game_bible.md` §12) and from a Skill orb's level (1–5). **Assayers grade on this scale from here on** — a licensed read returns **aspect + rarity grade** at whatever depth the reader's skill allows.

**The grades:**
- **Common** — the overwhelming majority of cores and materials. Ordinary banking fuel, floor-priced. (A damaged/cracked core reads *Common, damaged* — at or below floor.)
- **Uncommon** — a real, notable step up; draws a premium above floor (the gatekeeper core sits here — Pearce's believed-price ran above a factor's stamp).
- **Rare** — genuinely scarce; specialist money.
- **Epic · Legendary · Mythic** — exceptional, the stuff of stories and one-buyer deals; vanishingly seldom seen on a frontier counter.

**UNIQUE — a separate grade, off the ladder, for GROWTH ITEMS (and genuine one-of-ones).** A Unique item has **no reference class** — there is only the one, keyed to a single delver — so it **cannot be fully graded.** A licensed read returns its **family/category, not its contents.** *This is the mechanical reason the great forge-core "wouldn't close" to Pearce's read (Day 24): he could confirm it a class-resonant growth item of the binding family — Unique grade — and name the category of its threshold, but not the number, because there is nothing to grade it against.* Reading a Unique item to its contents needs either a far higher license (Saint Orison) or a reader native to the item's own grammar (Bill's Forceread, which out-resolves any western license on this class — `system_engineer_truth_v1.md`).

**Aspect** rides alongside the grade: *unaspected*, or the named aspect (earth, fire, electric/storm, poison, beast, ghost, … list open). A *made/worked* resonance (e.g. the great core's **forge-resonant** quality, or the frame-metal's "made stock") is noted as such rather than as a clean elemental aspect.

**Skill orbs keep their LEVEL (1–5)** as their grade — the rarity scale is for cores and materials, not orbs. (So: "a Lv.3 combat orb," but "an Uncommon earth core.")
