# MONSTER CATEGORIES — ABILITIES & NATURE (v1)

*Worldbuilding reference for the Frontier Fantasy Solo RPG. Consistent with `game_bible.md` (the canon and tiebreaker, including its voice rules). This is the source layer beneath the orb and aura catalogs — the physiology and behavior of what delvers fight, and where loot comes from.*

*These describe the **shape of a category**, not fixed creatures. Per canon, individual dungeons deviate and strength varies enormously within a category — a Beast can be a rat or a Titan. Treat each entry as a palette for building a specific monster, not a stat block to drop in. Categories are not priced or licensed; they are not owned, they are encountered.*

*Format per entry: nature · common abilities · danger · drops.*

---

## Beast

The largest and most ordinary category — creatures that are recognizably animals, however changed. Most of what a frontier delver kills is a Beast, and most awakenings happen on one. They run from vermin to apex predators to the rare Beast that grew into a Titan.

**Nature.** Beasts behave like animals: they hunt, flee, defend territory, travel in packs or alone according to kind. They are the most predictable category and the most studied, which is why green delvers are sent against them. A Beast wants to eat, escape, or protect something.

**Common abilities.** Physical traits rather than uncanny effects — heightened senses, speed, strength, natural weapons, thick hide, venom, climbing, burrowing, leaping. Stronger Beasts push these to preternatural degree: a pack-hunter that coordinates past animal capacity, a predator whose lunge no unawakened eye can follow, a hide that turns a blade. The sensory and movement orbs nearly all trace to Beasts.

**Danger.** Speed and numbers more than tricks. A Beast rarely does anything a delver can't understand — it bites, charges, runs them down — but faster and harder than a natural animal. Pack Beasts kill careless crews by flanking.

**Drops.** The broadest and most reliable loot in the world. Sensory, movement, and physical-augment orbs; hide, bone, fang, and sinew a cultivator-smith can work; essence stones in stronger specimens.

---

## Aberration

Creatures that do not resemble any natural animal — wrong shapes, extra parts, bodies that should not function. The category for everything that looks like it came from nowhere on Earth. Less common than Beasts and far less understood.

**Nature.** Aberrations follow logic of their own. Some hunt, some lure, some seem to exist only to persist. They are unpredictable in behavior because their drives don't map to animal need, and a delver cannot assume an Aberration wants to eat or flee.

**Common abilities.** The uncanny effects — telekinesis, distortion of sense or perception, mimicry, paralytic and caustic secretions, limbs and senses no animal has. Aberrations are where many of the stranger orbs originate: telekinetic grip, the glamour and mimicry abilities, perception tricks. Some carry weak fields that foul cultivation nearby.

**Danger.** They do things a delver doesn't expect and can't read. An Aberration that distorts what you see, grabs you from across a room, or wears the shape of something harmless is dangerous out of proportion to its strength, because the delver fights it wrong. The category that kills experienced crews who grew comfortable.

**Drops.** Strange, valuable orbs — telekinetic, sensory-distortion, mimicry — and materials with uses rune-workers prize and don't explain. Aberration loot sells high and is poorly understood.

---

## Construct

Made things, animate without life — assemblies of stone, metal, wood, bone, or stranger material, moving with purpose but no breath. Some are clearly built; some grew into shape no one designed.

**Nature.** Constructs do not tire, fear, bleed, or reconsider. They follow a pattern — guard a place, repeat a task, destroy intruders — with no instinct for self-preservation and no morale to break. A Construct fights until it stops working.

**Common abilities.** Tirelessness, immunity to pain, fear, poison, and bleeding; great durability; often great strength or relentless repetition. Many cannot be reasoned with, lured, or frightened. The defensive and endurance traits — hardened body, immunity to fatigue — trace partly to Constructs.

**Danger.** They never stop and never make a frightened mistake. A Construct that is slower and weaker than a delver still wins if the delver tires first, and it cannot be bluffed, panicked, or made to flee. Brute attrition with no exploitable mind.

**Drops.** Strange metals and worked materials beyond Earth smithing — the single best source of smithing stock in the world — along with durability and endurance orbs. The body itself is often the prize.

---

## Elemental

Embodiments of a force or substance — fire, stone, water, wind, lightning, ice — given form and will. Bodies made of the thing they are.

**Nature.** An Elemental is its element. It behaves according to what it's made of: fire spreads and consumes, stone endures and crushes, water floods and drowns. Predictable in temperament once a delver knows the element, alien in mind.

**Common abilities.** Direct command of their element — hurling, shaping, becoming it. Resistance or immunity to their own force and acute vulnerability to its opposite. Many can partly dissolve into their element to take a blow, then reform. The environmental-ward orbs (heat, cold) and elemental effects trace to this category.

**Danger.** Reach and area. An Elemental fights with its element at a distance and shrugs off the very thing a delver might bring against it — fire does nothing to a fire Elemental. The lethal one is fought with the wrong tool. The right opposite turns a deadly fight easy.

**Drops.** Potent essence stones, element-attuned materials, and elemental orbs and tome components. Their cores are prized by casters and rune-workers.

---

## Ooze

Bodies without fixed shape — acid, slime, devouring jelly, flowing matter. Among the simplest in mind and most dangerous to the careless.

**Nature.** An Ooze flows, engulfs, and digests. It is slow, mindless or nearly so, and rarely hunts in any clever way; it occupies space and ruins what enters it. Easy to avoid, terrible to touch.

**Common abilities.** Corrosion — destroying gear, flesh, blade, and rope on contact. Engulfing and trapping. Splitting when struck rather than dying, so a poorly judged blow makes two problems. Many cannot be cut or pierced to any effect, having nothing vital to hit. Immunity to many physical attacks.

**Danger.** They wreck equipment and punish the wrong attack. An Ooze that a delver could step around will dissolve a sword arm if struck wrong, and splitting Oozes overwhelm crews who keep hacking. Slow, but unforgiving of contact and of bad weapon choice.

**Drops.** Corrosive and engulfing orbs, reagents for alchemy, and rare residues. Ooze loot is valuable but ugly to harvest, since the body destroys what touches it.

---

## Plant

Rooted or vine-borne life — grasping growths, snares, seeding things, bodies of wood and thorn and sap. Often stationary, often mistaken for terrain.

**Nature.** Plants wait. Most do not pursue; they anchor, lure, and grasp what comes near, then feed slowly. A Plant is a hazard of place more than a hunter, which makes it deadly to the inattentive who walk into reach.

**Common abilities.** Grasping vines and snares, entangling and crushing; thorns and spines; spores, pollen, and sap that poison, sedate, or paralyze; regeneration from root or cutting. Some lure with scent or false fruit. The adhesion and grip traits owe something to climbing Plants.

**Danger.** Ambush by stillness. A Plant kills the delver who didn't recognize it as alive until grasped, then holds them while it feeds or while worse arrives. Hard to kill by halves — cut a Plant and it may regrow from what's left.

**Drops.** Alchemical reagents above all — sedatives, poisons, restoratives — plus entangling and regenerative orbs and workable ghostwood-like materials. The most valuable category for a chemist.

---

## Fungal

Spore-borne life — molds, growths, networks, the things that spread and rot and connect. Related to Plants in stillness but distinct in how they propagate and in what they do to the living.

**Nature.** Fungal monsters spread. Many are less a single creature than a connected colony, sharing awareness across a site, growing toward warmth and flesh. They corrupt and convert — turning the dead, and sometimes the living, into more of themselves.

**Common abilities.** Spore clouds that sicken, sedate, hallucinate, or take root in a breathing victim; rapid spread and regrowth; colony awareness that makes a whole site one alert organism; reanimation or puppeting of corpses claimed by the growth. The decay and colony-restorative effects trace here.

**Danger.** The air itself. Fungal sites poison the lungs before a blow is struck, and a crew that breathes wrong is fighting impaired or worse. Killing the body in front of you does little when the real organism is the whole room, and the dead may rise wearing the growth.

**Drops.** Powerful and dangerous reagents, colony and restorative orbs, and curatives derived from the same spores that sicken. Fungal loot is prized by alchemists and feared by everyone who harvests it.

---

## Spirit

The incorporeal — things of essence without solid body, haunting, hostile, hard to touch. The first of the three categories the Church built its apparatus around.

**Nature.** Spirits are bound to a place, a feeling, or an old death, and act out of that binding — grief, rage, hunger, watchfulness. Many cannot leave their haunt. They are not always immediately hostile, but they are always wrong to be near.

**Common abilities.** Intangibility — passing through solid matter, ignored by ordinary weapons; chilling, draining touch; manipulation of fear and perception; possession or influence of the living and the unawakened especially. The phasing and dread effects trace to Spirits.

**Danger.** They can't be fought with ordinary steel. A Spirit that walks through a wall and drains warmth from a man's chest is invulnerable to a delver without the means to touch the incorporeal, and it works on the mind — fear, illusion, possession — more than the body. The unawakened are far more vulnerable to a Spirit than the awakened.

**Drops.** Phasing, fear, and perception orbs; rare essence of unusual purity; components for the consecration and binding work the Church and rune-binders practice. Spirit loot is restricted-adjacent and watched.

---

## Undead

The animate dead — bodies or remnants moving past death, sustained by essence rather than life. The second Church category, and the most reviled.

**Nature.** Undead persist against the natural order. Some are mindless and driven only to spread death; some retain cunning and will. They do not tire as the living do and feel no fear of dying, being already dead. Many strengthen in fields of decay and weaken nothing for being struck.

**Common abilities.** Tirelessness and absence of pain or fear; draining touch that takes vitality and gives it to the dead thing; resistance to harm that would kill a living body; spread, raising more dead from the freshly killed; growing stronger amid rot and decay-fields. The wither and hold-wound effects trace here.

**Danger.** They don't stop and they make more of themselves. An Undead fights past wounds that would drop a living foe, drains the strength from those it grips, and a crew that loses someone may face them again, risen. Numbers that grow during the fight.

**Drops.** Draining and endurance orbs, essence of grave-cold character, and materials the Church demands be surrendered for destruction or consecration. Undead loot is the most heavily restricted of any category.

---

## Infernal

Things of malice and fire and bargain — the category that reads as deliberately, intelligently cruel. The third Church category, and the one it treats as outright damnation.

**Nature.** Infernals are intelligent, willful, and hostile by nature, not instinct. Many can speak, tempt, deceive, and bargain; many wield fire or worse as an extension of will. They are the category that wants to corrupt as much as to kill, and the one that can negotiate.

**Common abilities.** Command of flame and destructive force; deception, temptation, and the forging of binding bargains; resistance to fire and to ordinary harm; fear and dominating presence. The fire-soul and dread-presence effects trace partly here. The most dangerous carry strong auras of terror or command.

**Danger.** Mind and fire together. An Infernal fights with destructive power and a cruel intelligence, sets traps, makes offers, turns a crew against itself, and does not break or flee as a Beast would. It is the category most likely to win by getting a delver to agree to something.

**Drops.** Fire and destruction orbs, command and dread auras, and powerful tome components — among the richest loot in the world, and among the most restricted. The Church demands oversight of any Infernal harvest, and dealing in it unlicensed is among the gravest offenses.

---

## Giant

The great — creatures of human-like or Beast-like shape grown to enormous size and strength, short of the world-ending scale of a Titan. The category of overwhelming physical force.

**Nature.** Giants are, mostly, larger versions of comprehensible things — a Beast or a humanoid grown vast. They behave with animal or near-human drives, but every act carries crushing force. Some are dull, some cunning, all enormous.

**Common abilities.** Overwhelming strength and mass; reach and area no smaller creature has; durability that turns ordinary blows; the ability to crush, throw, and break terrain. Some carry weight-fields or shaking presence. The power-strike and pressure effects trace partly to Giants.

**Danger.** Sheer force and reach. A single blow from a Giant kills, and its reach denies a delver the safe distance that works on smaller foes. It need not be clever; it needs only to land once. Crews bring it down by numbers, distance, and not being where it strikes.

**Drops.** Large quantities of workable material and potent essence stones; strength, durability, and pressure orbs. A Giant's body is a windfall, if the crew survives to harvest it.

---

## Titan

The apex — the rarest and most catastrophic category, creatures whose strength approaches the top of the known scale. The Titan that defines the high end of the attribute scale took dozens of delvers to kill, and killed many of them first. No frontier crew is meant to face one.

**Nature.** A Titan is a localized catastrophe with a will. It is not a larger Giant but a creature operating at a scale the institutions can barely measure — strong enough that its presence alone reshapes a region's danger. Most who encounter one die; most that exist have never been beaten.

**Common abilities.** Everything the lesser categories do, at a scale that breaks the usual rules — force that levels ground, resistance that shrugs off coordinated assault, auras that crush or terrify across a battlefield, regeneration or endurance that outlasts an army. A Titan may combine traits from several categories at once. The world-ending effects in legend trace here.

**Danger.** It is the danger. A Titan cannot be fought by a person, only by a host, and even then at terrible cost. There is no frontier-tier engagement with a Titan that is not a disaster; the category exists at the edge of the catalog to mark the ceiling, not to be met in ordinary play.

**Drops.** The stuff of legend — orbs, auras, materials, and essence at the apex of every scale. A single Titan's body has founded fortunes and guilds. But Titan loot is national-interest matter, claimed by the great powers and the Church, and never an ordinary delver's to sell.

## ⚙ STAT BLOCKS — THE MECHANICAL LAYER (DM-side, precise tracking — S30)

*The category entries above are the **palette** — nature, abilities, drops. This section is how a specific creature gets the **hidden numbers** that make precise combat resolution consistent (`game_bible.md` §3, §8). These numbers are **DM-side and never surfaced to the player as numbers**: per the visibility split, the player sees an enemy's attack roll vs their own AC and whether their effect beat a DC, but an enemy's HP, AC, save bonuses, and damage taken are described in words, never numbered. A specific site builds its creatures' blocks (e.g. the Ashen Descent's Floor-1 blocks); this section is the template and the per-category baselines they're built from.*

**The stat-block line:**
> **Name** (Category). HP · AC · Attack (+to-hit / damage dice+mod, type) · Saves (Fort / Ref / Will, flat bonuses) · special (immunities, weakness seam, on-death, fight-pattern).

**How the numbers relate to the player scale** (player AC 11–24, HP 40–150, attack ~+5/+6, save DCs 14–19, damage ~1d8–1d12+mod):
- **AC** is the number a crew member's `1d20 + attack mod` must meet. Mob AC ~15–18 = hittable by a strong attacker (Jason +5, Bill +2 on aim plus the gun), missable by a weak one. Boss AC ~20–22.
- **HP** sets how many solid hits it takes. Mob HP scaled so 1–3 crew hits drop it (the threat is numbers + the creature's bite, not durability); elite/boss HP much higher (a multi-round focus).
- **Attack +to-hit** is the `1d20 + bonus` the creature rolls vs the crew member's AC; **damage** is its dice + mod applied to that member's HP (shown player-side as the member's HP change).
- **Saves** are the `1d20 + bonus` the creature rolls vs a crew member's spell DC (Julie DC 18–19, Charles DC 18, Myles DC 14). High Fort = soaks Life/poison/raw force; high Ref = dodges areas/aimed bursts; high Will = resists Hex/charm/fear. A mindless Construct/Undead has a moot Will (immune to mind effects entirely).

**Per-category baselines** (a starting shape; a specific creature deviates by its strength and the site's tier):
- **Beast** — moderate AC, low–moderate HP, high single-attack damage or pack tactics; high Ref on fast ones; no special immunities beyond toughness. Aspected hides break at the glowing seam (weakness).
- **Construct** — high AC and HP, immune to mind/fear/poison/bleed (Will moot), never tires; weakness = precise hits on seams/joints; can't be Hexed/charmed/feared.
- **Elemental** — resists/immune to its own element, acute vulnerability to the opposite (a matching-opposite hit ignores resistance and adds bonus); may dissolve to take a blow (a "miss" that's really a phase). High Fort.
- **Ooze** — immune to piercing (often physical generally — Bill's darts near-useless), splits when struck wrong, corrodes gear on a hit; low AC, high effective HP; needs area/elemental/caustic. Low Ref/Will.
- **Plant** — entangle (Ref save or held), poison (Fort save), regenerates unless burned/area'd; fire bonus damage. Moderate everything.
- **Spirit** — **immune to ordinary steel** (only essence/aspected/Life/Arcane touches it), works on the mind (fear/illusion — high Will), drains warmth/essence on contact; low HP once something CAN hit it; high Ref (hard to pin).
- **Undead** — tireless, no pain/fear (Will moot vs morale; mind effects do nothing), draining touch, resist killing-blows; **weak to Life/holy** (a Life effect bites hardest); high Fort, low Will-as-resistance-is-moot.
- **Infernal** — **resist fire** (a fire/hellfire attack from a non-hellfire source is wasted; Bill's *hellfire* still bites because it's structured), terror/dread aura (Will save or shaken), command of flame; vulnerable to cold/water-aspect (scarce here). The Descent's natives.
- **Aberration** — telekinesis, sense-distortion, mimicry; some carry a cultivation-fouling field (raises mana costs nearby); unpredictable — read it wrong and fight it wrong. Varied saves.
- **Giant / Titan** — off the frontier scale; Titans combine categories and break the rules. Not built to a baseline — authored as set-pieces, HP/AC far past anything above.

*Build a site's creatures into this format in the site's own file (the Ashen Descent's Floor-1 blocks are the worked example). Keep them DM-side; narrate enemy state in words.*
