## The throughline

Five days of building turned into a licensed man with a named enterprise. Bill took the provisional guild license, ran the Hollow Reach baseline he'd been angling toward, gave his father's pride a door to walk through, and — on the day the land grab around the dungeon stopped being patient — finally let the awakened world he'd been working at the edge of come onstage.

## Licensed, and the assay on record

Bill paid the license fee by Pickering's sealed draft and took the provisional license in person at the guild cage. The mandatory assay, run on the Bureau field instrument and stamped into the ledger, read **"Core: closed. Mana capacity: nil. Plain human, no impairment to license."** It is a record, not a filter — a baseline so any later change is what gets noticed. A man assayed closed who later turns up awakened triggers re-assay and registration. The clerk's parting note: *"You held stiller than most."* (This baseline is load-bearing for the Day-21 awakening.) Weapons registered, peace-bonded on premises only.

## The baseline — the permanent "before"

On Day 7 (Sunday) Bill ran the full surface program at Hollow Reach — Hollis driving, paid 200 cr on the return, under his deal-term condition: *"If you come walkin' back to my wagon wrong — an' I'll know wrong — I'm allowed to drive off an' tell the town."* The condition's first check **passed** ("count's right, both ways").

The findings are the only "before" that will ever exist: the surface site reads **stable — empty, but not killing**; a measurable **divergence** and a **teardrop**-shaped dead-life boundary; and a **threshold correlation** Bill has not yet reckoned with — his one timed stand at the mouth lined up with the horses' held stare and Hollis's skin-crawl in the same moment. He holds it as two timed entries that align, nothing more, and works it only in daylight by his own rule.

Then Bill and Doss deliberately **made the baseline invisible on paper.** She received the full recitation from memory (piles separate), but her office officially filed a true-but-useless receipt — *site quiet, no contact, no recovery, no further action recommended* — in the ordinary cabinet, not the locking drawer. The measurements exist nowhere in the building, only in Bill's four books and Doss's head. She named the cost: she is trading the baseline's **legal standing today for its existence tomorrow**, because the writ's reporting clause took Garlan's log inside a day. A new standing constraint follows, now governing the campaign's central operational question: once crew 4 is down the site is an active guild operation and her paper thins, so **the re-measure does not ride Sunday's permission — Bill comes to Doss first.** Crew 4 descended Tuesday (Day 9), Garlan leading; the re-measure after is the finding.

## The land assembly gets a name and a deadline

Calloway's docket map put a name on the buyer: **COULTER & REESE LAND COMPANY** — an Ohio incorporation, no local office, seven recorded instruments executed by agents under recorded powers of attorney, **all in one drafting hand**, notarized in Saint Orison. The parcels form a **broken horseshoe around the old adit claim but never touching it**, and the surrounding work is finished.

The **keystone** is that adit claim — where Hollow Reach sits — which is unpatented public ground the locators abandoned owing the county. It can't be quitclaimed off heirs; it must be taken from the county or by patent, a louder and more public act than anything done so far. They bought every quiet parcel around it and saved the loud one for last, for a window when no one's positioned to bid against them. The day they file on the adit (or the county posts a sale), the quiet window shuts, surface access ends, and it's public within the hour.

**Quill's haste-tell** (volunteered as a craftsman's grievance, the session's quietly biggest fact): the two filings recorded Monday morning were engrossed **in haste** — same good hand, suddenly hurrying — the same morning crew 4's wagons rolled NW. Five weeks of patient work, then sudden hurry. *Something changed.* Quill remains a hard player constraint: a venting friend, never a worked source; the intelligence rides in as grievance or not at all, with Calloway the paid backstop. Endangering Quill is a failure state.

## Quest — the enterprise named

Bill named the whole enterprise **Quest** — dull and hopeful on the surface, privately the Vigenère key of his father's cipher, the word that means *him*. Its arms, each individually boring: **Quest Instruments** (assay, calibration, fix-it, metal — the Pearce-building trade), **Quest Remedies** (the grandmother's medicine), **Quest Academy** (the children — the *first* purpose). Two stated purposes: lift the town's children out of poverty, and meet the shady concerns on equal footing — not by out-vaulting them but by being too rooted and dull to lean on, daylight held in reserve.

Bill filed a cheap **Quest curtain** at Calloway's (*Bill Weaver dba Quest*) to operate this month — a cover, not a separation; legally Quest is still Bill, so nothing heavy goes under it yet. Calloway's structural counsel, all load-bearing since: the eventual company **face** wants three properties — competent, trustworthy, and **unremarkable** — which is why Calloway stays *behind the wall* (drafting, the box, receiving money, never on the paper), **Pearce is the not-yet face** (his name on a reopened assay office writes itself), and the necks stay separate (Maddox holds the children's trust *alone*; the works want a different hand). The bank is years away and governed down hard — a second bank in a Combine town means war and federal eyes; not even said aloud near a Combine wire.

## The father loan

Bill sent his father the ask he'd been building toward: a ciphered eastbound wire keyed to **QUEST** — *loan, 5,000 over 12 months at 3%, signed Q* — over Calloway's advised 3,000, on his own read of Malcolm (a man who once tried to send him west with ten thousand free; five at interest is the message *I'm doing this myself, but letting you in*). A sealed letter follows by mail, carrying the warm half the cipher couldn't: it names Shaun and the empty chairs, owns the pride as a grown man's fault, and ends *"I love you, Father."* The money returns through **Calloway in trust, behind privilege, staged.** Min's carrier audit kept Bill's face off the depot (Calloway, who wires east constantly, carries it himself). **Seeded payoff (locked):** when Malcolm's money comes it will arrive *scattered* — varying amounts from different origins, all routing to Calloway — a structuring Malcolm devised unprompted, which tells Calloway something important about the man on the other end.

## The awakened world, onstage at last

The guild hall came alive — awakened delvers, crews forming, the board moving with delve work — and Bill made the deliberate choice to start being *known for the work* while keeping his face behind the firm's runner-channel. He took handheld instrument repair off the room (rebuilt a delver's gauze lamp better than new), then opened his **first essence-touched dungeon instrument** — a fouled essence-gauge for a prickly Iron crew boss, **Tamsin Bly** — working the *body only* with permission, hands visible, and honestly refusing the sensing element as past a tinker's place. Bly paid for the hour (his pride won't carry a free ride) and established standing trade: **Quest services the bodies of finer instruments for delving crews, via the firm's runner-channel**, and Bly spreads the name. Hessler mapped the delve economy (supervised day-delves on tame holes exist, found by asking the men who run them). Bill weighed and **rejected** asking Doss to take him down — wrong person, and the awakening is a deliberate life-fork, not a favor taken on the day the delving world turned shiny.

## What else moved
- **The firm.** Standing rates began; the mercantile retainer rounds started; the **foot-balm** product line set for Freight Lane (the firm's own ground), with the weeping/separation fault to be fixed before a single tin sells. **Quest Academy** took shape as Tatch advised — letters *and* paid work the same hour, Ada teaching the little ones, Bill not the face. **Winter coats** went into motion *through* the firm, Bill's name on no coat, priority ranked by age and need as marks not names, Pem approached person-to-person with no hook; the wool idea (buy raw from Dunphy, pay a depot sewing-hand) floated.
- **The Maddox trustee sit — won, with gates.** She'll sign at Calloway's Thursday morning *if the paper matches Bill's speech*: she answers to the **instrument**, not to Bill; a **named-succession clause** goes in; and the day it stops being about the children she **rules against him** the way she ruled on the shed. Tatch answered her questions himself and earned her verdict ("watched what I *did*, not what I said").
- **Pearce, first contact.** Reached and read (the assayer's pride, the bottle behind it); Bill left on the high note without following him through the door. The courtship is a *season* of patience — drink first, books never first, building last; never the landlord before the works exist. His building carries a ~340 cr three-year tax lien (Bill is the second person Calloway knows of who knows it exists) — a colder acquisition road if courtship fails.
- **Seeded/carried:** the cleanliness hypothesis (a medical idea that strengthens but can't yet prove; the microscope test deferred to the Pearce angle); the Lessard supper, Day 18 (the school's gatekeeper); Pem's compact (rules moving through Tatch); the watcher (still nothing on Bill — ten-plus days held).
