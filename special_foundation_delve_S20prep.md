## CLASSIFICATION (locked)

- **Subtype: CURRICULUM SPECIAL** (`system_cosmology_truth_v1.md` §SPECIAL DELVES #1) — a true Lattice site, advanced-placement reward. NOT a sink (Hollow Reach is the campaign's one sink — do not multiply), NOT a fault (no rigged lethality, no looping, no maiming reward). This is the genuine windfall the public category promises.
- **Ladder slot: a SPECIAL FOUNDATION delve** (`game_bible.md` §12) — single-occupancy, one person at a time; single-entry per person (arch closes to him after, win or lose); appears and vanishes on its own (six seen in the U.S.; longest observed after discovery ~a week — so it is a CLOCK, not a fixture). Its growth item is "a cut above" an ordinary Foundation item.
- **Why it's here now, causally (not a coincidence to hand-wave):** the Lattice manifests specials in response to a world's people rising and to local conditions. New Abilene's essence-substrate has been disturbed by the reclassification-driven traffic and, more to the point, sits near a sink leaking the shared root-substrate. A curriculum special surfacing in this corridor, this season, is the engine doing what it does — and it surfacing *on the road between town and the Reach* is the kind of proximity the shared substrate makes plausible. (DM: Bill cannot know any of this yet. To him and the world it is simply a rare arch that wasn't there before.)

## THE ARCH ON THE ROAD (staging for Day 21, first light, Sunday)

Bill rides/walks out under Doss's guild-survey paper, named survey to follow, on the Sunday frame matching the Day-7 baseline. Cold, hard, bright. The road has rush traffic on it now (Track 8) — but the arch appears off the road, not on the travelled line, so it reads as *his* find, not a thing a crowd is already swarming.

- **Discovery:** part-way out — past the last of the town's traffic, before the Reach ground proper — Bill's eye (Perception) catches what shouldn't be there: a short flight of worked stone steps cut down into a frost-heaved hollow a little off the trail, leading to a **sunken arch in worked stone** (`game_bible.md` §11, the flat-country form). Across it, the faint almost-transparent **blue shimmer** of a live barrier. Runes around the arch, the usual undeciphered kind. It was not here on the Day-7 baseline walk and it is not on any map. Fresh-manifested.
- **The Examine-arch window (canon, available to anyone, not an Inspect read — `game_bible.md` §12):** examining the arch returns a window listing requirements. For THIS site:
  - **TIER: FOUNDATION (SPECIAL).**
  - **OCCUPANCY: 1.**
  - **ENTRY: —** (Foundation opens the core; no Delver-Level floor. A special *can* carry a non-level condition, but this one's condition is simply Foundation's own: single-occupancy, one passage.)
  - **A line that reads as windfall** without explaining itself — the public read of a special. (The world knows specials "yield rewards a graded site never will.")
  - Bill, unawakened, gets the **world-facing version** of this window — the arch presents to anyone. He does NOT get System rendering yet (no open core). So he reads it as a delver would: a Foundation special, solo, no level gate, rumored to pay singular.
- **What the rush DOESN'T know:** nobody else is on it. If Bill leaves it and rides on, it may be found by others within its short life, or may simply close. (DM: if he passes and returns, roll the fiction — likeliest it's either gone or someone's queued on it; single-occupancy means a competitor is a wait, not a fight.)

## THE COLLISION (the real fork — surface honestly, decide nothing for him)

Bill is *committed*, today, to a **named guild survey under Doss's paper.** The arch puts three live tensions in front of him, all true, none of which the DM resolves for him:

1. **The survey vs. the hole.** A Foundation delve is not a half-hour. Going in means Doss's survey is delayed or undone while he's inside for an unknown stretch — and he just spent his town goodwill making that survey *named and urgent*. (He could also survey first and return to the arch after — but the arch's short life is the counter-pressure. Real trade-off, his to weigh.)
2. **Awakening vs. the assay clock (`system_engineer_truth_v1.md`).** Clearing this — or even a first kill inside — **opens his core**, which flips his Bureau status from the clean-specimen baseline to *awakened*, with the license wall payable within the fortnight, the instant any guild paper reflects it. He has chosen, this very day, to put his NAME on Reach paper. Awakening now stacks the single loudest personal change on top of the single loudest professional one, in the same 48 hours, in front of a cell that prizes him as a clean specimen. (Bill doesn't know the cell angle. But "awaken right as I make myself visible" is exactly the kind of timing his own discipline would flinch at.)
3. **Alone, unawakened, in a hole that still kills.** Foundation is single-occupancy by law — no Bly, no Okafor, no Dell, no supervision. Bill is a revolver (extractor now perfect, low hundreds of rounds), a knife (Melee 3, no live-resistance kill yet), Shooting 5, Engineering 7, no melee-under-fire experience, no core. **Death stays real** (`delve_progression_truth_v1.md`: a Foundation Tin can kill an unawakened engineer). This is the patient supervised-descent road's whole point being *skipped*. The on-ramp (§9) built toward a supervised first hole precisely so this wouldn't be solo.

**DM stance:** present all three plainly when he examines the arch / weighs it, in-character as things Bill himself would see. Do NOT push him in or steer him off. The player set this fork up deliberately; let it be real. If he goes, it's earned; if he doesn't, the clock on the arch is the consequence-shaped cost of caution.

## INTERIOR (if he enters — built survivable-by-competence, not safe)

A curriculum-special Foundation site, so: a genuine *lesson* shaped to the incoming man, not a meat-grinder. The Lattice tailors. Bill is a methodical engineer-alchemist with a gun, a mind, and no fighting core — so the site's challenge should reward *reading, preparation, patience, and mechanism* over raw violence, while still being able to kill a careless man.

**Form:** dry worked-stone galleries — old, clean, lattice-cut (the architecture reads *made*, regular, unlike a natural cave). One party, one path with a couple of real branches. A small site — Foundation is the shallow end — clearable in a focused stretch, not a multi-day expedition.

**Monster category: CONSTRUCTs** (`monster_categories_v1.md` — pull at run-time for specifics). Chosen deliberately:
- Constructs are *mechanisms*. They suit the Engineer: they have rules, weight, tells, failure points — Doss's "read the weight, not the eyes" lesson and Bill's "read a machine by what it can't do" instinct both apply directly, the hour she just gave him paying off immediately and diegetically.
- They are vulnerable to ordinary firearms (no resist-physical problem like oozes/spirits/elementals) — so his revolver is real, but ammunition, placement, and the thing's armored/weak geometry matter (a construct's joint, sensor, core-housing, or balance — Bill can SEE these).
- They let the first kill be a *thing*, not a person — clean awakening trigger (standard, per `system_engineer_truth_v1.md`; copy-clause/contact do NOT apply here).

**Suggested beat structure (adapt at run-time, keep it fair):**
1. **A room boss-flavored gatekeeper or lesser constructs first** — a sentinel or two that move predictably; Bill's first live kill happens here → **CORE OPENS mid-delve**, System BOOTS (see boot staging below). Give the boot its full due in the moment; he is now reading the rest of the hole with new eyes (literally — Examine, the Survey passive on the galleries).
2. **A mechanism-puzzle stretch** — the path itself is the lesson: a gallery that must be read and worked, not just fought (a counterweighted door, a sequence, a structural weakness), squarely his wheelhouse. This is where a *curriculum* special earns the name: it teaches him by being exactly the kind of problem he's built for.
3. **The dungeon boss — the win condition is defeating it** (`game_bible.md` §11). A single larger Construct — a **Warden**-type: durable, more than an ordinary monster, strikes with reach. NOT a fair fistfight: Bill survives it by *engineering* the fight — terrain, its weight and what it can't do, his gun into the one geometry that matters, possibly the gallery's own mechanism turned against it. Winnable by a clever, careful, well-prepared man; lethal to a reckless one. (If the player fights it dumb, let consequences bite per fail-forward / death-is-real — but a Foundation boss should be beatable by a sharp first-timer who uses his head, which is the whole point of giving Bill *this* site.)

**Essence note:** this is a Lattice site, NOT essence-thinned. So — unlike near the sink — Bill's freshly-booted System renders CLEAN and full-fidelity here. (Contrast to bank for later: the day he takes the System near the Reach, it will dim and flicker. Here it works perfectly. He won't know to contrast them yet; the DM does.)

## COMPLETION & PAYOUT (locked to canon)

If he clears the dungeon boss and walks back out the far arch alive:

- **Awakening allotment (already landed at first kill):** 10 points, placed by Bill deliberately (his edge + the universal awakening rule). Delver Level 0 → 1.
- **The first choosing (at awakening, the universal window's doing — rendered in full System detail for Bill per `system_engineer_truth_v1.md`):** three Skills, two lock forever. Tailored to the *incoming* man (no delving history to read yet): a methodical engineer-alchemist, high Precision/Intellect/Insight. Offer per discipline — one deepening a built line, one opening something new, nothing combat-shaped he's never done. **Author the three exact offers at run-time** once we see how the delve actually went (what he did inside reads onto the boot offer's flavor). Log all three in dm-notes §11(d).
- **Foundation completion itself pays NO further points** (`game_bible.md` §12) and costs none of his Tier-1 ten.
- **Foundation-only GROWTH ITEM — "a cut above"** (special bonus): an object keyed to his spirit and class (System Engineer) that strengthens as he does. **Hold its exact form to resolve live**, but design intent: something that serves the Engineer and the campaign's spine without being raw combat power — e.g. an instrument/tool-class growth item that deepens Examine, drafting, or the Survey (kin to his faculty), OR a keyed personal-gear seed for the linked-drafts road (class Lv. 4). A "cut above" means it has more headroom / an extra evolution stage than an ordinary Foundation item. Do NOT make it a weapon that trivializes fights; make it an *understanding/▼capability* multiplier consistent with the player-locked "multiplies understanding, never safety" dial.
- **Foundation-only STARTER INTRINSIC (level 1)** keyed to class, affinity, and the person (`game_bible.md` §12, `ability_framework_v1.md` §5). **This is the flagged wrinkle:** Bill is a System Engineer with no ordinary magic affinity. Per `system_engineer_truth_v1.md`, leave the exact shape to play and to the file that governs his window. **Resolve live**, design intent: an intrinsic that amplifies his lane (analysis/structure/precision-of-perception), domain-flavored only loosely if at all — something that deepens what he already is rather than bolting on a caster's discipline. (An intrinsic is awakened-only, costs cultivation, scales/evolves at thresholds — the slow lane. Give him a seed that grows with the class, not a finished power.)
- **Loot chest (per-person, contribution-graded, `game_bible.md` §11):** one chest after the boss. Curriculum-special loot can be singular. Keep it consistent with a Foundation site (modest in raw money terms) but let the special-ness show in *quality/uniqueness* over quantity — possibly a material or a small object that feeds his understanding (a worked construct-component he can Examine and learn from → a future blueprint seed), rather than a pile of credits. Author at run-time.
- **Cap:** he is now Foundation 1/1 (lifetime growth completion for the tier spent). The plate will eventually show `TIER CAP: REACHED — FOUNDATION 1/1` only when/if he tries again. He won't know the cap exists yet; the "your soft hole only pays the once" veteran aside (dm-notes §9) is the seed that lets him infer it.

## THE BOOT (when first kill lands inside — staging per `system_engineer_truth_v1.md`)

Do not infodump. Disorientation in a familiar register: pale draft-work lines hanging in his vision that no one else is there to react to; terse text in a hand almost his own.

- **CORE: OPEN.** / **CLASS ASSIGNED — SYSTEM ENGINEER. Lv. 1.** / status plate (name, his numbers raw, a mana figure with no referent — Intellect 16 → **MANA 160**) / **POINTS: 10 — UNALLOCATED. DELVER LEVEL: 1.** / the window's first choosing unfolds (three Skills, full draft-detail, note that two will close).
- "System Engineer" should mean nothing to him — let him chew it for sessions.
- **One deniable beat, recommended:** as the plates first unfold, a half-second impression of a woman's hands guiding his on a pencil. Gone before he can hold it. Plant and never mention again until the origin thread matures (tier 4–5 seed).
- Keep the universal layer (points, choosing) distinct from the System layer (Examine, Drafting, plates) even here.
- He's mid-hole, alone, possibly mid-fight-aftermath, cold, heart going — and the world just changed. Give it weight, then let the hole's remaining danger snap him back: revelation does not pause the Warden.

Small chance it's still open and solo-able if he's quick. Let the fiction decide honestly based on his timing, not punish caution with a rigged miss nor reward dithering with a frozen world.
- **No re-author needed if skipped** — the curriculum-special concept and the boot staging stay banked for whenever Bill awakens (here, the supervised Brush Creek hole, or the copy-clause). This prep is not wasted; it's the awakening kit, ready.
