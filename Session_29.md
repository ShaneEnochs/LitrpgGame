## OPENING — THE SAFE ROOM, THE DESCENT TO THE COLOSSUS

The crew (FORTUNE'S FOLLY) woke fresh in the last safe room. Pool topped ~210, active conversion ~63%, active drain realized, coil gun braced serviceable, the rare restorative on Bill's belt. Myles called the move down to Floor 5 — the Heart of the Waste and the Ember-Colossus, the dungeon boss — and deferred the opening read to Bill, as on the Obsidian Stair. Unknown ground: the crew had never cleared the Emberwaste; Bill's read was their first real intel on the boss.

## THE READ — THE EMBER-COLOSSUS, SLEEPING

Bill ran a wide, thin forward cone over the caldera, then held a soak-read on the dormant boss kept **below Pearce's detectability line** (a deliberate sub-threshold probe — slow, patient, invisible). The read resolved:

- **Terrain:** a ~200-ft caldera, a lake of slow fire in the center, broken slag shelves and benches (cover). Essence flooded — the richest fuel of the run; the binding-draught environment.
- **Chaff:** two clutches of lesser cinder-things by the lake edge, swarm-grade, half-asleep — not the problem.
- **The Colossus:** ~150 ft off across the lake, reading as terrain — a ~40-ft heap of fused earth-slag with a wagon-wheel **molten fire core** banked deep behind a cold shell. Settled, asleep-not-dead.
- **The wake/cycle:** it runs on a **heat-cycle**, not muscle — heats to move (slag goes pliable at the joints), cools to rest. Every big motion is a core-spend pushed outward → the shell goes **thinnest over the heart during a spend**. The slam thins/cracks the shell; the heart only comes bare after it moves.
- **The nova:** a pressure-ceiling in the cycle — banked heat that must dump all at once or crack it from inside. Live-only to time.
- **Safe-spots:** the cold benches away from the waterline read cool (true cover for the nova); the high shelf coolest of all.

## THE PLAN — DON'T FIGHT IT, DRINK IT

Through a chain of in-room experiments (all flagged honestly before they resolved), Bill built a new capability instead of a fight:

1. **Core-to-core conduit through himself** — works, but he's a **lossy, slow bottleneck** (the filter takes its cut at 63%; some sheds to his pool).
2. **Open-air conduit between two cores** — built off the wraith-channel memory + the essence-signature grammar (write *flow this way* into the air). Bigger and faster (no bodily constriction), but it (a) won't hold its own shape without a keeper, and (b) leaks at equilibrium (a one-way pipe, not a one-way pump — quits when the cores even out unless something supplies the *want*).
3. **The great core as the pump and keeper.** Bill scanned it: a **maker-core**, built around a deep lined **intake well** (an organ, not a tank), with a **self-sustaining aimed will** — it *wants* fire and pulls past equilibrium on its own. The two bottlenecks (who keeps the channel, who supplies the want) are exactly what it is *for*.

**Test on the Imp-Prince core (20 ft, open-air conduit, core holding the far end):** it drank a **full fire boss core to husk in seconds** and barely eased its hunger — the well is **deeper than a boss core**; capacity grows as it eats; the meal **deepened the well**, made it slightly larger, and **moved the binding needle** (warmer, more bound). Conclusion: the core can drink the Colossus dry. The remaining problems: **landing the pipe on a live, hostile, 150-ft furnace**, and **what a contested draw does** (a boss that size may pull back).

## THE PARTNERSHIP — BILL AND THE GREAT CORE

Bill went into the link (built it deliberately — regard-to-regard, off the two prior deep reads where it looked back). He understood the great core is **old (older than the dungeons), wise about the dungeon's machinery, and a *who*** — its handle, the nearest render: **"the one who holds the fire so it doesn't burn the others"** (a keeper; the hunger all delve was it pulling the room's loose fire *toward itself and away from Bill*).

**Bill offered partnership, not use** — gave it his whole life (his mother and her loss, his father and brother, the journey west, Pem, Mrs. Maddox, Calloway, the firm, the cage, the nun, Esther, the town, the assayer, the wrong-shaped void), and asked it to be a partner and grow together rather than a growth item to be spent. **It said yes** — the hunger turned from appetite to a *lean-toward*; the bond tightened hard from *yours-as-property* toward *yours-as-partner*. Two keepers, each finding the other. (No formal binding yet — the level-gate still ahead.)

It offered the distance solution: **don't throw the pipe and hold the far end — send the core to *be* the far end.** Bill builds the conduit; the core keeps it open under fire (holding is the one thing it's for). Cost: the two become **one circuit** for the draw (its will, Bill's gates, his pool backing its reach); if the Colossus out-pulls the circuit, both drain down one pipe.

## THE COLOSSUS — KILLED BY DRINKING (no melee, no party damage)

**Myles built his half:** Jason and Myles cross the cold benches while it sleeps and get set close; Bill builds and **hands off** the pipe; Myles **wakes and baits** the boss to keep its attention *forward* (so it never feels the straw); Charles backs Bill (triage + nova-timer relay); Julie is Bill's wall (cinder-clutches + approach). Bill calls the draw at 5 seconds and keeps calling it — *fast / slow / losing* — with the **window-kill (bait slam → burst bared heart) as the floor under the plan** if the drink failed.

**The fight:**
- Bill signaled *anchored, go*; Myles woke it; the heap rose into the Colossus, attention swung forward.
- Bill bit the pipe home and drove a full-bore read into the bared-thinning heart. First 2 seconds: clean fast drink. Then the Colossus felt it and **pulled back** → a stall (two furnaces, one straw). The core **held** (keeper against keeper) — and the Colossus, pouring everything *forward* into Myles/Jason, **couldn't fight the duel and survive the fight at once** (its pull-back was divided; the core's wasn't). The stall broke; the draw ran Bill's way — **a grinding, contested, *winning* draw.**
- **Bill shared his sight down the link, live** — the core had been pulling blind; with Bill's eyes it pulled **smart**, at the furnace's failing seams. The grind accelerated (cost: Bill's pool feeding read + reach + shared-sight; the core watched his pool *for* him and held the elixir call).
- **The nova:** Bill calc'd ~12 sec, called the count, ordered Myles/Jason to cold stone at 5. It dumped on **empty stone** (clean count, the men already down behind cool benches; Charles threw a cool-sheet on Bill's side; Julie the wall). The nova — the boss's wipe-button — was **spent for nothing**, and the heat it banked for it was being drained anyway.
- **Death-throw:** near-dead, the Colossus shoved its **entire remaining reserve down the pipe at once** (the cook-from-inside risk). Bill trusted the core; the core **opened the well and swallowed the spike** — turned the death-throw into its largest single meal. The Colossus went out.

**Outcome:** Colossus dead. **Nobody bled — Unbroken intact (5 bosses clean).** The **heart-core drank whole, NOT shattered** (the kill came down the pipe, not through the heart) → the run's headline prize, intact. The great core **full**, slightly larger, more present, more bound.

- **The core's drink-capture** holds the Colossus's **living standing-pattern** (absorbed whole, the way Bill absorbed the wraith — except it went down the *core's* well, so the core caught it). **Between the two of them, the Colossus is captured whole** — the first thing they've captured *together*. ## THE CLIFF — THE DOUBLE DUNGEON

After the binding, a **second arch** cracked open in the caldera wall (solid an hour before) — the first nested delve on record. Bill's full read: a **Special, off-ladder, deeper than the dungeon**, **single-passage** (enter or don't; no second try; no way back out this mouth), a **thinking sovereign** seated at the bottom (name-shaped pressure, a hard syllable, past Bill's resolution). The core read it *with* him and pressed half a step further — **recognition, not fear**; the thing down there is the kind of question it was made to ask. It *wants* to go (purpose, not hunger — like it found why it woke).

**The vote:** Bill called it an honest coin-flip (pioneers vs. blind), and chose to make his new gun + arsenal first. The crew landed: Jason in, Julie in, Charles **in with reservations on the record** (no survivor reports, an unbottomed sovereign, one-way), Myles in (now-or-never; never stronger; the arch won't come twice). Bill's vote (counts double, the only one who can read it): **"It wants to go. Plus now I've got all these hellfire darts, so…"** — **GO.**
