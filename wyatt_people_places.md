# Wyatt's Book — PEOPLE & PLACES

*My hand, my own window casing upstairs — my own wall now. Two books, not four — ruled it down one evening to save the searching. Anything that hangs a man stays short and crooked here; the straight of it lives only in C.'s box. Current to the night of Day 17 — Wednesday, November 12, 1862. Each person carries the running read, freshest at the end.*

## PEM — the second outfit (NOT mine; a counterparty)

- **The 100 is PAID** — counted onto C.'s desk first, Day 6. "You paid first. I'll remember it longer'n you think." It's his, it's gone, it's done.
- **The old share CONVERTED, not killed** — his cut of my delving proceeds stands as struck, if and when proceeds ever exist. Follows the man, not the hole. "Keepin' it open like an account."
- **THE COMPACT — answered, amended, papered.** He sent the rules (Ada's hand, his phrasing under), Tatch walked me through them:
  - **Four base rules:** (1) patches — he holds the awning man, I hold the depot man, neither works the other's watch without the other boss's yes first; (2) solo work whole to its outfit, shared jobs on Min's shared books, **the split named before the job, not after the pile** (he was hard on this); (3) crews never deal crew-to-crew — goods and word boss-to-boss through Tatch and him; (4) disputes we can't settle go to C. — to write down what was agreed, not pick a winner.
  - **His two hard lines:** no buyout ever, at any price ("I been somebody's property. I'm done bein' it. A paper that can be bought is a slower road to the same place"); clean exit — each walks out with what was his walking in.
  - **My answer:** yes to all four (the split-named-first is the one I'd have asked for myself); yes to both hard lines as things he's RIGHT to want, not grudging. One fix asked: name the *method* for splitting what we build together over the season.
  - **C. tightened it:** divisible built-value counted and split by share; indivisible (a kid taught a trade, a name, a supplier) NOT split — **a kid owns the trade he's taught; no boss claims a child's hands as property** (C. wrote that knowing him; it'll land). Built-value disputes to C. to record. Lasting shared-built things noted in Min's shared books as they're made.
  - **Min's amendment:** a standing right for either boss to read the shared book, both ways (binds me first).
  - **Now with Pem to read on his own time. Still PENDING — his reading + his answer + his two unfired pieces** (the annex second room; his own pressure named). Not yet executed.
- **Division by ground (Day 6, holds):** his outfit holds the **awning man**; the firm holds the **depot man**; product trades through Tatch and Pem; crews never deal direct.
- His stock, Day 6: knows the awning man cold — **Palace annex, pays a week ahead** — and **paid for TWO rooms this week.** The second room — still don't know who it's for. Open.

- **Crane** — **the range. BEGUN Day 16; a real turn Day 17.** 0.5/session, my own reloads, mornings, just turn up, no rain. "You'll do." Bought him a hot meal for his knowledge (level, not charity — he took it). The frame he gave me, worth more than the meal: the gun is the FIRST WORD underground, not the whole answer. Train the first shot from a bad start (snap-draw, moving thing, bad light). Train RELOADING blind and fast — that's the survival skill, not marksmanship; the man who dies is the one caught empty. And know the moment the gun's DONE and the knife takes over. The gun and the knife are one decision with two answers.** He opens up when his knowledge is bought, not begged — that's the key to the man. One of Doss's three legs.

The cross-draw was always the seam made physical — gun hand and knife hand off the same body. Commit to the knife when the gun's empty; and the reverse too, knife-to-gun, the cut that buys the half-step to draw. He still rides my telegraphing but it's tightening. He likes a student who makes him think; I've turned him from a knife teacher into a teacher of the hinge.** One of Doss's three legs; the knife wants hours and I'm putting them in.

## THE LAY OF THE TOWN

Falls from the high end (west, uphill) down to the rail (east) — grows downhill toward the money. Four streets across the slope, north to south: **Professional** (genteel, less traffic), **the Main Stretch** (the busy spine, full width to the depot), **Freight Lane** (working, muddy), **Back Row** (cheap houses to grazing flats). The armored rail runs the east edge. One road off the high northwest corner — the **N.W. road to Hollow Reach, a day's ride.**

- **High end:** the Catholic Church (Lessard); the **Argent hall** — the cage, the board, the yard (where I met Dell). The board posts a Bureau tier/grade per hole. **Hollow Reach got named a "Special Delve"** — pulls fools NW toward the worst ground in the territory thinking a windfall waits. No Hollow Reach posting on the open board — that's writ business, above the floor. Quest body-work known here (Bly, Hessler routing); mind the shine.
- **Professional Street:** C.'s office; Arrowood's surgery; **Pearce's shuttered stone-front assay office** (owned free, lien ~340; deep main room, back workroom with a real flue, a yard, quarters up, a cellar near nobody's seen — the crates of books boxed inside). The eventual works — building LAST.
- **The Main Stretch (west→east):** the Palace (annex rooms — [⟂⟂], two this week); the Drovers' (Fenner's bar; Pearce's evenings, the bar-end); the **Land Office** (Quill's desk; watch the keystone here); the **Oregon Clarion** (P.'s paper, the only press); the **Mercantile** (the counterman; the Quest account; my bone-black); the Marshal's office; the **Gunsmith** (the bench deal — kit owned; the speedloader build Friday; cartridges; reloading makings); the **Depot** at the rail (the telegraph — the watched window, busier now — Bess watches the freight window).
- **Freight Lane:** the Freight Office; **Maddox House** right behind it (NO LONGER home — tie cut Day 15; an honest lodger's table still open); **the Dunmore Brothers' warehouse** (the lift-works job — the catch holds, the cage hangs as proof; my tins ship and my bulk's stored here); **the Dunmore** boarding house (Esther's room — the cell knows it; distinct from the warehouse); the **Garrick Wagon Yard** (the hub job — proven; bench first-refusal; the Cinder road runs from here). The **Argent freight yard** sits up by the rail, NE — where crew 4 mustered and rolled.
- **Home now: my own house** (bought in trust, Wyatt the tenant; the address kept out of even this book — the books in its upstairs window casing, the dangerous thinking with them; a duplicate key cut Day 15). The kitchen's my dull workshop; the window bench took the warmer trials Day 17.
- **Back Row / low edge:** the **Stockpens** billiard hall ([R.], watched); the slaughterhouse (Marguerite's floor); cheap houses thinning south to grazing flats; the **Catholic Mission**, set apart southeast by the rail (Beatrice & Agnes; the fever wing; the herb garden — my winter school; the sealed account under the altar stone). Good light only. Known here as "John."
