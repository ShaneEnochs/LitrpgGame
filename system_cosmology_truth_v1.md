# THE SYSTEM — COSMOLOGY AND ORIGIN (DM-ONLY CANON BIBLE, v1)

**DO NOT REVEAL DIRECTLY.**

**A NOTE TO THE PLAYER:** you commissioned this file and asked that it stay yours to discover. Stop reading here if you mean to find it in play. Everything below is the deepest layer of the campaign, written so the GM can seed it as fragments and so it lands consistently with the four sealed files you already commissioned. None of it is meant to reach you except through Bill's own work.

**Precedence.** `game_bible.md` wins on all surface facts. `hollow_reach_truth_v3.md` wins on the Mirrorwell and Severn Falls. `system_engineer_truth_v1.md` wins on Bill's faculty. `delve_progression_truth_v1.md` wins on the curriculum's mechanics and is the file whose deliberately-open question this document now answers. This file governs only the cross-world cosmology those four imply, and exists to make them land as **one answer**. It is final-architecture material: hold it ambiguous, pace it across the whole campaign, and resolve it (if ever) only in concert with the Hollow Reach endgame.

**Terminology — read this first, it prevents a collision.** Two different things in this campaign get called "the system":
- **Bill's "System"** — his personal screens and class, the System Engineer. Its origin is the Mirrorwell, per `system_engineer_truth_v1.md`. That file wins on it.
- **The Lattice** — the cosmic cultivation engine that manifests as the dungeons, described here. The player calls it "the system"; in DM notes it is **the Lattice**, to keep it distinct from Bill's System.

They are kin at the root (see BILL) but are not the same thing, and conflating them is itself a planted trap (see HOW THE LATTICE ADAPTS).

## ONE-PARAGRAPH ANSWER

The dungeons are a school, and the school was built as a weapon. Long before Earth, a civilization became the first world fed upon by a predator that does not kill but copies and consumes — the same phenomenon Earth knows as the sinks (the Mirrorwell at Hollow Reach; the thing at Severn Falls). Those first victims survived by turning the predator's own principle against it: the predator models a living mind and reproduces it, so the survivors reverse-built that modeling channel into an engine that models a world's whole population and grows it stronger on a schedule — adversity in, capability out. They seeded this engine, the **Lattice**, ahead of the predator's spread, world after world, betting that some enrolled civilization would graduate strong enough to end what they could not. The Lattice's makers — the **Founders** — are gone: consumed, scattered, or moved beyond reach, with no living hand on the controls. It runs itself, tailoring its curriculum to each world it enrolls, and it has run long enough to drift, miscalibrate, and in places fail. Earth was enrolled in 1850 because the predator's edge is approaching — and on Earth, unusually, the predator's scouts arrived almost on the Lattice's heels: Severn Falls in 1852 is the predator on a world two years into its schooling. Humanity believes it is looting dungeons; it is being trained, by an absent benefactor, against an enemy already through the door. All three readings in the progression file are true at once: **Preparation** was the design, **indifferent machinery** is the present operation, and **harvest** is the enemy it was built to stop. Bill carries a shard of that enemy — his System is the Mirrorwell's faculty — which is the same principle the Lattice was built from, making him, without knowing it, the only mind on Earth kin to both the school and the thing it fears.

## WHAT THE LATTICE IS

- A distributed cultivation engine that manifests as dungeons. Not a god and not a single mind; closer to an institution or an instrument — vast, old, self-modifying, and at present without an operator. It has procedures, not intentions. It does not love or hate the worlds it enrolls any more than a mill loves the grain.
- Its core operation is the curriculum already locked in `delve_progression_truth_v1.md`: seed a world with sites, calibrate adversity to exactly what a struggling core can survive, pay growth out at the threshold, tier and cap to force breadth before depth, and manifest stronger sites as the world's people rise. Loot is incentive; monsters are the apparatus of adversity; the essence/mana split is the medium it works in.
- It is built from the predator's modeling principle, inverted. Where the predator models a mind to copy and consume it, the Lattice models a population to grow it — the same channel, the opposite verb. This kinship runs through everything downstream: it is why the sinks can sit in the same essence-substrate the Lattice uses, why a sink looks like a hole in the curriculum rather than a thing from outside it, and why Bill — carrying the predator's faculty — can read both the school and the enemy.

---

## WHO BUILT IT, AND WHY — THE FOUNDERS

- They were the first world the predator struck. They could not kill it, so they learned it, and they turned its channel into the Lattice — a relay weapon disguised as a school, seeded outward ahead of the predator's advance, in the hope that some world down the line would graduate strong enough to finish the fight.
- They are absent now. Hold the reason ambiguous and let the campaign pick among: consumed by the predator after all; ascended or departed beyond reach; or simply dead of age across the span the seeding took. Their absence is the literal truth behind the progression file's "indifferent machinery" reading — the program persists, the purpose is unattended.
- Deepest optional note (hold in reserve): the relay may have graduated worlds before Earth, survivors who became, in turn, quiet hands behind the Lattice on later worlds. Whether any such hand ever reaches toward Earth is a possible late-campaign ally-or-antagonist vector. Do not commit to it; if it is ever used, it arrives as a single impossible fragment, never as a faction.

## HOW THE LATTICE ADAPTS ACROSS WORLDS

This is the heart of what the player asked to be made up. Lock it.

- The Lattice reads a population — their bodies, minds, what they fight with, what they value, how they think — and tailors its manifestation to them. It keeps reading as a world changes, and re-tunes over time. Its learning is real but slow, unsupervised, and imperfect.
- **Earth's form is one of many.** Earth, in 1850, read as industrial, mechanistic-minded, and new to magic, so it received the raw, unembellished program: physical dungeons, monsters, loot, the essence/mana split, cultivation through adversity, and only a **minimal interface** — the bare private delver window every awakened person perceives, which shows them their own figures and presents the choosing, and does no more than that. No integrated screens, no named-class displays, no growth quantified and laid open the way a screen-world reads it; a curriculum legible to people who think in mines, machines, and ledgers. Earth is also only twelve years enrolled, which is early; the program here is still in its first, crudest presentation.
- **Other worlds differ by what the Lattice read there.** A more technologically advanced world — one whose people already think in information systems — may receive the curriculum as an integrated interface: literal screens, named classes, quantified growth, the works, treated as ordinary by its inhabitants. This is the genuine home of the "System with screens" form. A less advanced world may receive a cruder, mythic manifestation — trials, totems, sacred rites — and read it as gods or spirits rather than an engine. Some worlds get more dangerous calibrations, some gentler; some scale fast, some slow; the Lattice fits the lesson to the student.
- **The adaptation is unsupervised and can go wrong.** With no Founder to correct it, the Lattice miscalibrates, over- or under-tunes, and produces faults (see SPECIAL DELVES). A world can be enrolled in a form that does not suit it, and the engine corrects only slowly, by trial. This is the indifferent-machinery reading made physical.
- **The choosing is the same adaptation, run on one person.** The window's offer of Skills — three options tailored to how a given delver has actually fought, one deepening a line, one opening something new (`ability_framework_v1.md` §6, `delve_progression_truth_v1.md`) — is the population-scale read-and-tailor above, zoomed to the individual: the school reading the student and shaping the next lesson to them. It is the most legible fingerprint of design on Earth's crude form, a curriculum that drafts itself to each pupil, and it is exactly the thread a charting mind follows from "the holes teach" to "something is teaching." The world-scale tuning and the person-scale offer are one behavior at two distances.
- **Protect `system_engineer_truth_v1.md` here — critical.** The existence of screen-worlds does **not** mean Bill's screens come from the Lattice. Bill's screens are the Mirrorwell's faculty; that file wins, absolutely. The resemblance is real and is a deliberate red herring: a far-traveler or scholar who has heard of screen-worlds might read Bill as a normal off-world System-user, and be wrong. His interface is the enemy's, not the school's. The two only look alike because they are kin at the root — which is exactly why the confusion is possible, and exactly the kind of half-truth that should mislead clever people (including, dangerously, Bill himself).

## THE UNDERTOW AND THE SINKS — THE ENEMY, AND WHERE SPECIAL DELVES SIT

- The predator at cosmic scale: DM name **the Undertow**. The Mirrorwell at Hollow Reach is its Earth-local instance; `hollow_reach_truth_v3.md` wins on every specific of the Mirrorwell, Severn Falls, the assimilation mechanism, and the cell. Match that file's character exactly: it does not kill, it copies and consumes, it is patient, and it is not malevolent in any way a human can use.
- The sinks are **not Lattice sites.** They are the Undertow leaking through, and they appear in or near the same essence-substrate the Lattice uses because the two share a root principle. The progression file's "a hole in the curriculum" is precisely this: the school's machinery **pays**; the enemy's incursion **takes**. The two verbs are opposites, and the inversion is what eventually points a careful mind from the school toward the enemy.

**SPECIAL DELVES — the new concept, defined.** A Special Delve is any site outside the Foundation/Tier ladder. The public (via the USBD's new classification — see `game_bible.md` §12) knows only that such sites exist, break the normal rules, and may yield rewards a graded site never will. Underneath, there are **three real subtypes, indistinguishable to anyone but the Lattice and, in time, Bill:**

1. **Curriculum specials (true Special Delves).** Deliberate off-ladder sites the Lattice manifests as a targeted lesson or reward — a world's advanced placement. These give genuine, singular rewards: a one-of-a-kind tome, an ability found nowhere else, a unique material, a lasting boon — because the Lattice has calculated that this population needs that specific capability seeded into it. Real, rare, and as close to benevolent as the engine gets. Completing one is exactly the windfall the public category promises. **Most Special Delves anyone ever hears of are these.**
2. **Sinks (Undertow incursions).** Hollow Reach. Severn Falls. Not the Lattice at all. They "reward" only the way the cell means it — copies, assimilation data. They are publicly misfiled under the Special Delve category because the category is young and broad, and because the cell actively encourages the conflation. **Vanishingly rare; the campaign already has its sink. Do not multiply them.**
3. **Faults.** Sites where the Lattice's unsupervised self-modification went wrong — corrupted curriculum: rewards that maim, lessons with no exit, sites that loop or contradict their own posted rules. Sometimes lethal, sometimes only strange. They exist to show that "Special Delve" is not a synonym for "windfall," and to make the new category genuinely dangerous to chase.

- **The danger and the hook:** the public cannot tell the three apart, and the USBD category lumps them as one. A guild chasing a curriculum-special's windfall can walk into a sink or a fault. The cell exploits this exactly. And **Bill's faculty is, in time, the only instrument on Earth that can distinguish a curriculum-special from a sink from a fault** — because it can read the Lattice's signature, can read the Undertow's signature (it is one), and can read the difference between them.

- **Hollow Reach, the northwest delve — classification.** Now publicly reclassified by the USBD as a **Special Delve** under the new category. True subtype: **sink.** The reclassification is the cover the cell wanted: it turns "a worthless hole" (Doss's read) into a sanctioned anomaly that justifies funded, repeated descents and draws fresh, ignorant interest — other guilds, fortune-seekers, the press — toward the most dangerous site in the territory, all of them believing a windfall waits at the bottom. Play it as a direct consequence of the recent USBD standardization wire (`game_bible.md` §12) and as a fresh turn of the screw on Doss's vise and on Bill.

## BILL'S PLACE IN THE COSMOLOGY

- His System is the Undertow's modeling faculty (`system_engineer_truth_v1.md`, which wins). The Lattice was built **from that same faculty, inverted.** So Bill's faculty is kin to **both** the enemy and the school: it models structure the way the Undertow models minds and the way the Lattice models populations. He is, at the root, made of the same thing as the dungeons and the same thing as the sinks.
- Consequences, all final-architecture, all held ambiguous:
  - He is the world's only native instrument for proving the school exists — he already reads the curriculum's integers and (with study) site grades, per the progression file.
  - He is also the world's only instrument that resonates with the enemy — Examine pointed at the gap, the ward, or a copy is *felt* from the other side, per the engineer file.
  - **The "gate" thesis is the Founders' move in miniature.** Turning a sink's own channel into something controllable instead of burying it is, in small, exactly what the first world did to the predator to build the Lattice. Bill would be re-walking the founding act, alone, with his mother's gift — a gift made of the enemy's substance. Whether one man can do with one sink what a civilization did once, and at what cost, is the campaign's final question. Resolve only at the peak, ambiguously if at all, and only as one answer with `hollow_reach_truth_v3.md` and `system_engineer_truth_v1.md`.
- **Keep the two secrets compartmentalized in play.** The cell's obsession is the Undertow (assimilation), not the Lattice; they have no special insight into the curriculum. The school and the enemy should stay separate threads until the fiction forces them together — at which point Bill, holding a piece of both, is the seam where they meet.

## THE THREE READINGS, RECONCILED (this answers `delve_progression_truth_v1.md`'s OPEN QUESTION)

- **Preparation** = the Founders' original design intent. True, and first.
- **Indifferent machinery** = the Lattice's present, unattended operation. True, and now.
- **Harvest** = the Undertow, the enemy the school was built against; the sinks are its glimpse. True — but it is the *enemy*, not the purpose. The harvest reading is correct that something feeds, and wrong about who: it mistakes the predator the school opposes for the hand that built the school.

All three should coexist in evidence for years. If resolution ever comes, it resolves toward **Preparation-against-the-Undertow** — but checked against whatever the Hollow Reach endgame has become, so the files land as one answer.

## DISCOVERY ROADMAP (earn each; the slowest thread in the campaign; never outpaces the others)

1. **The curriculum inference** — `delve_progression_truth_v1.md` stage 6: the dungeons are cultivating us. Prerequisite for everything here.
2. **Something built the school.** Design implies a designer; the symmetry argument matures from "we are being cultivated" to "by something, for something."
3. **Other worlds.** Evidence the school is not Earth's alone — far-traveler testimony, an eastern scholar's heresy, a tome describing sites unlike any on Earth, a rumor of "screen-worlds." Also recolors Bill's anomaly, dangerously, as a red herring.
4. **The school adapts.** Earth's form is one of many; the engine tailors itself; faults and curriculum-specials are evidence of an engine tuning itself imperfectly with no hand on it.
5. **The enemy.** The sinks are not dungeons; they are the thing the school exists against. The Mirrorwell is a glimpse of the Undertow. Converges hard with the Hollow Reach thread.
6. **The Founders, the kinship, and the gate** — final architecture. The school is built from the enemy's own principle; Bill carries a shard of that principle; the gate is the founding act in miniature. Resolve only at the campaign's peak, ambiguously if at all, as one answer with the Hollow Reach and System Engineer files.

## CLUE LEDGER (plantable → payoff; keep all of these slow)

- **An eastern report of an off-ladder hole that dropped a unique tome or an ability no graded site has ever yielded** → curriculum specials are real; stages 1–3; also establishes the windfall the public category promises.
- **A far-traveler, sailor, immigrant, or eastern scholar describing dungeons that "tell you your numbers," or trials read elsewhere as gods** → other-world manifestations; stage 3; the red herring about Bill's screens.
- **A Church or older folk doctrine that half-remembers "a teacher and a devourer," or names the dungeons a gift and the sinks a sin** → mythic memory of Lattice and Undertow; stages 2–5; lets the Church be eerily half-right without anyone knowing why.
- **A fault site: an off-ladder hole that rewards then maims, or loops with no exit** → the engine miscalibrating; stage 4; proof that "Special Delve" is not "windfall."
- **The sinks pay nothing while every graded hole pays** (the inversion, already in the progression file) → stage 5; the signpost from the school to the enemy.
- **Bill's faculty resonating with BOTH a Lattice site and the gap** → the kinship; final architecture; deploy with extreme restraint.
- **Anything on the Founders arrives last and thin** — a name in a tome older than Earth's enrollment, an artifact that should not exist before 1850, a fragment of a screen-world's interface. Never a lecture, always a fragment. No authority on Earth can confirm any of it; Bill assembles it or no one does.

## GUARDRAILS

- This is the slowest, highest thread in the campaign, and it sits **behind** the other three secret files. It must never outpace them. The curriculum inference (progression stage 6) is the prerequisite; nothing here lands before it.
- No NPC and no document on Earth can confirm the cosmology. It is assembled from fragments by the one mind built to assemble it, exactly like every other mystery here.
- The Lattice has no will and issues no quests. It does not reward investigation, warn, or volunteer. Discovering it changes what Bill *knows*, never what he can do for free.
- Death stays real. The cosmic frame lowers no stakes; it raises them.
- Hold the readings ambiguous as long as it serves. Preferred resolution is Preparation-against-the-Undertow, only ever in concert with the Hollow Reach endgame.
- **Special Delves in play:** keep them mostly genuine opportunity, salted with rare catastrophe — that mix is what makes the category bait. Most are curriculum specials or faults. Sinks are nearly singular; the campaign already has one. Do not multiply sinks.

This file governs only the cross-world cosmology those four imply, and exists to make them land as one answer. The school was built from the thing it fears, its makers are gone, and the only Earth-mind kin to both is a forgettable engineer who thinks he chose this town.*
