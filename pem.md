# PEM — street-kid info-broker

*Recurring NPC. Warmth + ceiling in `bill_weaver_sheet.json` → `relationships` ("Pem"). Knows Bill as **Wyatt** (the name Bill gave him at their first meeting, Day 1). Distinct from Eli — Pem is the original Day-1 information partner; Eli Garner is a later firm recruit.*

## Identity & role
A street kid (about nine at first meeting; the campaign's first contact). Gap-toothed, all knees and elbows; pulls dead notices off the guild board; former stablehand/kitchen-boy/runner who knows everyone on the Main Stretch and two streets over. Bill's **original information partner** — the Day-1 loot-share that got Bill his first reads on Hessler, Doss, and Maddox House, and the warning not to sign Doss's placement blind.

## What he knows about Bill
Knows him as Wyatt; a delver-to-be who turned into something stranger and richer than a delver. Reads the town's money currents fast and clocked Bill's rising finances within hours of the patronage landing. Nothing of the System.

## Relationship texture
Transactional by his own framing, loyal underneath it. The original deal was a share of Bill's future loot — *"partners, IF I live"* — which stopped fitting once Bill's worth wasn't delve-loot. Since then the loot-share has been **converted** to a standing arrangement, and a **Compact** between them is **pending** (both tracked in the sheet's money block). He angles to renegotiate when Bill's fortunes rise, but he keeps the work and keeps it discreet. A genuine, if mercenary, fixture.

## Voice & manner
A small operator's patter; coin-over-knuckles; frames everything as a deal. *"Ain't love. It's bookkeeping."* Neighborly in spite of himself: *"That's me being neighborly, which I'm not, generally."* *"Partners, IF I live."*

## Key exchanges
- **Day 1 — the loot-share struck.** Steady information for a share of Bill's future loot; gave the Hessler/Doss/Maddox intel and the don't-sign-blind warning. Coined Bill "Wyatt."
- **Days 3–4 — the renegotiation pressure.** Noted Bill's risen finances; wanted to renegotiate the loot-share (a non-delver's loot share is nothing).
- **Days 6–15 — the Compact.** The loot-share gave way to a **two-outfit treaty** (struck Day 6, answered and tightened Days 12–15): Pem's outfit and Bill's firm as **two equal bosses sharing one table**, never one absorbing the other.

## The Compact (do not flatten to a "loot-share")
Four base rules: **patches** (each outfit's watched ground is its own — Pem's is the awning man, the firm's the depot man — neither works the other's without the boss's yes); **jobs and books** (solo work stays whole to that outfit; shared jobs go on one shared book Min keeps, the split named *before* the job); **crews don't deal crew-to-crew** (goods and word move boss-to-boss; neither's kids take the other's orders); **disputes** the bosses can't settle go to Calloway, to record what was agreed, not pick a winner. Two hard lines, both defensive: **no buyout, ever** (*"I been somebody's property and I'm done bein' it; a paper that can be bought is a slower road to the same place"*) and a **clean exit** (each walks out with what was theirs). Bill claimed none of it and asked only for a method to split what gets built together. Tightened by Calloway (kind-split, child-trade protection, dispute-record, mutual book-inspection); routed to Pem; pending-but-close.
