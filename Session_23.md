## THE TUESDAY GUILD AUCTION (first beat)

Bill went to read the real delve market — believed prices, eastern buyers' agents, a fighting orb to scout, the gatekeeper core gauged for consignment, the board read for a crew. He left the **great forge-core home** (behind the trust) and brought the **gatekeeper core + three frame-metal samples** (option for a Pearce slip). He held his abilities wide open all morning to study weaves across many live cases — a slow real bleed off the pool, noted.

**He came to watch and bought eight lots** — flagged the whole way as more appetite than intended; Insight tracked the visible-pattern cost (eight "Wyatt" lines in the factor's hand-filled book). The buys, in order:
- **Lot 2 — 3 eastern infused lamp-fixtures + a warming-bedstone (assayed), 60 cr.** Outbid the mercantile man **Tasker** to watch the believed price land (~50+ wholesale; town pays multiples). Plan: study then sell back to Tasker @50 (net 10) — and the buy-then-sell-back *reinforced* the harmless-amateur cover.
- **Lot 4 (as-found) — sack of unread cores, 13 cr.** Cheap banking fuel.
- **Lot 4 (niche) — an earth-aspected niche core, 65 cr.** Won over a local essence-worker ("the wall man"); a clean single-aspect specimen. *(Spent whole that afternoon into the filter.)*
- **Lot 8 — Warmstone + Cleardraw (Lv.1 utility orbs), 75 cr.** Study specimens; Warmstone is the magical analog of the iron hand-warmer.
- **Lot 10 — Steadyhand (Lv.2 Passive), 185 cr — SEATED.** Won over a marksman; seated on the spot into Bill's empty universal Passive seat — permanent, free-to-carry, stills tremor. His first seated universal Skill; the dullest, most useful buy of the day.
- **Lot 12 — an unassayed old charm, 22 cr.** Forceread read a faint *live* old weave the room priced at nothing. The one lot only Bill could value.

**Lots let go (the discipline):**
- **Lot 7 — Lv.3 combat orb (Concussive Strike).** Watched it sell to an eastern agent **@525** (seating floor uncertain for Might-12/DL-1; would've been a loud expensive buy). Studied its weave for free while two agents bid.
- **Lot 9 — beast core + beast-aspected material.** The day's held-powder target; contested to **140** vs the wall man (who'd lost the earth core to Bill and got his back up). Bill paused at top-of-band, can't-use-it-until-the-maker-core-binds, already-holds-one-aspect — and let it go. The wall man won and re-filed Bill as a harmless amateur (disguise bought by not-spending).
- **Lot 11 — Warding domain tome.** Sold east **@2,200**. Bill (no caster/affinity/license) sat out and read its structure for free — a tome is a *method/score* (spell-as-method, tome-as-teaching), unlike a seated weave.
- Also passed: the warded breastplate (studied instead — confirmed runes anchor weaves into dead metal), Wallrun (Movement), Lantern-eye (Passive), and the certified-core piles he didn't take.

**Market learned cold:** assayed cores trade near floor confidently; identical unread cores at a third, grudgingly (the stamp is most of the money — the whole Pearce thesis on a slate); aspected goods carry a premium and a use; combat orbs and domain tomes make hundreds-to-thousands and LEAVE the territory the hour they sell (which is why a western delver short a fighting Skill has no cheap road to one); three ways power enters a person (seated Skill / drafted ability / cast spell), read from living examples of each.

**Weave study (Forceread, all morning — banked, not all stored):** a live **sustained ward** on the caravan guard that *breathed* (idles cheap, flushes on perceived threat — the responsive, more-efficient living form vs the rigid rune-inscribed one); a **seated-but-idle active** (potential, no flow); a faint and then a fuller **Inspect-family reading weave** (reach-out-and-sample-back — kin to Forceread); a Lv.1 Movement and a Lv.1 Passive *seating* into other delvers' cores; and the **Lv.3 combat orb's** staged force-dump grammar, studied free.

**Tasker (NPC met).** After the sale, Bill caught the mercantile man plainly — said he wanted the lamps to study the works, no use for the goods after, and would sell them back at week's end for 50 (whole & resaleable, Tasker's condition). The story flattered Tasker's sense of the world (a tinker who can't leave a mechanism unopened) and reinforced the cover. Bonus: Tasker marks up exactly the eastern durables Bill's warmer/stove mean to undercut — a natural first customer for the western counter-goods (partnership leg-2).

## HOME BENCH — THE ASPECT-FILTER

Back home, door barred, pool part-recovered, Bill set out to **build the barrier weave** he'd read off the guard and then **strengthen it with the earth essence.**

- **The Standing Ward drafted.** Built from understanding (not copied) — a sustained-hold barrier; the void closed clean. **~70 creation; ~4/min sustained.** Drafted, NOT slotted. Learned it's a **Passive** if always-on (would need the Passive seat — Steadyhand's now) or could be drafted as a triggered **Active** (the responsive shape, fits a drafting slot — the smarter build).
- **The wall, then the road.** Trying to fuel the ward with the earth core's aspect revealed the missing middle: aspect lives in *essence*; his mana is *neutral*; the ward runs on neutral mana and makes a plain ward. To aspect the fuel he needs a **filter** — which he'd never built and didn't know existed an hour before. The draft's honest blank was shaped exactly like *a thing that aspects raw essence.*
- **The filter built.** Bill meditated/cultivated (passive essence→mana, topping the pool), then broke the earth core down as a *template* — read *what an aspect is* (a disciplined fold in essence) by holding an earth thread against a neutral thread — and ran his neutral mana against the grain, pass after lossy pass, Forceread keeping each correction. He **spent the entire earth core** (now a dead stone) and his pool hard, and came out with a **first-generation earth-aspect filter**: lossy (several units neutral in per usable unit earth out), but real and his. The conceptual key — aspect-as-fold, filtering-as-running-against-the-grain — makes the *next* filter (any aspect, given a specimen) far faster. An **earth-aspected ward is now buildable** (draft + run mana through the filter) once he has earth essence to feed it again.

**Canon clarified/added this session (player-directed):** the essence-vs-mana relationship made crisp — **essence = raw fuel, mana = refined spendable pool, conversion = the refining step.** A cultivator can **convert essence→mana**: passive (universal, slow = ordinary regen, faster in strong essence) and **active** (deliberate, fast, a TRAINED technique; essence-stones the common on-ramp — why casters carry them). Lossy; refuels the pool but never raises the Intellect-set ceiling; source = banked/ambient/stones, never the primary core; aspected essence converts worse than neutral. (Folded into `game_bible.md` §13 and `essence_aspects_v1.md` §4b at close.) Bill has not yet learned *active* conversion — latent, reason-into-able; his banked cores are now potential field fuel.

## THE PEARCE MEETING (the turn)

**Reaching him.** Insight-blessed: carry the note himself (respect, his ground, his clock), lead with the one honest single hook only he can do (the great forge-core wants a dungeon-licensed read), leave it with Fenner at the Drovers' (the door Pearce opened), and not turn up at the wounded office unannounced. Pearce answered **fast and sober, on the back of Bill's own note** — agreed to read it, named a proper fee, named the Drovers' (not the office), and set the frame himself: *"a licensed read on dungeon goods is a real thing and not a favor, so bring it like business and we'll keep it business."* Bill sent the runner ahead asking for privacy (framed as ordinary prudence, not cloak-and-dagger), and brought the gatekeeper core, the frame-metal, the unread cores, and the charm — holding the portfolio and leaving the great core home.

**Cover prepared (held, not spent up front):** a vague "reading Skill, came with the core" to explain any weave-sense tell — **never** named an *intrinsic* (too specific/checkable, points at the System's real category), and kept holstered unless a tell got caught.

**The consultation, in sequence:**
- **The unread cores** (easy warm-up). Pearce sorted them — mostly common/floor, one strong common, one cracked dud, and a **niche core with a faint earth-aspect** (the one Bill would later spend). His probing question — *what do you want them FOR* — Bill answered with a *shaped truth*: he **awakened yesterday on a special-foundation delve** into a **crafting Skill** that eats essence and mana, so he wants the fuel and doesn't care about the stamp. Leading with "I awakened yesterday / I'm new" made Pearce the senior man — a balm to an obsolete expert.
- **The frame-metal.** Pearce named it **made stock** — essence worked *into* the forging, not an enchantment *on* it; dense-wrong; *holding* something. A read that woke the scientist in him; he wanted his bench and better light. Bill fed the *work* hard while steering off the *provenance* (the closed door toward the forge/great core) — kept the mystery a *shared* one, and let drop the light shape of "we could run this down properly, if you wanted."
- **The gatekeeper core** (the hook). Bill framed it so the **license** mattered: a guardian core wants a *dungeon-licensed* read no factor can give — and Pearce is the only one in the territory. The thing the Bureau couldn't standardize away. Pearce did the licensed read like the man he was — the weariness gone, *being himself* — and gave a believed price well above a factor's stamp.
- **The charm** (the oddity). Bill set it down with the cover (a *feeling*, drawn to a thing the room called junk), and Pearce's **Inspect caught the live weave** the whole room missed — *"your gut's not wrong, this isn't dead."* Old worked weave, faint, unnameable cold, wants his bench. (Bill privately reads it live too; took Pearce's read at face value, showed nothing.)

**The Doss play — the seed flowers.** Bill told Pearce there was one more thing he hadn't brought to a bar — that **Doss** said show it to no one but him, that she'd called him the only man she'd trust to name it, and that she wished she could get him back on a guild payroll, the highest praise Bill's heard her give. A *third party's* (an officer's) judgment, reaching Pearce secondhand, that he couldn't read as Bill's play — and it broke the last of his guard. Pearce **invited Bill to the office himself** (twice), named himself *wasting* at the end of the bar, said *bring me a reason to remember what the bench was for*, and agreed: **tomorrow morning, the office, the thing Doss won't trust to anyone else.**

**The pay (the close).** Bill asked what he owed (per-engagement, not per-core; he'd settle at the end). Pearce priced it like a professional — folded in the cores, priced the metal light (read unfinished, owed the bench), and named a real licensed fee for the gatekeeper core (~tens of cr; ~55 all in). Bill paid it clean, unhaggled, and **topped it up** — framed as *correctness* (the licensed read is worth more than he charged), not charity. Pearce took it the way a craftsman takes a fair price honestly argued — restored a notch further: *out here, with his license, he can charge like the expert he is, and there's a man who'll pay it gladly* (the partnership's economic argument, lived not pitched).

**Result:** the partnership is no longer a thread Bill pulls — Pearce is reaching back. The portfolio stayed holstered (the meeting reached the dignity/work stage, not the business-structure stage); the great core stayed home (the wonder-card for the office). Bill walked out on a high, one beat early.

## EVENING — REASONING & THE DAY-24 PLAN

**The leak-as-alibi idea (reasoned to its wall, no mana spent on a failed draft).** Bill wondered if he could weld his scan into his leaky suppression weave — let the seepage read everything it touches, disguised as a green delver who can't seal. The literal weld *fails*: suppression bleeds *outward*; Forceread needs a *return path*; a leak can't carry a read home. But the reasoning taught the better shape: **the leak is a costume** (a green delver who can't suppress is the dullest read in the room — keep it, don't rush to seal) **and an alibi** (his soft-cone Forceread is the signal; the leak is noise that explains away any whisper of it — run them *paired*, the magician's two hands). A true always-on ambient scan is buildable but not worth it (costs the Passive seat Steadyhand now holds, runs continuous mana, reads as *more* detectable). Banked as a known option.

**The Day-24 plan (a sequence and kit, not a script).** Tomorrow at the office: lead with the great forge-core and let the *work* do the arguing; *receive* Pearce's opening rather than pitch over it; have the pieces ready in order — **dignity first** (equals, his name senior), **guild held close** (Doss's offer, only if he reaches for it), **concrete legs as answers** (the assay-and-craft house / the craft loop / the patents — one at a time, when he asks "how would it work"), **portfolio as a possible close** (his valuation makes it concrete — bring it, don't commit to playing it). And **hold the line against signing** — let the *shape* form, leave the *structure* for a third conversation; the man's already pulling, so don't get in its way.

Bill banked the stove, set the great core out for morning, and turned in. The night's rest refills the drawn-down pool toward full.
