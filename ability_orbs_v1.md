# ABILITY ORBS — CATALOG (v1)

*Worldbuilding reference for the Frontier Fantasy Solo RPG. Consistent with `game_bible.md` (the canon and tiebreaker, including its voice rules). Ability orbs grant **Skills** — sorted into the window's four categories (§13); the orbs below are all **Active** or **Movement**, since the Passive end (auras) is its own catalog (`auras_v1.md`). They draw on mana, are found inside dungeon monsters and almost never inside wild-spawned ones, **stay outside the domain gate** (no affinity needed — see `magic_domains_v1.md` §2), and are subject to USBD/guild licensing. An awakened person can feel what an orb does by handling it; an unawakened person cannot.*

*Format per entry: name · rarity · category · factual effect and cost · monster origin · legal status.*

---

## Seating, requirements, and the orb trade

- **Category.** Each orb grants an **Active** or **Movement** Skill, tagged per entry by its main function. Active orbs seat in the Intellect-sized active kit; the Movement orbs (Surefoot, Slow Fall, Light Step, Adhesion, Phase Step, Burst) take the **one Movement seat** a delver holds at a time, so they compete with each other (`ability_framework_v1.md`).
- **Requirements.** An orb carries **requirements that must be met to seat it** — fixed when it is found and readable before purchase by a trained assayer's Inspect (§13). As a rule, Common orbs ask little, Uncommon a modest attribute floor in their lane, and Rare a real attribute floor and often a **Delver Level floor** (the same number that gates tier entry, §12). A powerful orb can be bought and still sit useless until its floor is met; the figure is set per orb when it is read.
- **The trade.** A Skill a delver holds can be unseated, and when it is it **crystallizes back into an orb that carries its level** — kept in reserve or sold. A level-5 orb is worth far more than a level-1, since the buyer is buying the use-time already spent. This is what fills the orb market (§17): found and granted Skills trade freely; grown intrinsics, learned spells, and Bill's drafted abilities do not orb out (`ability_framework_v1.md` §8).

## Common

### Life Read

*Category: Active.*
Reads the physical state of a living target the user focuses on: rough condition, injuries, remaining stamina. Low use cost, a few mana per read. Reads bodies only — not lies, intent, or thought. Detects something like a favored old injury or low stamina, nothing mental. Drawn from predator beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted. Logged at the cage without comment. Common and cheap; standard for guild appraisers and working delvers. Sells openly.

### Surefoot

*Category: Movement.*
While active, the user does not lose footing on unstable or slick surfaces — scree, wet rock, a rotten rung, a narrow beam. Drains mana steadily while sustained; most users pulse it rather than hold it. Grants no strength or speed, and does not prevent a fall when the surface itself fails — only slipping. Drawn from climbing and burrowing beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted, cheap. Valued by prospectors and underground delvers. No USBD interest.

### Darksight

*Category: Active.*
The user sees in near-total darkness as if in dim light, in grey tones with no color. Low sustained mana cost. Does not function in absolute lightlessness — it amplifies faint light rather than creating sight from nothing — and offers no protection against sudden bright light, which briefly overwhelms it. Drawn from cave-dwelling beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted, cheap. Common among underground delvers. No restriction.

### Steady Hand

*Category: Active.*
Removes tremor and fatigue-shake from the user's hands while active, holding them level and precise. Low sustained cost. Aids fine work — fitting, drawing a bead, threading, suturing — but adds no skill the user lacks and no strength; it only steadies what skill is already there. Drawn from ambush-predator beasts that hold still before striking.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted. Valued by crafters, surgeons, and marksmen alike. Cheap.

### Carrying Voice

*Category: Active.*
Throws the user's voice clearly across a long distance to a chosen direction or point, without raising volume at the source. Low per-use cost. Does not improve the user's hearing and does not work both ways — it sends, it does not receive. Drawn from beasts that signal across distance.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted, cheap. Used by crew leaders and foremen.

### Scent Track

*Category: Active.*
Lets the user follow a specific scent trail by will, perceiving it as a visible direction that fades with the trail's age. Moderate sustained cost. Confused by water, crowds, and competing strong scents; useless on a cold trail. Grants no general improvement to smell. Drawn from tracking beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted. Valued by trackers and bounty crews. Cheap to modest.

### Slow Fall

*Category: Movement.*
For a few seconds after activation, the user falls slowly and lands without injury from heights that would otherwise harm them. Per-use cost, paid at activation. A short window only — it must be triggered during the fall, has a height ceiling above which it merely reduces rather than negates harm, and does nothing against being thrown into something. Drawn from gliding and arboreal beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted. Modestly priced; popular with shaft and cliff delvers.

### Iron Lungs

*Category: Active.*
The user can hold their breath far longer than normal and is unharmed by thin or stale air. Low sustained cost while held. Does not protect against actively poisonous gas — only lack of air — and does not let the user breathe liquid. Drawn from diving and burrowing beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted, cheap. Valued in flooded and gas-prone sites.

### Calloused

*Category: Active.*
While active, the user's skin resists abrasion, heat, cold, and small cuts — enough to grip a hot line, push through thorns, or handle rough stone without tearing. Low sustained cost. Stops scrapes and minor harm, not blades, bullets, or real force. Drawn from thick-hided beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted, cheap. Treated as a labor aid, not a combat ability.

### Pack Sense

*Category: Active.*
While active, the user knows the rough position and state — alive, hurt, distressed — of a small number of people they have bonded the ability to. Moderate sustained cost. Short to medium range, no communication, no reading of thought; it tells where and roughly how, not what. Drawn from herd and pack beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted. Valued by crews and families that delve together.

### Light Step

*Category: Movement.*
While active, the user moves with almost no sound and leaves little trace. Moderate sustained cost. Muffles the user's own movement only — it does not silence equipment that rattles, voices, or anything else, and it grants no invisibility. Drawn from stalking beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, flagged as a combat-adjacent ability in the user's file. Not restricted, but noted. Modest value.

### Heat Ward

*Category: Active.*
The user is unharmed by ambient high heat — a hot passage, proximity to flame, scalding steam — for as long as it is sustained. Moderate sustained cost. Protects against heat, not direct fire damage from a strong source, and not at all against cold. Drawn from heat-dwelling beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted. Valued near volcanic and forge-themed sites. Modest.

### Cold Ward

*Category: Active.*
The user is unharmed by ambient extreme cold — a frozen passage, deep chill, killing wind — for as long as it is sustained. Moderate sustained cost. Protects against cold, not direct frost damage from a strong source, and not at all against heat. Drawn from cold-dwelling beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted. Valued near ice-themed sites. Modest.

## Uncommon

### Keen Edge

*Category: Active.*
Augments any blade the user holds to cut deeper and hold its edge longer than the steel otherwise would. Augments the user's grip, not a specific weapon, so it works on any blade and travels with the user. Moderate mana per strike, more if pushed. No effect on firearms. Drawn from clawed or armored beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable as a combat ability — small extra fee, flagged in the user's file. Not restricted. A few hundred credits at the cage, more in private sale.

### Essence Sense

*Category: Active.*
Detects dungeon essence at close range; stronger sources register more strongly and directionally. Locates essence stones, warns of a nearby monster before line of sight, indicates richer areas of a site. Cheap to pulse, expensive to sustain. Short range. Reads presence only, never identity. Drawn from scavenger beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted, valuable. One property: a strong source registers clearly, and an essence-sink registers as an absence — a wrongness where presence is expected.

### Piercing Strike

*Category: Active.*
A struck blow ignores a portion of armor or hide, driving force through to what lies beneath. Mana per strike, scaling with how much penetration is forced. Works through any weapon or the user's own body; adds penetration, not raw force, so a weak blow that gets through is still a weak blow. Drawn from horned and tusked beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable as a combat ability, flagged. Not restricted. Holds value.

### Power Strike

*Category: Active.*
A struck blow lands with markedly greater force than the user's strength would produce. Mana per strike, more for a heavier blow. Adds force, not penetration or accuracy; a glancing or badly aimed hit gains nothing useful. Cannot be sustained — each strike is paid for separately. Drawn from large impact-driven beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable as a combat ability, flagged. Not restricted. Holds value.

### Quick Draw

*Category: Active.*
For a brief burst, the user's speed in drawing and bringing a weapon to bear is greatly increased. Per-use cost at activation. Affects the draw and presentation only, not sustained combat speed, accuracy, or reflexes; a fast draw onto a bad aim is still a bad aim. Drawn from strike-fast beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable as a combat ability, flagged. Not restricted. Sought by sidearm users.

### Second Wind

*Category: Active.*
On activation, the user sheds a measure of fatigue and pain and recovers stamina, becoming briefly fresh. Significant per-use cost, and a long cooldown before it can be used again. Recovers stamina, not injury — wounds remain, and the relief from pain is temporary and can mask harm. Drawn from endurance beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted. Valued by anyone who delves long. Modest to good value.

### Blood Clot

*Category: Active.*
On activation, the user's own bleeding slows and clots rapidly, closing minor wounds and stabilizing worse ones. Per-use cost. Stops bleeding; it does not mend the underlying injury, restore lost blood, or work on another person. Overuse to push through serious wounds risks the user collapsing once it lapses. Drawn from regenerating beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted. Valued highly relative to price; common in crew kits.

### Adhesion

*Category: Movement.*
While active, the user can grip and climb sheer surfaces — vertical rock, smooth walls, an overhang — with hands and feet. Moderate sustained cost. Bears the user's own weight and a modest load, not more; a sudden shock or a crumbling surface still drops them. No effect on actively repelling or frictionless surfaces. Drawn from wall-climbing beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted. Valued by vertical-site delvers. Modest.

### Muffle

*Category: Active.*
The user can deaden sound within a small area around a chosen point for a short time — a forced lock, a snapped branch, a struggle. Moderate per-use cost. Affects a small fixed area, not the user in motion, and a sound louder than the ability can hold still leaks through. Drawn from silent ambush beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, flagged combat-adjacent. Not restricted, but its uses draw attention; a person with this on their papers may be asked why. Good value.

### Threat Sense

*Category: Active.*
The user feels hostile intent directed at them before it acts — a held breath behind a door, a drawn bead, a beast about to break cover. Moderate sustained cost. Warns of intent toward the user only; it gives direction and imminence, not identity, count, or what waits without ill intent. Easily flooded in a melee where everything means harm. Drawn from prey-beasts with acute danger sense.

*Legal:* Registrable, flagged combat-adjacent. Not restricted. Valued and pricey.

### Mark Quarry

*Category: Active.*
The user marks a single target they can perceive; for a time after, they sense that target's direction and rough distance even out of sight. Per-use cost to mark, low sustained cost to track. One mark at a time, limited duration and range, broken by the target crossing certain barriers. Tracks position, nothing else. Drawn from pursuit predators.

*Legal:* Registrable, flagged combat-adjacent. Not restricted, but flagged uses include manhunting; noted by marshals. Good value.

### Steady Aim

*Category: Active.*
While sustained, the user's aim with a ranged weapon holds true against their own movement, breath, and minor disturbance, tightening at distance. Moderate sustained cost. Steadies the shot; it does not guide the projectile, account for a moving target, or grant marksmanship the user lacks. Drawn from beasts that strike accurately at range.

*Legal:* Registrable as a combat ability, flagged. Not restricted. Sought by shooters; holds value.

### Spit Venom

*Category: Active.*
The user can produce and project a short stream of a paralytic or caustic toxin from the hand or mouth a short distance. Per-use cost, scaling with potency. Short range, limited volume before it must recover, and a hardy or armored target may shrug it off; the toxin is the user's own and affects them if mishandled. Drawn from venomous beasts.

*Legal:* **Restricted.** Registrable only with a combat permit and toxin endorsement; unlicensed possession is an offense. The kind of orb people sell on the side rather than at the cage.

### Stonework

*Category: Active.*
The user can soften and reshape a small volume of natural stone or earth by touch, then let it set again, over the course of a sustained effort. Heavy sustained cost. Works on natural stone and earth only — not worked masonry, metal, or ward-stone — slowly, on a small volume at a time, and the result is no stronger than ordinary stone. Drawn from burrowing and tunneling beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted. Valued by miners, sappers, and shelter-builders. Good value.

### Glamour Trinket

*Category: Active.*
The user can make a small held object briefly appear to be a different small object of similar size, to the eye only. Moderate sustained cost. Sight alone — it does not change weight, texture, sound, or function, fails under touch, and cannot disguise anything larger than what a hand closes around. Drawn from mimic-type aberrations.

*Legal:* Registrable, flagged. Not restricted, but its obvious use in fraud means a flag and the occasional hard question. Modest to good value.

## Rare

### Phase Step

*Category: Movement.*
On activation, the user becomes briefly intangible and passes through solid matter a short distance before solidifying. Heavy per-use cost and a real cooldown. The window is brief and the distance short; solidifying inside solid matter is fatal, so the user must clear the far side, and nothing can be carried through that is not part of the user and their close gear. Drawn from spectral beasts.

*Legal:* **Restricted.** Registrable only under a high-tier permit; prized for escape and infiltration, and read accordingly. Rarely sold openly; commands a high private price.

### Lend Vigor

*Category: Active.*
The user can transfer a measure of their own stamina and recovery to a person they touch, easing fatigue and quickening another's natural healing over time. Significant sustained cost drawn from the user. Gives only what the user spends — it costs them what it grants, cannot transfer injury away, and does no true healing, only support. Drawn from symbiotic pack beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted, highly valued. One of the few orbs the Church and guild medics actively bid for. Expensive.

### Iron Skin

*Category: Active.*
While active, the user's skin and the flesh beneath harden, turning aside blows, blades, and lesser impacts that would otherwise wound. Heavy sustained cost. Reduces harm, never negates it; a strong enough force, a precise thrust to a gap, or sustained punishment still gets through, and the user is slower and tires faster while it holds. Drawn from heavily armored beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable as a combat ability, flagged, and noted at higher tiers. Not restricted. Expensive and sought by front-line delvers.

### Burst

*Category: Movement.*
On activation, the user moves with a single explosive burst of speed over a short distance — a lunge, a closing rush, an evasion. Heavy per-use cost, short cooldown. One burst per activation, short range, and the user must control the stop; the ability grants the speed, not the footing or the landing. Drawn from ambush sprinters.

*Legal:* Registrable as a combat ability, flagged. Not restricted. Expensive.

### Wither Touch

*Category: Active.*
The user's touch drains vitality from a living target, weakening it and briefly bolstering the user. Heavy per-use cost. Requires sustained skin or near-skin contact, works poorly through armor, and returns far less than it takes; against a strong target the contact needed is dangerous to hold. Drawn from undead-category creatures.

*Legal:* **Restricted.** Registrable only under a combat permit with a hazard endorsement, and viewed with suspicion by the Church given its origin. Sold privately and dear, away from the cage.

### Read the Room

*Category: Active.*
The user perceives the emotional state and rough intent of people within sight — fear, deception, aggression, calm — as impressions, not words. Moderate sustained cost. Reads feeling and disposition, not specific thoughts or facts; a disciplined or trained mind reads as calm regardless of truth, and strong ambient emotion drowns out fine detail. Drawn from social-predator beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, flagged. Not restricted, but valued in negotiation, law, and confidence work alike, and treated warily. Expensive.

### Hold Wound

*Category: Active.*
On activation, the user suspends the worsening of their own injuries for a time — bleeding, poison spread, shock held in stasis rather than reversed. Heavy per-use cost, strict duration. It buys time, nothing more; when it lapses, everything held resumes at once, so it is survival until real aid, not a cure, and misjudging the window kills. Drawn from creatures that endure mortal damage.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted, highly valued. A last-resort orb; medics and deep-site crews pay well for it.

### False Face

*Category: Active.*
While active, the user can subtly alter their own features, voice, and bearing enough to pass as someone else of similar build, or as no one memorable. Heavy sustained cost. Alters appearance within the user's own frame — it cannot change size or mass, fool someone who knows the person intimately, or survive close physical scrutiny, and it holds nothing once dropped. Drawn from shapeshifting beasts.

*Legal:* **Restricted.** Registrable only under special license; unlicensed possession is a serious offense given its use in fraud and evasion. Almost never sold openly; among the more valuable orbs on the private market.

### Telekinetic Grip

*Category: Active.*
The user can grasp and move an object at a distance as if with an unseen hand, within sight and short range. Heavy sustained cost. The grip is roughly as strong as one of the user's own hands and no stronger; it tires quickly, manages one object at a time, and cannot finely manipulate what the user could not handle by hand. Drawn from spectral and aberration-category creatures.

*Legal:* Registrable, flagged. Not restricted. Broadly useful and broadly coveted; expensive.

### Predator's Pace

*Category: Active.*
While sustained, the user's reflexes and short-burst speed are heightened — quicker reactions, faster hands, a half-step ahead of the moment. Heavy sustained cost that mounts the longer it runs. Sharpens reaction and speed, not strength, judgment, or endurance; held too long it burns the user out badly, and it cannot be leaned on through a long fight. Drawn from apex pursuit predators.

*Legal:* Registrable as a combat ability, flagged and noted at higher tiers. Not restricted. Among the more expensive combat orbs.
