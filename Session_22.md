## What happened

### Morning — System bench work (home)
Bill brought up a blank Drafting space for the first time deliberately and confirmed its nature: empty by default, it renders only what he understands, and it never volunteers anything. Reasoning toward a quieter long-range weapon (the delve's report having bothered him), he discarded crossbow, spring, and compressed-air designs in favor of a **mana-driven coilgun** — heavy steel chisel/bodkin **darts** drawn and parked in the build space, truncated rifle furniture kept (stock, short barrel, sights, trigger), powder/primer/smoke/flash/fouling discarded.

He tried to push raw mana through a wire as electricity and **failed** — mana is the fuel for *powers*, not itself a current; ~4–5 mana bled off as formless heat. He then built a **galvanic cell** at the kitchen table (copper, zinc scavenged from galvanized scrap, vinegar electrolyte) to run the wrapped-nail **electromagnet**, and *studied it working* — closing the knowledge gap: a current makes a circulating field, a coil stacks it, an iron core carries it, the field exists only while the current flows, and it can be switched by switching the flow.

With the gap filled, he **drafted a "mana-cut: electromagnet" converter-weave** — a will-controlled electromagnet, complete and priced (creation ~30, use ~2/activation or ~1/min sustained). He confirmed his suppression weave is itself a converter, and that weaves share a **grammar** (intake → fold → output-cut), each output its own "cut."

### The guild hall
**Re-registered — awakened, cored, classed.** The old "tested closed" assay is now dated history. Hessler processed it warmly (and reordered Bill upward a notch — a fix-it man who can *suppress* and asks how to *craft for the hall*). Bill sat the **re-assay (300 cr, billed to tab)**, and while the apparatus read him, he read *it* with Examine — capturing the working principle of a core-and-mana detector ("push a structured field, read the bend"); the runework heart of it rendered as a blank, gated behind rune study he hasn't done. He sat at **natural rest** on Hessler's counsel (the weave eased from "banked fire" to natural — not flared, not clamped — so his paper matches what he usually reads as), and profiled as an ordinary middling cultivator. Confirmed in play: the System/window is undetectable by the assay.

Hessler laid out the **rune/enchant-maker road** (cored ✓ / years of rune *study* + a teacher / the inscription license at Saint Orison, last) and named **Pearce** repeatedly without knowing he was handing Bill a thread. Bill read the hall's **buy book** (the *floor* — what the Argent paid recently): niche construct cores ~30–60 ea; the gatekeeper core mid-hundreds (~150–200+); the low sensor chip single-digits-to-low-tens; the **unclassified frame-metal scrap-plus until named**; the great forge-core **off the book entirely** (one-buyer / believed-price item, confirmed not-for-floor-sale). He bought **three level-1 utility orbs as STUDY SPECIMENS** (Light, a Mend/patch-tool, a Surefoot) for ~120 cr.

### Home — bench study
Bill **STORED** the Light weave clean (the first stranger's weave he's fully captured — weave-reading rung 2) and the patch-tool weave (with more effort); **partially read** the Surefoot orb and learned that movement is a *different grammar* than the converter family. He **refined his own magnetism-cut** against the polished Light weave (a tighter fold; creation cost ticked down). He opened a new project — **can compatible weaves be spliced into Skills that don't yet exist** — and reframed it from "stir together" to "splice within a family first."

### Afternoon — the silent gun, designed
Bill had the design's central leap: **magnetism pulls, it doesn't shove.** He reframed the coilgun from "one violent pulse" to a **staged succession of timed coil-grabs** — each coil waking ahead of the dart, dying before it can anchor, the speed living in the *succession* rather than the strength, the timing intervals crowding closer toward the muzzle as the dart accelerates. The void in the draft **closed** — knowledge earned by *reasoning* from things he already knew (the captured nail, the relay, the ratchet, the music-box barrel), not a field trip. He designed the full weapon: a **manually-levered, spring-fed box-magazine repeating coilgun** firing identical guided iron darts, accelerated by a coil procession timed off a **music-box-style timing drum**, the whole thing driven by his own seated weave — **fireable by no one but him.** He spec'd the magazine in detail (beneath-receiver box, follower and spring, feed lips, a carrier with a half-barrel centering groove, a critical retaining pawl to prevent double-feed, anti-rotation guide grooves, up-feed straight into the bore). He drafted it on paper with the **mana/drive left off the page** — on paper it reads as a dull clockwork-and-coil curiosity.

### The gunsmith
Bill brought the coilgun plates to his building partner for a **half-commission** build (like the speedloader mule). The gunsmith loved it — the **timing drum** is the hook ("that's the watch — where this lives or dies"). When he asked the power-source question, Bill answered with the **truth**: it runs off his own essence, fires only in his hands. No lie needed — it read as ordinary cultivator gear, and the gunsmith filed it under "cultivator gun" without needing to know what Bill actually is. The gunsmith named the silent-gun social hazard himself and pledged discretion. **First push: a drum stub plus a short coil section, proved by hand for clean breaks and then by Bill for the actual pull, before building the full gun.** Word in a few days.

### Calloway's office
Bill set up an **instruction-ledger / drawing authorization** against the gift-trust — he can now draw by instruction (amount and purpose logged under privilege), with cash for daily life and trust-drafts-to-named-payees for big purchases. Calloway steered him firmly off a personal checkbook (it would map him) and explained the right instruments for each kind of spending. Bill started a **workshop LEASE** search (alteration rights to be written in) and — on Calloway's counsel — **dropped the forge** from the lease (a private forge is loud, fire-feared, trade-implying, and Bureau-adjacent; held as a separate later decision). He **drew 1,000 cr** for near-term purposes (gift-trust now ~3,100; cash on hand ~1,160). The bank box is still in progress (~1–2 weeks); no new remittance landed.

### Evening — the patent portfolio
Bill considered apprenticing under Pearce and, reading the man, decided against it (a proud, broke man hears "apprentice me" as charity or purchase). He landed on a **business partnership of equals** instead, and built the plan: an assay-and-craft house with Pearce as the senior public name on what he's senior at, the craft loop (Bill delves and makes, Pearce assays and certifies), a guild tie-in that could restore Pearce's standing, and — the clever leg — **co-owned patents under Quest Instruments with Pearce's name at the top**, income routed through the Quest side while Pearce draws named, public, respectable income. He drew up a **four-design patent portfolio** (the lift catch, the hand-warmer, the speedloader, the self-feeding stove — the bright-but-explicable inventions only) to bring Pearce **to assay and value**, as the key that opens the partnership. The **coilgun is deliberately excluded** and will never go on a public record.

He banked the day and slept.

- **Drafting + the knowledge gate** — the space renders only what Bill understands; gaps render as voids; he closed a void by *reasoning* (legitimate — earned understanding, not a fetched fact).
- **Examine on a complex device** — read the assay apparatus's working principle; the runework heart blanked behind rune study (honest "no referent").
- **Weave-reading** — SEE (rung 1, natural/early) and STORE (rung 2) proven on external orbs; UPGRADE applied to his own draft (refined the magnetism-cut against the Light weave). Movement weaves are a different grammar than converter weaves.
- **Limits-as-seeds pricing** — narrower scope = cheaper creation and use; there are two roads to cheaper (craft, with a floor; and limits, which trade capability). The dedicated coilgun drive is far cheaper than the general magnet by its good limits.
- **Slotting rules** — 3 drafting slots at Lv.1 (separate from universal Skill seats); a slotted ability can't be edited in place (only dissolved over a day with sunk creation mana, or re-slotted by paying creation again); abilities level 1–5 by meaningful use under stakes; there is a hidden ceiling/branch past Lv.5 Bill can sense but not read.
- **Choosing-locked Skills** can still reach Bill as orbs (the Surefoot orb proves the side door).
