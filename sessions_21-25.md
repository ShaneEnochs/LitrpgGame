# ARC 5 — DAYS 21–25: THE AWAKENING, AND THE MAKER'S ROAD
*(Consolidated, lore-corrected narrative of Sessions 21–25 — the campaign's hinge. History layer — current values in the JSONs, current threads in `campaign_state.md`, NPC detail in `npcs/`, System mechanics in `game_bible.md` and the catalogs. The one big lore-correction: the rarity scale introduced on Day 25 was the old six-tier version; current canon is the **four-tier** scale — Common/Uncommon/Rare (each Low/Mid/High) and **Unique** — with Legendary/Mythic as power-tags on Unique and Epic retired. The durable rule below — a Unique growth item reads *family, not contents* — is what carried over.)*

## The throughline

Twenty-one days of an unawakened fix-it man ended in a single morning. On the road to the named Reach survey Bill walked into a fresh off-map delve, killed a machine, and woke up a **System Engineer** — and spent the next four days learning what that means, turning Pearce from a courtship into a partner, mapping the road to bind his maker-core, and signing onto a Platinum crew bound for a delve that doubles as his fuel run. The dull-fixer cover is over; what replaces it is a registered awakened maker who signs his name on purpose.

## The awakening (Day 21)

Riding the guild rig to the named survey, Bill spotted off the road a **fresh, off-map arch** that hadn't been there on the Day-7 baseline — a **Special Foundation delve, occupancy one**. He went down alone through the blue barrier.

Inside were dressed-stone galleries and **constructs** — tireless, pattern-bound machines, ideal quarry for a man who reads mechanisms. He read the first Sentinel's single load-path with care, dropped its gait at the hip joint and killed it through the sensor housing — and its core opened. **The System booted: Class System Engineer, Level 1.** The Choosing offered Inspect, Surefoot, or **Forceread**; he took Forceread and locked the other two away forever. He pushed his boot points into Precision and Intellect, carrying both *through* the unawakened human ceiling — the first thing only a man who reads his own figures would know to do. He sheared a Gatekeeper's loaded joints and drew its **core** out intact, emptied the warming niche-constructs, and found the chamber that explained the whole place: a **forge** drinking concentrated essence up a shaft and compacting it into cores — *how cores are made* — feeding the constructs. He shed its load, waited out the wind-down, and drew the **great central forge-core** from its cradle. The forge died; the Special completed.

At the shaft he cultivated on the concentrated essence, packed his core dense, felt his **first gate** strain — and **deliberately did not open it**, so it would lock later at a higher Intellect (the patient play, visible only to a man reading the numbers). The completion payout granted the **Schematic** intrinsic (studied mechanisms kept as recallable internal drawings), more attribute points, and the identification of the great forge-core as a **class-resonant growth item** — a tool only a System Engineer could bind and carry. **The maker-core.** He copied the arch runes, took a third sample of the strange frame-metal from a halted dig, and walked out; the Special collapsed behind him, and barely any time had passed outside.

## The Reach, read with an open core (Day 21)

He drove on to the survey. The workings read **dead-sterile**, and his now-open core felt a steady essence **draw toward the mouth**. A **writ-control post** logged him in a book that is *not* Doss's. The survey, laid over the Day-7 baseline, ran all three columns worse and worsening toward the mouth — and with his core Bill felt the inner seal **warm, under live load, and tended**, holding something that pushes, with an "occupied nothing" past it. The draw leaned harder toward his full core, as if it had **noticed** him.

## The Doss alliance, and core suppression (Day 21)

Back at the cage, Doss felt his raw open core at once — yesterday he'd read as nothing — and Bill led with the awkward truth (awakened on the road; here are the witnesses), which earned the trust. Behind a shut door they became **covert allies**. Bill laid out the whole human layer — Amos missing and Esther's disbelief, the depot and awning watchers, the first crew paid off through the cage by a man who wasn't guild, the attempted Esther payoff — and his felt thesis: the place **takes people and gives back something not quite them.** Doss corroborated (Garlan, who led descent one and reads *wrong* to her; the clock discrepancy — a cheerful "crew alive" check-in arriving after her gut says they were gone) and crossed over. The plan: bury the survey as leverage, triage people over the slow walled hole, **protect Esther and Rusk**, Bill the hands and Doss the guild-eyes, never seen together — and Bill must **damp his core.** He learned **suppression** by reading her flare-and-damp twice, found his own lattice weave, and refined it that night. The legal road: re-register awakened, register the haul, and get the unidentifiable great core to a dungeon-licensed assayer — the town's old one, **Pearce, retired** (Doss wished aloud she could get him back on a guild payroll).

## The System, learned (Days 22–25)

Across the next days Bill explored what he'd become, mostly at his own bench:
- **Drafting** renders only what he understands; gaps show as voids he closes by *reasoning*, not by being handed facts. **Examine** reads a device's working principle (the runework heart blanks until he studies runes). **Weave-reading** runs SEE → STORE → REPLICATE → UPGRADE; he stored several utility weaves off bought orbs and refined his own.
- He **re-registered awakened** at the guild — the old "tested closed" assay now dated history — and confirmed the System/window is **undetectable**: at natural rest he profiles as an ordinary middling cultivator. Hessler laid out the rune/enchant-maker road (cored, then years of rune study with a teacher, then the inscription license at Saint Orison) and kept naming Pearce without knowing it.
- He designed a **silent coilgun** off the core idea that *magnetism pulls, it doesn't shove* — a staged succession of timed coil-grabs driven by a music-box timing drum, firing guided iron darts, run off his own seated weave so it is **fireable by no one but him**. On paper the drive is left off; it reads as a dull clockwork curiosity. The build began at the gunsmith's (half-commission), the gunsmith filing it under "cultivator gun" without needing to know what Bill is.
- He learned that **aspect lives in essence** while his mana is neutral, and built a first-generation **earth-aspect filter** (spending his earth cores) — lossy but his, and the key that makes the next filter fast. Canon set: **essence is raw fuel, mana the refined spendable pool, conversion the lossy refining step** that never raises the Intellect-set ceiling.
- He **seated Steadyhand** (a Level-2 passive that stills tremor — his first seated universal Skill) and drafted a **Standing Ward**.
- A standing-canon **rarity grade** for cores and materials was established (current four-tier form: Common/Uncommon/Rare, each Low/Mid/High, plus **Unique**). The load-bearing rule: a **Unique** growth item has no reference class, so a licensed read returns its *family/category, not its contents* — which is exactly why the great forge-core "wouldn't close" to a licensed read.

## Pearce: from courted to partner (Days 23–25)

The long courtship turned. At the Tuesday auction Bill read the delve market to its bones — assayed cores trade near floor while identical unread ones go for a third (*the stamp is most of the money* — the whole Pearce thesis), and combat orbs and domain tomes make hundreds-to-thousands and **leave the territory** the hour they sell. Then Pearce reached back: he read the gatekeeper core and the charm at the Drovers', and the next morning, at his own resurrected office, he read the **great forge-core** to the edge of his license (a made, keyed, binding-family growth item — category, not contents) and the **frame-metal** as *made stock* (essence worked into the bone of the metal — *"I read it. Somebody makes it."*).

When Pearce finally asked to know more, Bill answered with **trust in proportion, not facts** — *I won't tell you what the work is, not because I don't trust you but because of what it is; what I need is a man who can know a little, ask no more, put his name on what I set in front of him, and never mention it across a counter.* Pearce crossed something on that — *you'd trust me with the quiet before the facts, and the quiet's the harder thing* — and named his own terms: **equity, not wages; his licensed name on top where the law wants one.** **The word "partner" came out of Pearce's mouth.** The old charm turned out to be a wearable **suppression aid** (it damps Bill's leak from outside); Bill told him exactly what it did, a gift of trust larger than the sentence. The four-design **patent portfolio** (lift catch, hand-warmer, speedloader, self-feeding stove) became the funding leg — bright, explicable money from day one while the real work grows underneath.

The **shape was agreed by Pearce's hand; nothing signed** — built, not floated, with the structure left for a third conversation at **Calloway's table, the three of them, Friday 10:00.** The legs, settled with Calloway: **Quest Instruments, 50/50** (Bill's cash, goods, and patents valued against Pearce's license, name, and office as stake-in-kind); co-owned patents filed under Quest with both names, **Pearce's on top**; firm income split 50/50 with **each partner's private handwork his own**; all assay and all delve materials flow through the firm **without the firm acquiring any right to ask how or where Bill got them**; premises are Pearce's office (leased from him, so it stays his pride) plus Bill's workshop. Calloway's quiet engine: **bind his fortune to your silence, not his tongue** — equity forfeit and money clawed back if he talks, his name on every filing, so betrayal razes him too. The **coilgun is excluded forever** from any public record.

## The maker-core, mapped (Day 24)

Cycling fuel through the great core, Bill mapped its binding from the inside. It wants three things, all on the road he's already walking: **(1) a banked draught of essence delivered above a threshold** (a cup into a cold boiler does nothing); **(2) a higher standing** — a Delver-Level gate he sits a hair under, crossed by delve-completion points, not class level; **(3) the draught offered all at once, aspected if he can manage it** (a forge-kin core fed matching essence runs richer). And, setting it back in its canvas, he felt **the first faint stir** — a warmth that wasn't his own, *almost as if the thing noticed him noticing it.* The great core is **sapient**, waking slowly — the opening of the sapient-core-to-bonded-familiar arc (the delve cores world-wide are quietly waking, unknown to anyone).

## The world changes, and the Platinum road (Days 24–25)

The gates **changed their terms** (reported in the *Oregon Clarion*): party-size minimums by grade (Platinum 5, Gold 4, Silver 3), and examining an arch now returns a **quests box** naming tasks and posting set, non-money rewards in advance. No one knows the cause — and it rhymes, quietly, with the cores waking and Bill's own Monday clear.

Bill **signed forward to a tryout** with an all-Platinum crew — **Myles** (tank), **Charles** (healer), **Jason** (fighter) and **Julie** (mage), Jason and Julie siblings — who have cleared three Tier-1 Platinums and want a fifth with range. Hessler spent his standing to warn that they *barely make it back* — a healer hollowed out, the crew limping in days late calling it a good run because nobody died *that* time. Bill stepped over it eyes open: *"I came across the continent to do this. I want to hit the final tier. I want the secrets of these delves. Tin won't get me there; Platinum might."* The tryout leads to a **Saturday delve in the Emberwaste** — an infernal, fire-and-earth-thick Platinum site (open hellish exterior, five floors with bosses, roughly three days out), which doubles as Bill's **fuel run** for the filter and the binding.

Around it: Marguerite's **fourth and final** blade lesson crossed the one gate no yard could give — cutting a joint that *resists* and committing through it (Melee to 4, a new Butchery subskill, and her standing open-counter offer). The **speedloader and revolver finished and proved** (the banked firearm-mechanism advance landing), and the **coilgun build began** — it finishes and test-fires Friday, which will take the System Engineer class to **Level 2**. A seed planted for the delve: running mana through mundane steel **degrades** it, so Bill's revolver will fail underground and he'll want a new gun forged from the frame-metal *made stock*. And in the crowded hall Bill quietly clocked a too-quiet core that resolved into the most innocent person in the room — the **licensed assayer** at the guild counter (face filed, not flagged).
