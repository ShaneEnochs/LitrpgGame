## The day in order

**Morning — Sarah Maddox, the house answered.** Before Mass, Bill caught Sarah at the back door and answered the question she'd left standing: the house can keep the children warm and they can meet there if they want the room, but **ownership and responsibility are his — his risk, his neck, not the firm's to answer for.** She accepted it; a man who'll own the risk of a thing out loud was the answer she wanted. But she held her **gate firm** — any child she's not met comes to her first before he's under his roof or in his books, warm house or no — and noted that a house where grown plans get talked draws more eyes than one where tired children sleep, and the risk in that is his to carry, as he'd just said. Then she left for Mass.

**A pickle to Hessler.** Bill sent Eli with 2 cr for a Keller pickle, carried up to the cage ahead of him — the old gesture that softens the clerk's sour morning. (Pocket ~234 → ~232.)

**The new house — the firm's overnight use, and the key.** Bill found the house used and put back, kept discreet (no ticks or blankets showing — a tradesman's house, not a barracks), warm through. Tatch (house key on the table) and Min (firm book open) at the kitchen table; the rest scattered to posts. Tatch's report: locked sound, opened at first light, in and out by twos via the back lane, unmarked. **Bill told Tatch to keep the key and get a duplicate cut on firm funds** — a spare so a lost key never locks the firm out, the firm's standing in the house run on its own coin. Tatch: smith's shut Sunday, he'll cut it Monday at the low-end farrier (not the nearest — discretion).

**The books with Min.** Bill asked plainly: is the firm earning off the kids' learned skills, or still drawing down? Min's read, unsoftened: the firm hasn't gone backward, but the treasury's rise is two things only — the Garrick wheel job (112 net) and Bill's own 100 going out and back. Strip those and they'd be eating the pot. The recurring earned trade (Cobb's sharpening, the few salve tins at the cage, the odd small fix through the channel) is real but pennies against a week's wages. **Not yet self-sustaining on the children's skills.** Min: "I'd as soon we ate our own bread… we're learned enough to start earning and not earning enough to keep ourselves."

**The teaching plan (Quest Academy, designed).** Bill's move toward self-sufficiency, worked through with Tatch and Min: he **teaches the able kids who can then teach** (Cobb → edges/lamps, Tom-the-firm-trades… — by aptitude; Ada & Min → letters), and they carry it down the line — a firm that makes its own tradesmen without Bill in the room. **Shifts keep the watch lit** (can't pull the whole firm off-post during the rush). **Letters bound to the trade** (want a trade, sit for letters). **Money carried as investment on Min's books**, not charity. **The teacher question:** no school or schoolmistress to hire in New Abilene; the mission teaches letters but that's the "John" world (an alias wrinkle); Ada and Min can start the littles now; the clean outside teacher is the same person the school wants, a road that runs through Lessard's supper Day 18. **Trades for immediate money** (Bill's read): edges the floor (Cobb, cheap tools, days to a sellable edge, universal demand); then lamp/instrument cleaning (delver trade, Quest channel), maintenance rounds (shops/homes), wheel work (freight, slower); spread the kids so the market doesn't flood. **Front of the week makes teachers** (Tom up to teach a hub, the rounds method into a steady kid or two), then the relay runs. The gathering is **held for Bill's word.** Pem's outfit is a boss-to-boss Compact matter, not Bill's to hand directly.

**The guild hall.** Busier than a usual Sunday — the rush ignores the Sabbath; strangers thick at the contract board on the NW postings; a delver crew settling a bounty, one man swinging a full ore-sack one-handed (awakened day-labor, unremarked).
- **Hessler — the sample tins + the filing favor.** Bill gave Hessler two hand-lettered sample tins (comfrey/ache; yarrow/chapped skin), branding/die coming end of week, asked his true opinion. Hessler (chronic bad back) clocked the comfrey-for-ache as "cleverer than a bribe," pocketed both; **come Tuesday he'll know if it earns its tin.** (Proof tins ~5 → ~3.) Hessler's grumble: every crew wants gear certified before heading NW, the one certifier drowning, crews sitting two-three days on bench slots — **the Aldine clearance** sitting three weeks ("a clearance that doesn't get cleared… sits till it breaks"). Bill **hauled and sorted Hessler's dead certification-file crate** (sealed ones untouched) — and his Appraisal eye read the certification trade from the inside, its bottleneck sitting right where Quest's bench works. Hessler: "we're square and a hair past it — call on me when you want the hair." (Firmed to ally + a small owed goodwill.)
- **The hair collected.** Bill asked Hessler's help finding knife training; Hessler pointed him to **Dell** (already in the yard, Bill's to walk up to — no introduction needed; with Marguerite holding the cutting side, Bill has the knife covered twice over, and what he's lacked is **hours**). Gun training Bill already has (**Crane**). Bill offered to knock out **certifications Monday**; Hessler will stand him in front of the right arrangement, paper and pay both square — the rush backlog made into a paying job.
- **Payment reconciliation + new policy.** A books gap was found: the **Day-11 counter repairs** (a movement, a case spring, a fine gauge body) and the Day-10 lamp had never been priced. Settled as collected-through-the-channel and routed to the firm treasury (gauge body 18, movement 6, case spring 2, lamp 0 = **+26; firm treasury ~422 → ~448**). **New labor-routing policy (player-set, named as agreed at the morning meeting):** Bill's **expert labor — certification, instrument body-work, gunsmith-grade fixes — settles to his own pocket** now; the **firm earns off the kids' own trades.** Set straight with Hessler.

**Doss — the re-measure, and the Foundation fork.** Bill read her first (Insight): worn from being handled all morning, so the play was to **give, not take** — and he had the thing she wanted, the clean re-measure. He opened light, she eased and gave him a minute.
- **The re-measure: committed.** Doss wants it; she'll **arrange cover on the posted (land-company) ground** — more than a Sunday's work — and **sends for him when it's set.** The ground's changed: crew down (live op), the mouth's ground gone to a company posted private and guarded, the road full of strangers. Under her hand = a guild reason to be there but on her paper (its own cost); freelance = trespass where serious money watches.
- **The Foundation fork.** Nothing of the kind near; nearest registered is **the Pilgrim's Well** — east (rail then a day north, ~four days), held by its claim-owner, ~500 cr the entry, the book of names ran long since the standard wired. **You found where you first open your core, and nowhere after** — do an ordinary delve first and the Foundation option is spent. Bill's answer to "which is it": he came to delve and to understand these places, got pulled into making a living, hasn't started the job he came for; an opened core lets him study the delves, and a Foundation site is the best footing. Doss took it as an answer that fit him. **She'll write a letter of introduction** — an officer's honest vouch (his head, hands, steadiness; not delving she hasn't seen), a vouch not a key — **conditional on him getting ready first** (Crane's shooting in his hands, hours in the yard, be a body a small crew trusts in one hole). She writes it fresh when he's set; timing his. "Come back set, not soon."

**Esther (as John) — the recognition list handed over.** Bill walked to the Dunmore boarding house in the early dark, greeted her warmly, said straight he'd come only to check on her given the delve talk. Her grief turned to resolve: the town's treasure-talk, men cheering toward the hole that took Amos, crews going down again, no one asking after the lost — and she can't let it lie, and has no standing. **She handed over the recognition list — "the caraway things":** Amos salts twice before tasting; chews a caraway seed got from a German in the war; deals cards left though writes right; a scar on his left hand and the lie he tells about it; takes his coffee with too much chicory — "the things that don't change in a man, that a stranger wearing his face couldn't know to fake." Her request: be the one who asks after what became of Amos now crews are active, and keep the list so if he ever walks back up, someone besides her knows how to be sure it's truly him. **Bill promised he's already doing everything in his power to understand that place and what became of the men — no how, no Amos promised — and took the paper and thanked her** for the work of it. (The list is now in Bill's keeping — sensitive.)

**The Dunmore lift-works — the job, the partnership, the build started.** Bill brought **Holt and Cobb** to the Dunmore Brothers' freight-and-storage warehouse (the in-town posting, never taken). Introduced himself as Wyatt, the kids as apprentices; read the brother (worn-through, scraped past patience, won't be charmed). **Set the kids' safety roles first** (cage blocked on timber, drum chocked, nothing touched till the load can't move; Holt = securing/danger-discipline, Cobb = close work under his eye, both clear of the hoistway) — which is what told the brother Bill had run a hoist.
- **Inspection (taught):** the brake band lining greased from an over-oiled bearing above it (the six-inch drop); a worn upper guide shoe racking the cage off square (the fault never posted, the one that hurts someone); a single hemp rope holding it all.
- **The leap (Intellect):** a **rail-catch safety** — spring-loaded dogs held off the rails by the rope's tension; tension goes slack, the dogs throw into the rails and wedge the cage fast. Plus a drum pawl and a counterweight. Bill's discipline flagged the cost: a unique catch is *remarkable.*
- **The pitch + the demo:** Bill named the faults, then the safety; offered a **plain repair guide** so the brother's never chained to him (it's physics, universal); and offered to **stand under the loaded cage himself when the rope's cut.** The brother's terms: build it, hang a full load, cut the rope, let him see it bite — do that and the job's Bill's, and his mouth runs to every hauler.
- **The patent question** → Bill's plan: route it through **Malcolm** (not Calloway) — a short keyed alert wire East, drawings/spec by sealed letter behind the channel; if no prior art, a patent lawyer searches and files; **Bill the named inventor.** (Ordinary U.S. patent regime, not the USBD; novelty unknowable from here; a patent is loud, against his forgettable posture, and pulls against the open guide.)
- **Pricing + the partnership.** Bill ran the ranges (Intellect: unfair >400, fair 300–375, good-for-him 250–280) and priced it **good-for-him at ~265 all-in** (inspection folded in, repair, the safety build, the extras, the guide, parts at cost) to win the man and his word-of-mouth. With Insight he traded the premium for **standing arrangements** (each handed back as the brother's own gain): **Quest Remedies distribution on his freight runs** (cut TBD), **firm gear-maintenance** (terms to set → firm income), **storage** of Quest's bulk goods off Bill's house — all **contingent on the cut-rope proof.**
- **Handshake → written opinion squared first → the work started.** Bill shook on it, set the written opinion down (faults, repair + safety build, the standing arrangements named beneath) and both marked it. Then the teardown, taught the whole way (Holt's danger-discipline drilled; Cobb chased a fault to its cause). The **safety build can't finish today** (spring-steel dogs and counterweight to source); parts list set.
- **Calling it — bonuses + lists.** Bill worked till the light tipped, called it, **gave Holt and Cobb 10 cr each** out of pocket (above firm wages, money to eat well). Split the parts lists — **mercantile to Holt, blacksmith to Cobb** — to go in first thing Monday (both shops keep the Sabbath).

**The Drovers' — Pearce, the fourth pass, a real corner turned.** Bill came **early** this time (in Pearce's good first-hour window, sober, the pride taut but no maudlin edge). He ordered a rye, opened the notebook to the lift design, and worked it for himself — Pearce's eye snagged on the mechanism and stayed. Bill cut the "washing your day away" edge, gave a soft intro ("came here for his own quiet"), and offered **a round for ten minutes of his opinion** — a deal between technical men. Pearce: "Keep your round. You can buy it after… I'll not be paid up front for talk," and turned the notebook to his own eye.
- **Pearce's read (real assayer's work):** the notion's sound, the geometry honest — but two gaps. A **spring sits cocked under load for months in a dirty freight house; the temper creeps, takes a set you'll never see, and you're staking a man's life it throws full and even after a year idle.** And: does the catch grab gentle, or stop the cage dead and tear the rails out of the wall? "Clever — not the other thing. But you've got a metal problem dressed as a mechanism problem."
- **Bill's leap off the critique:** stop asking the spring to grip. Mount the dogs as **weight-driven wedges** — cam-faces angled so the cage's own fall drives the wedge *tighter* into the rail (self-feeding), the spring reduced to a nudge into first contact; a winter-weakened spring can still nudge. Shape the taper so it bites **progressively** (grips over a few inches, spares the spine and the works). The trigger fails safe (rope-tension holds the cams off; slack *sets* the catch). Built to be tripped and checked on a schedule with witness marks. Then the honest edge: "that's metal, and metal's past my trade."
- **Pearce engaged.** He named the metallurgy plainly (no metal is both strong and warning; hard steel/spring temper snaps clean without notice; soft wrought iron *draws* and warns; fatigue turns good iron crystalline; pick the duty to the metal — hard grip-face, tough wrought life-bearing parts; case-harden a tough body) — and that **you can't tell true stock from a bar that'll betray you by looking; you want a man who can read the metal.** He heard himself name his own dead trade as the one thing standing between the catch and a man on the floor.
- **The toast + the consult.** Bill bought the round (his on Bill, "the man earned it"; the bought rye Pearce *drank*, where an hour before he'd have left it to make a point) and toasted **the principle** — you can't tell the true ones by looking, you load them up and see which draw and which snap cold. Then Bill asked him plainly: **sit an hour as a paid consultant, read the actual stock on the rack** so the build's right and safe — "when I told that freight man I'd stand under it, I was counting the physics, not the metallurgy; that was a blind spot from lack of experience." Pearce agreed: **Monday, at the blacksmith, reading the bars before they're bought; fee paid after, by value.** Find him via the Drovers' first, the shut assay office (his back room) second; runner sent only when Bill's walking to the smith, midday or after; bring the notebook.
- **The books.** Bill said straight he'd take a look through Pearce's stockpile sometime, lend or sell, then backed off (no pressure; the consulting alone helps vastly). Pearce: **"Sell — no."** ("A man sells the last of his trade, that's the day he agrees it's over.") But: **"Lendin's a different word… I'll think on it. That's not a no, and it's not yes-come-tomorrow. Get your lift standing and your metal read true first."** And: "a man that actually wants 'em is the first I've met. I'll not forget you asked it straight." The deepest step in the courtship yet — his trade handed back on his own terms.

**Close — the new house, first night.** Bill caught that the **blacksmith list (Cobb's) is now the old spring design** and put a **hold** on it through Tatch (nothing ordered off it; redraw the spec, buy at the rack with Pearce reading the metal; the mercantile list stands). Home to his own warm house, barred the door, **locked Esther's list in the house strongbox** for the night (its long-term hiding place — and possibly Calloway's box — an open to-do; the ciphered notebooks are still in the Maddox window casing, not yet moved). First night under his own roof; slept well.
