- **THE COIL GUN PROVED.** The gunsmith finished it overnight (Bill's pencilled change made real — first coil-stop moved in). With the gunsmith watching at the bench (door bolted, shop empty), Bill bridged his core to the drive eyes-open and fired the first live shot: **silent**, no report/flash/smoke, just the drive's tick-snap and a swaged slug buried deep in the sand butt. **Works perfectly, exactly as drawn** (player's standing ask honored).
- **→ SYSTEM ENGINEER Lv.1 → Lv.2** fired as a literal terse pop-up the instant the slug landed (see System ledger below). Bill paused, told the gunsmith "I've got a delver thing happening," and read the new faculties — including **EXAMINE: SELF**, turned inward for the first time, which showed his core/gates/suppression-weave as a readable plate AND, far down at the part of himself the System lives in, a void that reads **wrong — too clean, edges too straight, made-not-missing**. He reasoned it (boundary not fog; made not missing; no test available yet) and filed it unsolved.
- **THE DART SYSTEM.** Asked "what if different designs for different functions?" Bill drafted a six-part ammunition family in the blueprint space (eyes open, live cost-previews running) and sketched each to paper as he built it. Common pattern across all: ferrous driving core, smooth bearing band, standard tail to feed the magazine. The six: **range dart** (long/slim/hardened — distance, flat flight, narrow wound); **bodkin** (triangular hard point, heavier shaft — the DEFAULT, bites bone/scale/hide); **broadhead** (cutting head — fixed edges, after the gunsmith priced folding-edges as a jam-risk project); **wedge** (stubby, brute, staggers heavy targets); **harpoon** (back-burnered — line-stowage an unsolved problem); **glass-crowned alchemy dart** (screw-off crown, fillable with poison/holy water/acid/oil — Bill's own problem to solve: what survives the firing acceleration). Gunsmith priced it honestly: bodkin/range/wedge makeable now, broadhead a project, harpoon/glass left for after.
- **THE 500-CR ORDER.** Bill named the Platinum delve and asked what 500 cr buys *now*. Gunsmith: money can't make his hands faster, but 500 buys him at the wheel till dawn (and overpays the steel by a mile — he named the excess **retainer**, first-call on his wheel). Result: a DEEP belt by morning — the 40 proven slugs case-hardened into bodkins, ~100 bodkins total, ~15 range darts, a handful of wedges, broadheads after. **−500 cr.** First serious cash across the gunsmith's bench (the handshake stake became a real arrangement).

### The crew tryout — north freight lot, an hour before dark — PASSED HARD

- Met all four: **MYLES** (tank/leader, calm, decides fast, "I bring my crew home"), **JASON** (damage, hot/grinning, instant warmth), **JULIE** (mage, cool/planner, Jason's sister, the careful read, warmed once Bill proved competence), **CHARLES** (healer, dry fatalist, prayed for exactly a range gun).

Insight-picked the **bodkin + called shot** as the most-impressive-and-truest demonstration. Ran **40 darts at speed, silent**, in **~30 seconds** (Julie counted) — through staves, *holing and throwing the boiler-plate*, eyes-up reloads between clips. Dead silence after, which is its own answer.
- Bill slung the gun, picked up his spent clips, folded his hands, and waited — read by Myles as a man who already knows what he did and isn't asking to be impressed (Myles's highest tell).
- **PASSED, all four.** Asked his two questions: pay (no coin ever — a graded chest each on the kill, Platinum-rich, + the new examine-quests on top; full share, his gun earns it) and kit (water/3 days' food, bedroll, **fire-resist salve**, stout boots, knife, essence stones if carried; Charles carries the heal, so carry light and be the gun).
