## ONE-PARAGRAPH ANSWER

The delve progression system is **a curriculum.** Fixed integer payouts, hard lifetime caps per tier, grades calibrated exactly to what a struggling core can survive, loot scaled as incentive, and a world that manifests stronger sites as its people grow stronger — a natural law behaves like none of that. A **training program** behaves like all of it. The dungeons are doing to humanity, at planetary scale, precisely what the guilds half-consciously do to dungeons when they drive cattle through the arch: feeding a thing adversity to make it stronger, on a schedule, toward a purpose the thing being fed does not know. Humanity believes it is looting the dungeons. The structure of the payout system is the strongest evidence in the world that the dungeons — or whatever stands behind them — are cultivating humanity. Who set the curriculum, and what the harvest is for, stays open (see THE OPEN QUESTION). What is locked is this: **the system is designed, the design can be discovered, and Bill Weaver — whose class reads the machinery behind the numbers, where every other delver sees only their own figures move — is the only instrument capable of proving it.**

## THE MECHANISM (DM detail, locked)

- **Cores grow like worked material: stress, then accretion.** Adversity stresses a core; the completion payout is the accretion. Stress without payout (a hard delve abandoned short of the terminus) hardens slightly but grants no attribute growth. Payout without stress is impossible to absorb — it dissipates (the carried-man rule) or, if forced, ruptures the core.
- **Rupture is real and lethal.** A core force-fed raw essence past what its stress state can hold bursts. Death is fast and ugly — internal, not explosive in the gunpowder sense, but delvers who have seen it say "burst" and mean it. Historical instances exist: early-years cultivators who gorged on essence stones in dungeon environments and died for no reason anyone could name. A handful of eastern researchers hold "burst core" case files they cannot explain. **Cell note:** if the USBD cell ever turns its attention to progression research, force-feeding cores is exactly the experiment they would run on bodies nobody misses. Material for later; do not introduce casually.
- **The payout lands at the threshold** — the moment of exit back through the arch after the terminus is cleared. Delvers feel a single surge. The dungeon is, in a real sense, settling an account: adversity rendered, growth paid.
- **An open core is the receiving vessel.** A payout requires an awakened core to land in; a person who somehow completes without one (fought hard, never personally killed anything, core still shut) receives nothing. In practice this edge case is rare — a delver who fights through a dungeon almost always kills something, and the first kill opens the core mid-delve, in time for the threshold.
- **Settle-by-struggle:** an ordinary cultivator's *delve-payout* points distribute along the axis of how they actually struggled. A delver who carried the line gets Might and endurance; a scout who survived on awareness gets Perception; a problem-solver gets Intellect. This is observable in aggregate (fighting men come out of holes built like fighting men) and is one of the patterns a careful researcher could chart. **The one universal exception is the awakening allotment** — the 10 points placed at the core's opening are distributed by the new delver, not settled by struggle, alongside the first choosing; from the first delve payout onward, ordinary delvers are back to settle-by-struggle, and only Bill keeps choosing (see BILL).
- **Delver Level is derived, not paid.** It is simply total points ÷ 10, floored — not a separate currency the dungeon hands out. The arch checks it at tier entry (the gates at Delver Level 5 / 14 / 22 / 36, `game_bible.md` §12), reading it straight off the core's accumulated points; nothing else consults it. The formula is unpublished and uncharted: the world watches the barrier refuse a man without knowing the number behind it. Every window shows its owner's points now, so the arithmetic is in principle available to anyone — but no one has worked out that the gate *is* points ÷ 10, or that points are fixed quanta to begin with (see DISCOVERY).
- **Grade can rise; tier cannot.** Tier is fixed at manifestation. Grade reflects how hard the site currently fights, and delver/animal deaths strengthen it (existing canon). The guilds driving cattle are upgrading grade — better loot, and unknowingly, better payouts. **The economy runs both directions: dungeons pay growth out and take lives in.** Nobody in 1862 has put the two halves side by side. The first person to do so will be looking at a transaction, and transactions imply a counterparty.
- **Caps are per person, per tier, lifetime, across all sites of that tier.** A capped tier still yields loot, kills (and therefore awakening for the unawakened), and essence-environment recovery — only attribute growth stops. The cap does not announce itself; a delver just stops feeling the surge. Most who hit the Foundation cap assume the hole "went dry for them" — a folk reading that is half-right in the worst way.
- **Hollow Reach and the sinks sit outside the system entirely.** No spawn, no terminus, no payout, no tier, no grade. Under the taxonomy it is unclassifiable — which deepens the legal limbo the truth file already establishes. Locked: **standard dungeons pay; the sink takes.** The two verbs are opposites, and a mind that has understood the curriculum will eventually notice that the Mirrorwell looks less like a dungeon than like a hole in the curriculum — a place where the machinery is inverted, breached, or doing something else. Do not resolve what that means casually; it borders the campaign's final architecture per `hollow_reach_truth_v3.md`. (**Update — Special Delves:** the new public *Special Delve* category now gives the world a box to misfile such sites in. "Unclassifiable" stays true at the level of the real system — they sit outside the curriculum — even though the public can now label them. What Special Delves actually are, and their subtypes, is in `system_cosmology_truth_v1.md`.)

## BILL AND THE SYSTEM

- **He alone sees the machinery.** Post-awakening, a completion renders on his Status plate as discrete unallocated points (e.g. `PAYOUT: +3 POINTS — UNALLOCATED`) he can isolate, log, and correlate across delves, with his **Delver Level** ticking up as the running points cross each ten (`DELVER LEVEL: 4`). Every delver now reads their own figures move; what stays invisible to all of them is the *structure* behind the movement — that payouts are fixed quanta, that Delver Level is points ÷ 10, that caps and the adversity gate exist as rules — because each sees only their own surge, never the pattern across sites and people. Bill's plate and Examine let him hold the mechanism still and prove it. *(Rendering rule, reconciled with `system_engineer_truth_v1.md`: the plate quantifies attributes, and unallocated payout points are attribute-numbers — so they render numerically. The essence core's own state stays in words, per that file. A payout is an essence event, but what the plate shows is its attribute result, which is the plate's idiom.)*
- **He allocates every payout deliberately** (his edge, consistent with the player-locked "a little faster, never trivializing" dial): the awakening 10 are placed by everyone now, but from the first delve payout on, ordinary cultivators get settle-by-struggle while Bill assigns each surge's points to attributes from the Status plate. This is power, but small, slow power — the points still come only from real delves, gated by real adversity, against real caps.
- **The choosing renders for him in full.** Where an ordinary delver gets three offers as terse words and a felt sense, Bill's plate renders them with exact figures — costs, wording, category tags — and he can log them. The offers are tailored to what he has been doing, and **a menu that reads the man is itself evidence of design.** A natural law does not draft a fresh curriculum for each student; a reader does. This is a new clue feeding the curriculum inference (DISCOVERY stage 6), and one only Bill is placed to notice, because only he sees the tailoring laid out as numbers across many offers rather than felt one at a time.
- **The adversity gate applies to him fully.** If he is carried, the plate renders the truth without mercy: `PAYOUT: 0 — INSUFFICIENT LOAD.` The System never flatters him.
- **Caps render only when hit:** `TIER CAP: REACHED — FOUNDATION 1/1.` It does not warn in advance (the System never volunteers).
- **Class synergy:** correct deductions about the progression system, verified by test, are exactly the kind of "deep study of genuinely novel phenomena" that feeds System Engineer class levels (`system_engineer_truth_v1.md`). His proof of the quantization, his discovery of a cap, his demonstration of the adversity gate — these are class-level-worthy beats. The curriculum inference (discovery stage 6 below) is worth a major one.
- **Examine on a dungeon mouth or interior**, at sufficient class level and knowledge, may eventually return tier/grade reads (`SITE: TIER 1 — GRADE: COPPER, RISING`). Gate this behind real study of multiple sites; early Examine returns should be accurate near-nothing. When it matures, he becomes the only walking site-assayer in the world — commercially explosive, operationally dangerous knowledge. Smiths talk; assayers would scream.

## DISCOVERY ROADMAP (pace across the campaign; each stage EARNED)

1. **Completions pay.** Common delver knowledge; he learns it the day he starts asking delvers real questions, and feels it at his first threshold.
2. **The payout is fixed by the site, not the man.** His System shows identical figures across different completions of the same site, and different figures across sites. A few careful comparisons prove it. Every delver now reads their own points move, so Bill is not the only one who *could* compare — but private windows, secretive delvers, and no shared records mean no one does. He is the only instrument rigorous enough to prove fixed quanta across many sites and people rather than merely suspect it.
3. **The adversity gate.** A crewmate carried out hurt gets nothing; a man who fought gets the surge; Bill's plate quantifies both. Struggle is the price of absorption. This is the door to the deep law.
4. **Caps.** He hits Foundation 1/1, or correlates veteran testimony ("your soft hole only pays the once") with his own data. From one cap he can hypothesize the ladder; confirming Tier 1's cap of ten takes years of his own delving or other people's careful records.
5. **The world scales.** Manifesting sites trend stronger as regional cultivation rises. Eastern researchers are a half-step from this already; Bill's data could complete it. (Pickering would kill for this story; publishing it would be a strategic act with consequences.)
6. **The inference (the campaign-scale beat):** fixed payouts + hard caps + survivable calibration + incentive loot + responsive scaling + offers tailored to each delver = **design.** Design = designer. Designer = purpose. The day Bill formulates "the dungeons are cultivating us" is the day the campaign's sky changes color. Handle like the System origin tiers — earned, staged, never confirmed cheaply by an NPC. The world contains no authority who can confirm it; he must prove it to himself, and then live with being the only one who knows.

## THE OPEN QUESTION (who set the curriculum — handle like final architecture)

Locked: the system is designed. Open: by what, and for what. Sanctioned readings to plant against each other; do not resolve casually, and never resolve before the Hollow Reach architecture demands it:

- **Preparation.** Something is coming, and humanity is being trained for it. The escalating tiers, the caps that force breadth before depth, the world generating stronger sites on schedule — a curriculum implies a graduation. (Preferred reading if one is ever needed, because it makes every delve both a gift and a warning.)
- **Harvest.** Strong cores, like strong dungeons, are worth more to whatever feeds on them. The guilds fatten dungeons with cattle; the dungeons fatten humanity with loot and growth. The symmetry is the argument. The Mirrorwell — which takes minds whole — may be a glimpse of the collector.
- **Indifferent machinery.** The system is an engine running a program whose purpose ended, or never concerned Earth at all; humanity is incidental load. The sinks are malfunctions. (Coldest reading; keep available to undercut comfortable conclusions.)

These readings should be able to coexist in evidence for years. If the campaign ever forces resolution, prefer Preparation — but check it against whatever the Hollow Reach endgame has become first; the two files must land as one answer.

## THE E–S+ DELVER RATING (future canon — how it actually emerges)

The rating does not exist in 1862 and should not exist for some time. When it comes, it comes from money, not science:

- **Origin:** eastern guild underwriters and the USBD's contract office, jointly, once the Tin–Platinum grading idiom standardizes. The problem it solves is actuarial — pricing contracts, bonds, and insurance on delvers requires a record-based measure of what a delver has survived. Celebrity culture (eastern canon) then seizes it and makes it famous.
- **The mapping** (locked, per the player's specification): a delver's rating reflects the grade composition of their completed delves. **E** mostly Tin · **D** mostly Copper · **C** mostly Iron · **B** mostly Silver · **A** mostly Gold · **S** mostly Platinum · **S+** a record of exclusively Platinum completions.
- **Character:** it is a paperwork rating, not a power reading — it measures what a delver's documented record says they survived. **What it really is, unknowingly:** a record-based estimate of a delver's Delver Level — the rating reconstructs from contracts and completions the number the window shows privately as points ÷ 10. The world approximates the hidden figure without knowing the figure exists, or that an exact one sits on every delver's window. Unregistered delvers have no rating at all; falsified records, bought completions (which the adversity gate makes biologically worthless but bureaucratically real), and the gap between rating and reality are built-in story material. An S-rated eastern celebrity who has never delved west of the Mississippi versus an unrated frontier hand with twenty hard completions is exactly the frontier-versus-legend tension the canon already runs on.
- **Introduction:** bring it onstage when the East reaches into the campaign — a rated delver arriving in the territory, an underwriter's circular on Pickering's desk, a guild notice at the cage. It should feel like news from a faster world.

## STATE OF KNOWLEDGE, 1862 (what NPCs can plausibly say)

- **Frontier delvers:** completions make you stronger; soft holes stop doing it after a few; hard holes give more; fighters come out built for fighting. All folk knowledge, all true, none of it counted.
- **Doss-level guild officers:** know the cattle-driving science, the loot-grade correlation, and the assayers' slang. Could repeat the Foundation-cap finding as trade gossip. Have never seen tiers above 2 and treat "Tier 3" as a theoretical category researchers argue about.
- **Eastern researchers:** are within reach of stages 4–5 of the roadmap and decades from stage 6 without an instrument like Bill. Their papers are where the Foundation cap, the grading taxonomy, and the first world-scaling suspicions live.
- **The cell:** no special insight into progression — their obsession is assimilation. But their files would make them fast students, and a clean specimen who starts demonstrating impossible knowledge of growth mechanics becomes more interesting, not less. Keep the two secrets compartmentalized in play until the fiction forces them together.
- **Nobody, anywhere,** knows the adversity law as a law, the exact payouts, the full cap ladder, or that the system is designed.

## GUARDRAILS & DM PRINCIPLES

- **The math stays slow.** The caps total 51 completions lifetime (Foundation among them pays no points of its own); nobody will ever run that table. Foundation hands Bill 10 points at awakening, plus a growth item and a starter intrinsic for clearing it — meaningful, never explosive. The lifetime ceiling is 510 points — the 10 from awakening plus fifty Platinum completions — which is Delver Level 51, against the 600 it would take to max all six attributes. Years, not sessions, separate anyone from the upper scale; no Tier 3 completion is on record anywhere, and whether higher-tier sites already exist unrecognized is an open question (keep it open until the world's overall power rises).
- **Adversity is not flavor text.** Every paying delve must be genuinely dangerous for the people it pays. If a session hands out points without real risk, the law itself says the points shouldn't have landed.
- **Death stays real.** A Foundation Tin hole can still kill an unawakened engineer with thirty-two cartridges and no melee training. The payout system raises stakes; it never lowers them.
- **The caps are the anti-grind.** Honor them exactly. They force delvers upward into danger — which is the curriculum working as designed, and the design showing.
- **Bill's numeric sight is an instrument, never an oracle.** It measures what he risks himself to measure. No delve, no data.
- **The curriculum inference is a revelation, not a lore drop.** No NPC states it. No document states it. It is assembled, like everything else in this campaign, from evidence by the one mind built to assemble it.
