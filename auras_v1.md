# AURAS — CATALOG (v1)

*Worldbuilding reference for the Frontier Fantasy Solo RPG. Consistent with `game_bible.md` (the canon and tiebreaker, including its voice rules).*

---

## What an aura is

Every awakened core gives off spiritual pressure — a field around the person that other awakened can feel and that reads as raw power (see `game_bible.md` §13 and `cultivation_body_forging_v1.md`). An aura is that same field shaped to *do* something: a sustained effect radiated outward to affect the area around the cultivator rather than a single target.

In the four-kind framework (`ability_framework_v1.md`), **auras are the Passive category's costly, sustained end** — Skills that run on their own once projected, opposite the rare, free always-on boons at the other end of the same category. An aura climbs the 1–5 ability track like any Skill, carries seating requirements readable before use the way orbs do (an affinity or core-strength floor on the stronger ones), and — being a found or granted Skill — crystallizes back into a sellable aura-orb carrying its level if the cultivator gives up the seat (`ability_framework_v1.md` §8).

Three things separate an aura from an Active ability orb:

- **Area, not aimed.** It touches everything in range — friend, enemy, and bystander alike — unless the aura's nature says otherwise.
- **Sustained and costly.** It drains mana continuously while held, and the radius scales with how hard the user pushes it, so a wide aura empties a pool fast.
- **Hard to hide.** An aura is felt by other awakened people nearby, and stronger ones bleed past their owner's control. An aura cannot be carried through a crowd unnoticed the way an orb can be pocketed.

Auras come from the same source as orbs — found inside dungeon monsters, almost never wild ones — and are read intuitively by an awakened handler. Because they radiate, the law treats them more strictly as a class: even a benign aura is registrable, and several are restricted purely because a field that touches everyone nearby is a field imposed on people who did not consent to it.

**Two canon rules:**
- A cultivator can hold **one aura at a time** by default — the Passive category's seat limit, parallel to the single Movement seat and held separately from the Intellect-sized active kit (`ability_framework_v1.md`). Projecting is a whole-core act, not something layered like the active kit.
- An aura affects the **awakened and unawakened differently** in many cases, because the unawakened have no core to push against. Some auras barely touch them; others hit them hardest.

## Common

### Composure
Radiates steadiness. Within the field, fear, panic, and the shakes are dampened — animals settle, green hands hold, a crew under pressure keeps its head. Low to moderate sustained cost, radius scaling with spend. It suppresses the feeling, not the cause; real terror pushes through, and a person leaning on it can be steadied right into a bad decision. Affects the unawakened as strongly as the awakened. Drawn from herd-guardian beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted. Valued by crew leaders, teamsters, and medics. The one aura nobody objects to feeling.

### Warmth
Radiates gentle heat through the field, taking the killing edge off cold air, frozen passages, and exposure. Low sustained cost, radius scaling with spend. It warms the area, no more — it does not protect against a strong cold source, melt anything quickly, or do harm. Affects the awakened and unawakened alike. Drawn from warm-blooded burrowing beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted, cheap. Valued by deep and cold-site crews.

### Clarity
Radiates a faint mental calm. Within the field, distraction and mental fog ease, and focused work comes a little easier. Low sustained cost. It clears the mind, not the senses — it does no thinking for anyone, breaks under real stress, and grants no insight. Touches the awakened more than the unawakened. Drawn from patient, watchful beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted. Valued by crafters, rune-workers, and scholars. Cheap.

## Uncommon

### Dread
Radiates wrongness and fear. Within the field, the unsettled feeling of being prey grows — hostile animals hesitate or break, and people feel the urge to leave. Moderate sustained cost. It pushes on instinct, not the body; a disciplined or enraged mind resists it, and the awakened push back with their own cores, so it bites hardest on the unawakened and on beasts. Cannot be aimed — it touches the user's own people too. Drawn from apex predators.

*Legal:* **Restricted.** Registrable only with a combat permit; projecting it in a settled place is an offense, since it imposes terror on everyone in range. Sold on the side.

### Vigor Field
Radiates a low restorative field. Within the field, the awakened recover stamina a little faster and shed fatigue more easily over time. Moderate to heavy sustained cost drawn from the user. Slow and broad, not a heal — it eases tiredness, not injury, does nothing in the moment of crisis, and gives back less than the user spends to project it. Touches the awakened far more than the unawakened, who have no core to feed. Drawn from symbiotic colony creatures.

*Legal:* Registrable, unrestricted, valued. Prized by long-haul delve crews and field medics. Expensive.

### Burden
Radiates a physical heaviness. Within the field, movement is harder for everyone but the user — limbs drag, steps slow, as though the air itself resists. Moderate to heavy sustained cost. It slows, it does not stop; a strong body pushes through, and it touches the user's allies as much as their enemies. Affects awakened and unawakened alike, bearing harder on the weak. Drawn from massive, gravity-anchored beasts.

*Legal:* **Restricted.** Registrable only with a combat permit. Not usable in a crowd without an offense, since it pins everyone in range. Holds value.

### Ward Field
Radiates a thin protective field. Within the field, incoming force and harm are slightly blunted for everyone inside, the user included. Heavy sustained cost. The blunting is small and even — it spreads thin across all it covers, so a packed field protects each person less, and a strong blow still lands. Affects the awakened more than the unawakened. Drawn from shell-bearing colony creatures.

*Legal:* Registrable as a combat ability, flagged. Not restricted — a defensive field imposed on others is tolerated where an offensive one is not. Expensive.

### Quickening
Radiates a heightening field that the user can extend to chosen allies within the field. Within it, those the user includes react and move a little faster. Heavy sustained cost, rising with the number included. Unlike most auras it can spare the unwilling, but it sharpens speed only — not strength, judgment, or endurance — and held too long it leaves those it touched drained. Affects the awakened only. Drawn from pack-hunting pursuit beasts.

*Legal:* Registrable as a combat ability, flagged. Not restricted. Sought by coordinated delve crews; expensive.

## Rare

### Suppression
Radiates a dampening field that fights other cultivation. Within the field, the abilities, spells, and auras of others cost more mana to use and run weaker, as the field pushes against any core but the user's own. Heavy sustained cost. It taxes the awakened only — the unawakened feel nothing — and it does not stop anyone, only makes their power expensive and dull; a strong enough opponent burns through it. Cannot exempt allies. Drawn from anti-essence aberrations.

*Legal:* **Restricted.** Registrable only under high-tier permit; feared and tightly watched, since a field that weakens everyone's magic at once has obvious uses against lawful casters. Rarely sold openly; very expensive.

### Command Field
Radiates the user's intent to those who follow them. Within the field, allies the user has bonded move in better coordination, share a rough sense of the user's direction, and hold under pressure they otherwise would not. Heavy sustained cost, rising with the number bonded. It steadies and coordinates the willing — it compels no one, reads no thoughts, and does nothing to an enemy or a stranger. Affects the awakened and unawakened among the bonded alike. Drawn from hive and pack-leader creatures.

*Legal:* Registrable, flagged. Not restricted, but a known marker of crew leaders and officers, and read that way. Expensive and sought.

### Decay Field
Radiates a draining rot. Within the field, the living tire and weaken, wounds knit slower, and the dead and undead within it grow stronger. Heavy sustained cost. It works slowly and touches everything living in range, the user's allies and the user's own recovery included; only its hold over the lifeless spares the user the worst. Bears hardest on the unawakened. Drawn from undead-category creatures.

*Legal:* **Restricted.** Registrable only under high-tier permit with a hazard endorsement, and viewed with open suspicion by the Church given its origin and its favor toward the undead. Sold privately, dear, and rarely.

### Sanctuary
Radiates a stilling peace that holds violence at bay. Within the field, the will to fight ebbs — aggression cools, beasts lose their fury, and a confrontation loses its heat. Heavy sustained cost. It calms the impulse, not the cause; a set purpose or a cold intent pushes through, it protects no one from a blow already committed, and it binds the user's allies as much as their foes. Affects awakened and unawakened alike. Drawn from rare placid apex creatures.

*Legal:* Registrable, flagged, and of keen interest to the Church, which prizes and watches it. Not restricted, but uncommon enough that an owner is known by name to the licensing office. Among the most valuable auras.
